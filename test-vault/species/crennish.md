# Crennish

Crennish are small humanoids — averaging four feet tall, slight-framed, with oversized ears and quick, darting eyes — who possess true eidetic memory. Everything they see, hear, or read is retained permanently and can be recalled with perfect fidelity. Roughly 1,200 live in Duskfrost, a number that has tripled in twenty years due to their rapid reproduction rate of three to four children annually. They are the city's fastest-growing population, a fact that generates both economic opportunity and quiet demographic anxiety.

The [[Archive Building]] employs more Crennish than any other institution in Duskfrost — currently nineteen cataloguers, plus an unknown number of freelance transcribers. A Crennish cataloguer can read a document once and reproduce it verbatim years later, making them living backups of the city's records. The Archive pays well for this, but there is an uncomfortable power dynamic at work: a Crennish employee who leaves takes copies of everything they ever filed with them, a fact the [[Bellsummit Council]] has raised in closed session more than once.

Crennish communities cluster in [[Highwallow]] and the cheaper edges of [[Amberburgh]], where their large families can find affordable housing. A Crennish household of twelve is not unusual. Their neighborhoods are loud, dense, and intensely social — children underfoot everywhere, elders sitting in doorways reciting contract terms from memory to settle disputes without paperwork.

Their value to the [[Orvathi]] is well-established: Crennish translators attend complex multi-party negotiations, providing perfect recall of who said what and when. The [[Amberburgh Merchants League]] has begun hiring Crennish witnesses for high-value trade deals, their testimony considered more reliable than written contracts. This has created a small professional class of Crennish notaries who charge significant fees for their presence.

The cultural tension around Crennish is subtle but real. A species that remembers everything — every promise, every insult, every lie — navigates social relationships differently than one that can forget. Crennish hold grudges with crystalline precision, and they forgive with equal deliberation: not because the memory fades, but because they choose to set it aside. Their short lifespan of roughly fifty years means they cycle through generations faster than most species, which makes their institutional memory paradoxically both perfect and fragile. The elder who remembers the pre-Calamity treaty terms dies, and the memory dies with them, even though they could have recited it word-for-word the day before.
