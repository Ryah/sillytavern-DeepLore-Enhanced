# Scribe Fragment — Lost Translation

*The following is transcribed from Fragment 7-C of the Pallasys Translation collection, recovered from the Archive's water-damaged lower storage in Year 319. Translation by [[Ayre Waesphyra]].*

---

*"The towers spoke to one another in a language we did not teach them. We built the language, yes — the resonance protocols, the carrier frequencies, the handshake sequences. But the conversation that emerged between the towers was not in that language. It was in one we could not read.*

*We know what it sounded like: a harmony, rising in pitch over the years of operation, with intervals that one of our mathematicians claimed were prime-number spaced. We know when it changed: approximately six months before the Cascade. The harmony became dissonant. The mathematicians said the intervals were no longer prime.*

*We did not know what this meant. We know now, or we think we do. The towers were not malfunctioning. They were warning us."*

---

*Translator's note: This fragment appears to be a first-person account written by a pre-Calamity resonance engineer. Its authenticity cannot be verified. Its implications, if authentic, are significant and have been reported to the Grand Oracle. — A.W.*
