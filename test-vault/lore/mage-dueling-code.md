# Mage Dueling Code

The Mage Dueling Code governs formal arcane disputes between licensed practitioners. A sanctioned duel requires: a licensed arbiter, a registered venue with containment wards, mutual agreement on power tier limits, and a signed waiver from both parties. Unsanctioned dueling within city limits carries a two-year suspension. The Code has been amended seventeen times; the current version bans seven specific sigil combinations that previous versions permitted.

The Code originated in Year 167, when the [[Duskfrost Academy]]'s third headmaster grew tired of repairing buildings damaged by practitioners settling professional disagreements in the street. The original version was four pages. The current version is sixty-three pages, reflecting two centuries of practitioners discovering creative ways to hurt each other that the previous version's authors did not anticipate. Each amendment corresponds to a specific incident. The ban on resonance-harmonic sigils in dueling contexts (Amendment 9, Year 241) followed a duel that shattered every glass object within three city blocks. The ban on biological transmutation sigils (Amendment 14, Year 298) followed a duel that left one participant permanently unable to digest solid food. Amendment 17, the most recent, bans a specific sigil combination that the text describes only as "the Wynlar Sequence," after [[Gorduin Wynlar]] demonstrated it in a sanctioned duel and both the arbiter and the venue's containment wards failed simultaneously. Gorduin won the duel. He also paid for the venue's reconstruction and has not dueled since.

Power tiers are the Code's most practical innovation. Each duel specifies a maximum tier — Tier 1 permits only basic kinetic and thermal sigils, Tier 2 adds sensory and binding effects, Tier 3 permits the full licensed repertoire minus the seven banned combinations. Tier 4 duels are theoretically possible under the Code but require Triumvirate authorization, as the expected damage radius exceeds standard containment ward capacity. No Tier 4 duel has been authorized in sixty years. The last one, between two senior Academy researchers disputing attribution of a theoretical breakthrough, destroyed the venue, damaged four neighboring buildings, and resulted in a draw that satisfied neither party. The theoretical breakthrough in question was later shown to be based on a transcription error.

Unsanctioned dueling — the street fights the Code was designed to prevent — continues despite the two-year suspension penalty. The [[Wardenship]] investigates an average of eight unsanctioned duels per year, mostly in [[Highwallow]] where the [[Resonance Scar Properties|Resonance Scar]]'s interference makes forensic reconstruction of sigil work unreliable. The penalty for unsanctioned dueling resulting in death is permanent license revocation and criminal prosecution — a deterrent that works well among practitioners who value their licenses and poorly among those who never had one. The [[Highwallow Underground Economy|unlicensed practitioners]] of Highwallow settle their disputes by their own rules, which are simpler and more final.
