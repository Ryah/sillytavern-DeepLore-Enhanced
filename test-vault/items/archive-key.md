# Archive Master Key

A single physical key that opens every lock in the Archive's restricted collection. There are, officially, two copies. [[Gorduin Wynlar]] holds one. The other is in the Head Archivist Kettleback's personal safe. A third copy is rumored to exist. The Archive's security protocol assumes this rumor is true.

The key itself is unremarkable in appearance — a heavy iron skeleton key with no visible arcane markings, no distinguishing features, and no obvious reason it should open locks ranging from mechanical pin-tumbler to ward-sealed containment doors. The Academy's metalwork department examined Gorduin's copy in Year 301 and concluded that the key contains no arcane components detectable by standard instruments. It should not work. It works anyway. The prevailing theory is that the locks recognize the key rather than the key fitting the locks — that the Archive's security infrastructure was designed around this specific object, which may predate the Archive itself. Gorduin considers this theory "plausible and deeply inconvenient," as it implies the Archive's restricted collection was planned before the collection existed.

The rumored third copy has been the subject of periodic security reviews since Year 278, when an unauthorized entry into the restricted stacks was detected by ward alarm but left no physical evidence of how the intruder bypassed the locks. Nothing was taken. Nothing was damaged. A single reading chair had been moved approximately two meters from its usual position, and a text on [[True Names — Theory and Danger|true name theory]] had been removed from its shelf and left open to a specific page. The page discussed the theoretical derivation of true names for arcane constructs rather than living entities. The security review concluded that the intruder had either picked every lock in sequence — requiring skills beyond any known practitioner's capability — or possessed a third key. The [[Wardenship]] investigated for six months and produced no leads. Gorduin responded by adding a secondary ward layer to the vault that triggers if a key other than the two registered copies is used. The secondary ward has not triggered. This is not reassuring, because it either means the third key does not exist or that whoever holds it has not returned yet.

A [[Speaking Stone]] pair is kept in the same vault, linked to an unknown counterpart outside the Archive. Gorduin has attempted to trace the counterpart's location through the stone's resonance signature, with limited success — the signal suggests the paired stone is somewhere in the [[Hollow Roads]] tunnel network, moving intermittently.
