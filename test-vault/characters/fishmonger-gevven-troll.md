# Gevven Troll

Fishmonger in [[Bayside Shushail]]'s morning market, present every day before dawn and departed before midday. He is a neighborhood fixture — knows every face, every regular customer, every subtle shift in what the district is doing and who is doing it. He does not sell this information, but he does mention things in conversation, which is a distinction that makes him comfortable and keeps him safe. He has been doing this for thirty years and intends to do it for thirty more.

Gevven's stall occupies the same spot in the morning market it has occupied since his father ran it. He arrives at four, sets up by four-thirty, and by five he is holding court — selling fish, trading gossip, and reading the district's mood from who shows up and who doesn't. A regular customer who misses three days is either sick, in trouble, or dead. Gevven notices, and he asks around, which is how he has become Bayside Shushail's unofficial welfare system.

What people take for an old man's nosiness is something more deliberate. Gevven was a dock runner in his youth — same job [[Mivi]] does now, different era — and he learned to read districts the way sailors read weather. He retired from running when his knees gave out and took over the stall, but the skill set is the same: watch, remember, connect. He knows the Bonehands' operational rhythm from the buying patterns of their people, and he knows the Solarguard's patrol schedule from when their off-duty soldiers come for breakfast.

He shares pre-dawn coffee with [[Dockmaster Bram Solvik]] most mornings, and their conversations are the closest thing Bayside Shushail has to a morning intelligence briefing. Neither man would describe it that way. They would say they are old friends who like fish and strong coffee, and both statements are true, and neither is complete.
