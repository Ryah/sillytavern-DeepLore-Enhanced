# Chorstap Row

A market street in western [[Amberburgh]] specializing in imported and unusual goods — spices from the [[Kethvali]] caravans, [[Zuvine]] handicrafts, Academy-certified arcane components at retail prices. Busy during morning and midday, closes by late afternoon.

The street is narrow enough that opposing shop awnings nearly touch overhead, creating a fabric tunnel that traps the smell of everything sold beneath it: cumin and cardamom from the Kethvali spice merchants, the brine-and-shell scent of Zuvine handicrafts, the sharp ozone tang drifting from the arcane component dealers, and the pervasive sweetness of the candied fruit vendor who has occupied the same corner for twenty years. Goods are displayed on folding tables and hanging racks that extend into the street, forcing foot traffic into a single-file stream during peak hours. Prices are posted in chalk on slate boards, negotiation is expected for anything over five marks, and shoplifting is handled by the merchants themselves with a collective efficiency that makes [[Solarguard]] intervention unnecessary.

The arcane component shops draw the most unusual clientele — [[Duskfrost Academy]] students purchasing reagents for coursework, [[Oracles of Might]] guild members who prefer retail anonymity to guild requisition paperwork, and the occasional independent practitioner whose needs the shopkeepers have learned not to question. The Academy certification stamps on these goods are genuine; the [[Wardenship]] inspects Chorstap Row's arcane dealers quarterly and has shut down three unlicensed vendors in the past decade. The legitimate dealers consider this enforcement a competitive advantage and report suspected unlicensed activity with civic-minded enthusiasm.

By late afternoon, the street empties with startling speed. Awnings retract, tables fold, goods vanish behind locked shutters. By dusk, Chorstap Row is a quiet corridor of closed shop fronts that gives no indication of the morning's commerce. The merchants who work here live elsewhere — the street has no residential space — and the transition from market to silence happens so completely that newcomers to Amberburgh sometimes walk the evening street and wonder what it is for.
