# Archive Volume Disappearance

Eight years ago, three volumes vanished from the [[Duskfrost Academy]]'s restricted collection without any record of checkout, triggering what became the longest and most politically embarrassing investigation in the Archive's history. The restricted section employs triple-ward access logging — a system installed after the [[Academy Fire of Year 211]] — which means either the wards were circumvented by someone with significant arcane capability, or they were deliberately disabled by someone with authorized access. Neither possibility is comforting.

The subsequent two-year investigation implicated seventeen people and charged none. The [[Wardenship]] examined the ward logs and found a six-hour gap in the record that corresponded to no known maintenance window. The [[Solarguard]] interviewed every registered practitioner with Archive access and produced a 200-page report that concluded, with visible frustration, that the evidence was "consistent with multiple contradictory scenarios." The investigative committee was quietly disbanded when two of its members received credible threats from unidentified sources.

The three missing volumes are: a necromantic theory text attributed to the pre-Calamity scholar Vetha Morvane (one of only four surviving copies of any Morvane work), a collection of pre-Calamity diplomatic correspondence between city-states that referenced resonance grid weaponization (relevant to the [[War of Kroyhr]]), and an untitled volume bound in black leather with a silver eye embossed on its cover. The third volume's contents were never catalogued in detail — it was acquired during a bulk purchase from a private collector and placed in restricted storage pending examination that never occurred. The fact that someone stole a volume whose contents the Academy itself did not fully know suggests the thief had better information than the institution.

[[Gorduin Wynlar]] was consulted during the investigation and remains unsatisfied with its conclusions. He has publicly stated that the disappearance is connected to the [[Velkhast Succession Crisis]], though he has not explained how. The Academy has offered a standing reward of five thousand gold crowns for information leading to recovery. No credible leads have emerged. The silver-eye volume, in particular, has become something of an obsession for certain collectors in [[Bayside Shushail]]'s black market, where rumors of its contents range from the plausible to the absurd.

Gorduin's private suspicion centers on [[Archmage Tessavel Orindal]], who had unrestricted access to the restricted section during the six-hour gap in the ward logs and whose refusal to endorse [[Corwin Velkhast]] in the succession dispute Gorduin considers circumstantially related. His theory — unproven and politically explosive — is that the diplomatic correspondence volume contains information about pre-Calamity resonance grid weaponization that the Academy's current leadership would prefer to keep buried, and that the other two volumes were taken alongside it to obscure which text was the actual target. He has shared this theory with [[Warden-Captain Orin Castellan]], who found it plausible enough to keep the case file open but insufficient to act on.
