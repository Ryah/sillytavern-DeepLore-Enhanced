# Nightmarket Customs

The [[Amberburgh]] night market operates under a parallel set of customs distinct from daytime trade law. The key conventions: no questions about provenance, no [[Duskfrost Triumvirate]] insignia displayed, and the first offer is never the real offer. [[Wardenship]] patrols exist but maintain deliberate blindness to most transactions. The night market has survived four official attempts to close it; the fifth attempt is currently stalled in committee.

The market opens at full dark and closes at the third bell before dawn, occupying the same streets and stalls that host [[Amberburgh Trade Customs|legitimate daytime commerce]]. The transition is ritualized: daytime merchants lock their stall fronts, night merchants unfold their own tables in the gaps between, and for approximately twenty minutes the two populations overlap in a practiced choreography of mutual non-acknowledgment. Many daytime merchants are also night merchants, operating under different names with different stock. Everyone knows this. No one says it.

Goods available at the night market fall into three categories. The first is ordinary merchandise sold without the Commerce Board's transaction taxes — food, cloth, tools, minor luxuries. This accounts for roughly half of all night market commerce and is the reason the Triumvirate tolerates the market's existence: taxing it would reduce volume more than it would increase revenue. The second category is regulated goods sold without required licenses — Academy-controlled arcane materials, restricted alchemical reagents, [[Forbidden Sigil Catalog|sigil templates]] that are not technically forbidden but require permits no one wants to apply for. The [[Bonehands]] control most of this trade. The third category is goods that are flatly illegal: stolen artifacts, unlicensed [[Blood Binding Contracts|blood-binding]] services, and occasionally information — names, locations, access codes — sold by people who make no pretense of legitimate occupation.

The night market's internal enforcement is handled by the Stall Council — an informal body of senior night merchants who adjudicate disputes, assign territory, and maintain the market's one absolute rule: no violence on market grounds. Violations result in permanent exclusion, enforced by the collective refusal of all night merchants to do business with the offender. This is more effective than it sounds, because the kind of person who needs the night market cannot afford to be shut out of it. The Wardenship's deliberate blindness extends to the Stall Council's enforcement activities, a courtesy that both sides find preferable to the alternative.
