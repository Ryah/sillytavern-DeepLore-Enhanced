# Human

Humans are Duskfrost's majority species, comprising approximately 55% of the city's population. They dominate the Triumvirate houses, fill most Solarguard ranks, and occupy the broadest range of social positions from [[Bellsummit]] nobility to [[Highwallow]] destitution. Their defining trait, arcane-politically speaking, is variance — human bloodline affinities range from powerful to nonexistent, with most falling toward the latter.

The statistical reality is that most humans have no meaningful arcane affinity. Perhaps one in fifteen shows sufficient potential for Academy training. Of those, perhaps one in ten develops significant ability. This means that the vast majority of Duskfrost's human population experiences magic as something that happens to them or around them, wielded by specialists who are often not human, in institutions that charge for the privilege.

This creates a political dynamic: humans govern the city through numbers, wealth, and institutional control, while non-human species — particularly elves and dwarves — hold disproportionate arcane influence. The Triumvirate is entirely human. The Academy is led by an elf. Neither fact is coincidental, and the tension between political power and arcane power is one of Duskfrost's enduring structural conflicts.

Human culture in Duskfrost varies dramatically by district. [[Bellsummit]] humans are formal, politically sophisticated, and conscious of lineage in ways that echo elven culture without the longevity to sustain it. [[Amberburgh]] humans are commercial, practical, and defined by guild and trade affiliation. [[Highwallow]] humans are resilient, community-oriented, and profoundly suspicious of institutions that have historically failed them. [[Bayside Shushail]] humans are working-class, harbor-oriented, and operate in the grey zone between legitimate and Bonehands-adjacent economies.

Humans' short lifespans relative to elves and dwarves create urgency — a human politician has decades, not centuries, to build a legacy. This makes human politics faster, more aggressive, and less patient than the institutional maneuvering of longer-lived species. The [[Velkhast Succession Crisis]] is a characteristically human problem: a house built on one lifetime's relationships, destroyed by that lifetime ending.

Half-elves and other mixed-heritage humans exist in meaningful numbers. They are technically human under Triumvirate census law, technically elven under elven lineage custom, and practically defined by which community they grew up in rather than which blood they carry.
