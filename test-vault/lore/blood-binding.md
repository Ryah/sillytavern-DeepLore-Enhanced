# Blood Binding Contracts

Blood-binding is a legal practice recognized by the Triumvirate in which contracts are enforced through arcane means rather than legal process. A practitioner seals the agreement with a sigil drawn in the blood of both parties; violation causes physical consequences ranging from discomfort to death depending on the contract's terms. Technically regulated. Practically widespread.

The process requires a licensed binder — an Academy-certified practitioner trained in the specific sigil geometries that convert contractual language into arcane enforcement. The binder draws the contract sigil using blood from both parties mixed with a stabilizing reagent (proprietary to each binder, and a source of endless professional rivalry). The sigil encodes the terms in a simplified logical structure: condition, violation threshold, and consequence. The consequence is the critical variable. Minor contracts specify discomfort — headaches, nausea, a persistent feeling of wrongness. Commercial contracts typically specify escalating pain. The most serious contracts, reserved for state agreements and inter-house treaties, specify death. The [[Duskfrost Triumvirate]] requires all death-consequence bindings to be registered and counter-signed by a Triumvirate magistrate. Approximately sixty percent of death-consequence bindings are actually registered.

The legal status of blood-binding exists in a carefully maintained gray zone. The Triumvirate recognizes binding contracts as enforceable, which means a party who suffers consequences from violating a binding cannot bring suit against the other party for the suffering. However, the Triumvirate does not require blood-binding for any transaction, and the [[Duskfrost Academy]] officially discourages it as "an unnecessary arcane risk in situations where conventional legal remedies exist." This is the Academy's polite way of saying that binding sigils occasionally malfunction, interpreting metaphorical contract language literally or triggering consequences for technical violations that neither party intended to enforce. A famous case in Year 289 saw a merchant suffer three days of agonizing pain because he delivered a shipment of "finest quality" wool that was, by an arcane standard no human would apply, merely "excellent quality."

The [[Severance Dagger]], if it still exists, is one of the few known means of breaking a blood-binding without fulfilling its terms. The [[Wardenship]] considers this capability sufficiently dangerous that the dagger's disappearance from their custody is treated as an ongoing security incident rather than simple theft. Unlicensed binding — practiced extensively in [[Highwallow]] and the [[Amberburgh]] night market — uses cruder sigil work that is less precise but also less prone to literal interpretation. The unlicensed binders consider this a feature.
