# The Drowned Anchor

A dockside tavern in northern [[Bayside Shushail]] that operates on the principle that sailors require inexpensive drinks and no questions. The Drowned Anchor delivers on both. Rumors, shipping schedules, and crew availability are freely traded here. The [[Bonehands]] leave it alone because it is useful; the [[Solarguard]] leaves it alone because it is easier.

The building leans slightly toward the water, a structural inclination that has been worsening for twenty years and that the owner considers a selling point rather than a hazard. Inside, the ceiling is low, the tables are bolted to the floor, and the air is a permanent fog of pipe smoke, salt damp, and the sweet-sour smell of the house ale — a brew of indeterminate recipe that costs two coppers and tastes like it. The walls are covered with nailed-up ship ephemera: charts, pennants, a harpoon head, and the mounted anchor that gives the tavern its name, which hangs above the bar at an angle that suggests its mounting hardware is as structurally reliable as the building itself. The lanterns are dim enough to flatter everyone and bright enough to count change.

The clientele is dockworkers, sailors between berths, fishermen too tired to walk home, and the harbor district's permanent population of people who prefer company to solitude but conversation to commitment. Information moves through the Drowned Anchor the way water moves through a net — impossible to contain and pointless to try. A sailor buying drinks will mention a shipping route. A dockworker will mention which berths are being cleared for unexpected arrivals. A fisherman will mention what the [[Zuvine Harbor Settlement|Zuvine]] are doing differently this week. None of this is espionage; it is simply what people talk about when they drink in a room where everyone works the harbor.

The tavern's neutrality is less formal than [[The Hidden Dragonfruit Tavern|the Dragonfruit]]'s but equally real. The Bonehands do not recruit here, collect here, or conduct business here, because a tavern that serves as a pressure-free information commons is more valuable intact than exploited. The Solarguard harbor patrol drinks here off-shift, out of uniform, and finds it the most efficient intelligence-gathering method available — better than informants, cheaper than surveillance, and considerably more pleasant.
