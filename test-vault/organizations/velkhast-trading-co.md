# Velkhast Trading Company

The commercial arm of [[Duskfrost Triumvirate|House Velkhast]], controlling three of the five major harbor berths and the primary trade routes to the northern coast. Currently paralyzed by the Velkhast succession dispute, which has turned a profitable shipping operation into an exercise in institutional anxiety. Ships still dock. Cargo still moves. But every significant decision — new contracts, route expansions, capital investments — sits unsigned on a desk that nobody is authorized to clear.

<div class="meta-block">
[Type: Noble House Commerce | HQ: Velkhast Wharf Office, [[Bayside Shushail]] | Acting Director: Steward Callum Dorr (Human) | Employees: ~600 (dockworkers, clerks, sailors, factors) | Founded: Year 89 | Status: Operational but stalled]
</div>

The Company's operations span three harbor berths, a fleet of fourteen trading vessels, and a network of factors in coastal cities from Thornmouth to the Pale Reach. Under normal circumstances, it is the single largest employer in [[Bayside Shushail]] and the engine of Duskfrost's import economy — northern timber, preserved goods, manufactured tools, and the luxury imports that keep [[Bellsummit]]'s households comfortable. Under the current circumstances, it is a machine running on institutional momentum while its two potential owners fight over the steering mechanism. Steward Callum Dorr, a career administrator elevated to acting director by the simple fact that both succession claimants trust him to not favor the other, manages day-to-day operations with the careful neutrality of a man who knows his position exists only as long as neither claimant wins.

The succession paralysis has created ripple effects across the city. The [[Amberburgh Merchants League]] has noticed that berth assignments — normally allocated by the Company on annual contracts — are being handled month-to-month because Dorr lacks authority to sign long-term agreements. The [[Bonehands]] have noticed that the Company's security presence at the harbor berths has thinned as experienced guards leave for more stable employment, creating opportunities for pilferage that would have been unthinkable two years ago. The [[Salvagers Compact]] has noticed that the Company's expansion plans — new berth construction that would bury active salvage sites — have stalled, which is the one positive consequence of the dispute for anyone who isn't a Velkhast.

The Company's internal tension goes beyond the succession. The six hundred employees who actually run the operation — the dockworkers loading cargo at dawn, the clerks reconciling manifests, the sailors spending months at sea — have no stake in which Velkhast cousin inherits and increasing frustration with an institutional paralysis that threatens their livelihoods. Dorr has quietly authorized a modest pay increase to prevent a labor action, funding it from the Company's reserves in a decision that technically exceeds his authority and that both succession claimants will object to if they ever stop fighting each other long enough to notice. The harbor does not wait for noble houses to sort out their inheritance. The tides come in. The cargo needs moving. Someone has to do the work regardless of whose name is on the charter.
