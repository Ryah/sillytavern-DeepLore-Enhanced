# Elemental Band

A simple metal bracelet of pre-Calamity manufacture that grants the wearer resistance to minor elemental effects — light rain passes around them, small fires do not ignite their clothing, and minor cold does not penetrate. Provides no protection against significant elemental forces. Currently in the [[Amberburgh Merchants League]] auction house pending sale.

The band is approximately two centimeters wide, made of the same unidentifiable pale metal found in several pre-Calamity artifacts including the [[Angel Crown]]. It has no visible sigil work, no inscriptions, and no moving parts. It fits any wrist — expanding or contracting smoothly to accommodate the wearer without any apparent mechanism. The [[Duskfrost Academy]]'s materials department has tested the metal's composition six times over the past century, each time the band has changed hands and become temporarily available for study. The results are consistent: the metal contains no known elements in any known combination. It is simply a substance that does not exist in modern metallurgy.

The protection it offers is genuinely minor — useful for a merchant who walks to work in the rain or a traveler who sleeps near a campfire, useless for a soldier facing a fireball or a sailor caught in a storm. The Academy's testing confirmed that the band's protective field extends approximately one centimeter from the wearer's skin, creating a thin buffer that redirects or absorbs minor elemental energy. The field collapses instantly under significant force. One researcher described it as "the arcane equivalent of a light coat — you will not get wet in a drizzle, but do not expect it to save you in a flood."

The band has changed hands fourteen times in recorded history, each time through sale rather than theft or inheritance. Its current auction listing in Amberburgh has drawn interest from three parties: a Bellsummit noble who wants it as a conversation piece, a [[Thyren]] trade delegation whose interest suggests they recognize the metal, and the Academy, which wants to study it for a seventh time in case the previous six analyses missed something. The expected sale price is between two hundred and three hundred crowns — significant for a bracelet, modest for a pre-Calamity artifact. The Thyren delegation's bidding strategy suggests they will pay considerably more.
