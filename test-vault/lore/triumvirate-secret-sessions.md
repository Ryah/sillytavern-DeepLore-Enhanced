# Triumvirate Secret Sessions

Beyond the public council record, the three [[Duskfrost Triumvirate]] houses maintain a parallel meeting practice: closed sessions with no official transcript, held in a rotating venue not disclosed to staff. The practice is technically legal under the charter but politically contentious. An estimated 40% of actual governance decisions are made in closed session; the public record ratifies the results.

The rotating venue system was established after a Year 245 incident in which a [[Bolludine Shard Politics|Bolludine]] courier was found to have been eavesdropping on closed sessions held at the Triumvirate's official chambers. The courier was prosecuted; the Bolludine family denied involvement with transparent insincerity; the Triumvirate relocated their private meetings to a circuit of undisclosed locations that changes quarterly. The selection process is itself secret: each house proposes a venue, the three proposals are sealed, and one is chosen by lot. The other two houses learn the location only on the day of the meeting, delivered by a bonded messenger who destroys the notice after delivery. This level of operational security would be impressive for a military command. For a municipal government deciding property tax rates, it is widely considered excessive. The Triumvirate's position is that any decision worth making in private is worth protecting.

The content of closed sessions is known through leaks, inference, and the occasional indiscretion of junior staff who overhear fragments. Major decisions known to have originated in closed session include: the classification of the [[Rappiop Grove]] discovery, the Wardenship's budget for [[Highwallow Underground Economy|Highwallow enforcement]] operations, the terms of the most recent [[Guild Hierarchy Laws|Charter guild]] application denial, and — most significantly — the three houses' private agreement on how to divide research access to the resonance grid fragment, an agreement that contradicts the public joint-custody arrangement and that all three houses deny exists. The [[Oracles of Might]] are believed to have intelligence on the closed sessions' content, obtained through methods that [[Gorduin Wynlar]] describes as "responsible awareness of civic developments."

The [[Binding Compact]] does not explicitly permit or prohibit closed sessions — it addresses only the public council's structure and voting procedures. Three legal challenges have been brought arguing that governance decisions made outside the public council lack legal force. All three were dismissed by judges appointed through the public council process, which is to say, judges who owe their positions to decisions made in closed session. The circular logic is not lost on the petitioners, who have begun exploring whether the Compact's amendment procedures could be used to force transparency. This would require a two-thirds vote of the public council, which would require the Triumvirate's cooperation, which would require the Triumvirate to vote in public to eliminate their ability to meet in private. The probability of this occurring is zero, and everyone involved knows it.
