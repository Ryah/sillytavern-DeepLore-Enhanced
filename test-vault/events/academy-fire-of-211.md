# Academy Fire of Year 211

A catastrophic fire destroyed the [[Duskfrost Academy]]'s original east wing in Year 211, consuming an estimated four thousand volumes and killing three junior archivists who were working the night shift. The blaze started in the restricted collection annex shortly after midnight and spread through the timber-framed upper floors before the [[Wardenship]] responders could establish containment wards. By dawn, the east wing was a stone shell filled with ash.

The cause remains officially undetermined, which in Duskfrost means politically inconvenient. The Academy's internal investigation identified three possible ignition points, all in sections housing pre-Calamity theoretical texts — specifically, manuscripts related to resonance grid architecture that House Morveth had contributed during the [[Breaking of the Morveth Arcane Monopoly]]. The investigation's final report used the phrase "spontaneous arcane ignition" without apparent irony. No one was charged. Several scholars, including [[Gorduin Wynlar]]'s predecessors in the archival faculty, believe the fire was deliberately set to destroy specific texts before the broader academic community could study them. The question of who benefits from that destruction has never been satisfactorily answered.

The immediate aftermath reshaped the Academy's approach to knowledge preservation. A fireproof vault was constructed beneath the rebuilt wing, access protocols for the restricted collection were tripled in complexity, and the Academy began its ongoing program of creating duplicate copies of irreplaceable texts — a program that is, three centuries later, approximately 40% complete. The three dead archivists are commemorated with a small plaque in the rebuilt east wing that most students walk past without reading.

The longer-term consequence is more troubling. Several historians now believe the destroyed texts contained information about the resonance grid's operational principles that would have advanced understanding of the pre-Calamity network by decades. The fire may have been set specifically to prevent discovery of what later became the [[Resonance Grid Discovery]] — meaning someone in Year 211 already knew what a junior researcher would stumble upon two centuries later beneath [[Rappiop Grove]]. If true, this implies a continuity of knowledge and intent that the Academy has not publicly acknowledged.
