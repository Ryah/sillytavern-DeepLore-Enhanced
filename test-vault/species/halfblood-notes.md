# Halfbloods and Mixed Heritage

Mixed-heritage individuals — called halfbloods in common speech and persons of mixed lineage in official documents — are a significant and growing demographic in Duskfrost. No reliable census exists, but the [[Highwallow Mutual Aid Hall]] estimates between 2,000 and 3,000 residents claim mixed heritage, roughly one in eight of the city's population. The number is almost certainly higher, since many mixed-heritage individuals pass as one parent species or the other and see no reason to correct the assumption.

Trait inheritance follows no clean rules. A [[Human]]-[[Delivese]] child might inherit cold resistance, or height, or neither, or a compromised version of both that leaves them uncomfortable in all temperatures. [[Zuvine]]-surface pairings sometimes produce children who can hold their breath for twenty minutes but cannot actually breathe water — a dangerous partial inheritance that the Zuvine community considers a strong argument against intermarriage. [[Velhari]] mixed children occasionally manifest fragmentary illusion ability: a flicker of color at the fingertips, an involuntary shimmer when startled. Some combinations produce unexpected arcane sensitivity; others seem to cancel it entirely.

Social acceptance is, as with most things in Duskfrost, a function of district and economics. [[Bellsummit]] is formally inclusive — the [[Bellsummit Council]] passed anti-discrimination ordinances two decades ago and enforces them with genuine if imperfect effort. [[Highwallow]] is practically inclusive because it cannot afford to be otherwise; when everyone is struggling, excluding a neighbor over bloodline is a luxury. [[Amberburgh]] is indifferent in the way that markets are indifferent — money spends the same regardless of who earned it.

[[Scorchhelm]] is the problem. The district's prejudice toward mixed-heritage individuals is open, persistent, and sporadically violent. The [[Solarguard]] has been formally reprimanded three times for failing to investigate assaults on halfblood residents, and the [[Bonehands]] actively exploit the vulnerability — mixed-heritage workers in Scorchhelm accept worse terms because they have fewer alternatives. The Triumvirate acknowledges the issue in annual reports and does nothing meaningful about it, a pattern that has persisted long enough to constitute policy.

Among mixed-heritage communities themselves, there is no unified identity or political movement. A Human-[[Elf]] halfblood in Bellsummit and a [[Vorruk]]-[[Orvathi]] halfblood in Highwallow share a census category and little else. What they do share is a particular navigational intelligence — the ability to read which parts of themselves to foreground in which company, a skill that most learn young and practice without thinking about it. Some find this exhausting. Others find it an advantage. Most simply find it ordinary.
