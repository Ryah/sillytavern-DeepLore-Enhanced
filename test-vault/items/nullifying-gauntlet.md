# Nullifying Gauntlet

A single gauntlet (no matching pair exists) that suppresses arcane effects within a radius of approximately one meter of the wearer's hand. Extremely useful for safely handling dangerous artifacts. The [[Wardenship]] has three and considers them essential equipment.

The gauntlets are heavy — approximately four kilograms each, constructed from layered metal plates over a chain mesh lining, with no visible sigil work on any surface. They appear to be purely mechanical objects, which is theoretically impossible given their function. The [[Duskfrost Academy]]'s materials analysis has identified the metal as an alloy of iron, copper, and a third component that registers on spectrographic analysis but does not correspond to any known element. This unknown component appears in trace amounts — less than 2% of the alloy's composition — but removing it (as the Academy attempted with a fragment from a damaged fourth gauntlet) causes the suppression effect to cease entirely. The working theory is that the trace element is the same unidentifiable metal found in the [[Angel Crown]], the [[Elemental Band]], and other pre-Calamity artifacts, appearing here in a different alloy context.

The suppression field is absolute within its radius: all arcane effects cease. Active sigils go dark. Enchantments suspend. [[Blood Binding Contracts|Blood-bindings]] temporarily lose their enforcement mechanism (a discovery that has made the gauntlets a high-priority target for several individuals currently under binding contracts). The field also suppresses the wearer's own arcane abilities, which means the gauntlet cannot be used by a practitioner who needs to simultaneously perform sigil work — a significant operational limitation. The Wardenship's standard procedure is to pair a gauntlet-equipped handler with an unequipped practitioner who provides arcane support from outside the suppression radius.

The gauntlet is frequently used when handling the [[Heartwood Staff]], whose properties resist conventional containment, and during transport of the [[Hell's Statue]], whose infernal energy signature the gauntlet suppresses completely while active. The three working gauntlets are assigned to the Wardenship's Artifact Response Unit and are never stored in the same location — a precaution against theft that also reflects the uncomfortable reality that if all three gauntlets were in the same room, the combined suppression field would disable every ward and enchantment in the building, including the structural wards that keep the building standing. The damaged fourth gauntlet, reduced to fragments during the Academy's analysis, is the reason the Academy no longer requests to study working gauntlets. The Wardenship's refusal is polite. The underlying message is not.
