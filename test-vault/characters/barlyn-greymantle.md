# Barlyn Greymantle

Barlyn Greymantle has been laying stone in Duskfrost for sixty-three years, which is young for a [[Cirilae]] and old for a building career. She holds the rank of master mason in the [[Marblesmiths]] guild and one of the two Cirilae seats on the [[Amberburgh]] district council, positions she treats with identical seriousness: you measure twice, you cut once, and you do not leave the work half-finished.

Her hands tell her professional history — the mortar dust of six decades has seeped into the porous surface of her stone skin, leaving permanent pale streaks across her knuckles and palms. She considers this unremarkable. Other species find it slightly alarming, which she considers their problem.

On the district council, Barlyn represents the Cirilae community's interests with the same methodical persistence she brings to masonry. She speaks rarely in sessions, asks precise questions, and votes on the basis of whether a proposal will actually work rather than whether it sounds impressive. This makes her unpopular with councillors who prefer impressive-sounding proposals and popular with the ones who have to implement them.

Her relationship with [[Grolgrurim Bluntgut]] is the relationship between craft and commerce — she builds things, he sells access to the people who build things, and they have maintained this division of labor for forty years without significant disagreement. She considers him a competent businessman. He considers her the best mason in the guild. Both assessments are professional rather than personal, which is how Cirilae prefer it.

She was part of the Reconstruction-era workforce — not directly, she is too young for that, but her mother was, and the stories of building [[Highwallow]]'s flood barriers while the Resonance Scar destabilized the ground beneath them are part of Barlyn's professional inheritance. She maintains those barriers personally, on her own time, because no one else remembers which stones are structural and which are cosmetic.
