# Jar of Paradise

A sealed glass jar containing a self-sustaining ecosystem approximately the size of a small room when accessed from inside. Origin unknown. Currently in the possession of a [[Highwallow]] herbalist who uses it to grow medicinal plants year-round. She declines all offers to purchase it.

From the outside, the jar is unremarkable — a thick-walled glass vessel approximately thirty centimeters tall, sealed with a ceramic stopper that does not appear to be removable. The interior, viewed from outside, shows a miniature garden: tiny plants, a small pool of water, insects the size of dust motes, and what appears to be soil. From the inside — because the jar can be entered, through a mechanism no one has successfully described — the space is approximately four meters square, with a ceiling high enough to stand comfortably. The plants are full-sized. The pool is deep enough to draw water from. The air is warm, humid, and smells of growing things. The transition between outside and inside is instantaneous and disorienting: one moment you are holding a jar, the next you are standing in a garden with the jar's glass walls curving overhead like a greenhouse dome. Leaving works the same way in reverse, though the herbalist reports that the exit sometimes takes a moment longer than the entry, as though the jar is reluctant to let go.

The herbalist — known locally as Grandmother Tessith, though she is neither old enough nor related to enough people for the title to be literal — acquired the jar from a [[Gnaino]] trader approximately fifteen years ago in exchange for a service she declines to specify. She uses the interior space to cultivate plants that cannot survive Highwallow's climate: tropical medicinal herbs, heat-loving fungi, and three species that the [[Duskfrost Academy]]'s botany department has been unable to identify and suspects may be pre-Calamity survivors. The interior's ecosystem requires no external input — the water cycle is self-sustaining, the soil regenerates, and the light source (a warm, diffuse glow from the glass walls themselves) provides adequate growing conditions year-round.

The Academy has offered to purchase the jar four times, study it twice, and classify it as a restricted artifact once. Grandmother Tessith has declined all seven proposals with the same explanation: "It grows what I need it to grow." The Academy's attempts to create similar pocket-dimension containers have produced results ranging from "catastrophic implosion" to "functional for approximately eleven seconds before collapsing." The jar's stability — fifteen years of continuous use with no degradation — suggests either pre-Calamity engineering of extraordinary sophistication or something that was not engineered at all but grown. The Gnaino trader who sold it to Tessith has not been seen in Highwallow since the sale. Tessith does not seem concerned by this.
