# Thyren

Thyren are humanoids whose biology exists in active symbiosis with plant life. Their touch accelerates growth in compatible species — a seedling becomes a sapling in hours, a cut stem re-roots overnight. Roughly 120 Thyren live in Duskfrost, most of them in or near [[Rappiop Grove]], where the concentration of cultivated plant life makes the district feel, in their words, "loud enough to think in."

The symbiosis is not metaphorical. Thyren skin hosts a colony of symbiotic microorganisms that produce growth-accelerating compounds on contact with compatible plant tissue. The relationship is mutual: the microorganisms draw nutrients from the Thyren's bloodstream, and in return provide enhanced immune function and a subtle photosynthetic supplement that means Thyren need roughly a third less food than a [[Human]] of equivalent size, provided they get adequate sunlight. Thyren who spend too long underground or in windowless rooms grow sluggish and irritable — a condition they share, with mutual sympathy, with houseplants.

Their skin shifts color with the seasons — warmer brown in winter, greener in summer — which other species find charming and the Thyren find unremarkable, the way a Human might find it unremarkable that their hair grows. More distinctive is the Thyren practice of wearing living plant cuttings braided into their hair, each cutting maintained through skin contact and chosen for personal or cultural significance. A Thyren's hair-garden is considered intimate; asking to touch it is roughly equivalent to asking to read someone's diary.

The Academy contracts seven Thyren to maintain Rappiop Grove's living architecture — the hedge walls, tree-arches, and the massive canopy structure over the Grove's central amphitheater. This work is Duskfrost's most visible Thyren contribution, but the community's larger economic role is in urban agriculture. Thyren-tended rooftop gardens across [[Highwallow]] and [[Amberburgh]] produce a disproportionate share of the city's fresh vegetables, and their services are in constant demand from anyone trying to grow anything in Duskfrost's uncooperative climate.

Thyren culture is patient and cyclical, organized around growing seasons rather than calendar dates. Their community decisions are made in what they call bloom-councils — gatherings held when a designated indicator plant flowers, which may happen on schedule or may not. Other species find this approach to scheduling maddening. The Thyren consider rigid timekeeping a surface-level anxiety that ignores natural rhythm.

Their relationship with the [[Bhissalae]] is the most active cross-species research collaboration in Duskfrost. Bhissalae biologists studying aquatic symbiosis have found Thyren plant-bonding to be the closest terrestrial parallel, and the two communities share data, specimens, and an annual joint symposium that the Academy funds and most other species find impenetrably technical. The Thyren who maintain the harbor-side gardens have developed a practical exchange with [[Zuvine]] foragers — cultivated algae for freshwater specimens — that has outlasted three changes of harbor management.
