# The Hollow Roads

The Hollow Roads are a network of pre-Calamity tunnels beneath and around Duskfrost, connecting to an unknown number of other locations at unknown distances. The [[Duskfrost Academy]] has mapped approximately 20% of the accessible tunnel network. The [[Gnaino]] know more. They are not sharing.

The mapped sections reveal tunnels of remarkable engineering — smooth-walled passages averaging three meters in diameter, cut through bedrock with a precision that modern tools cannot replicate. The walls bear faded [[Antecedent Script]] inscriptions at regular intervals, most too degraded to read. Where they are legible, they appear to be directional markers and distance notations, suggesting the network was a transportation system rather than a defensive or industrial installation. The tunnels maintain a constant temperature regardless of season or surface weather, and the air remains breathable even in deep sections sealed for centuries, implying a ventilation system that still functions through unknown means.

Access points within Duskfrost are scattered unevenly. [[Bellsummit]] has three known entrances, all in the basements of government buildings and all secured by the [[Wardenship]]. [[Highwallow]] has at least seven, several of which open directly into the [[Resonance Scar Tunnels|Scar-adjacent tunnel sections]] where conditions become significantly more dangerous. [[Scorchhelm]] has two military-controlled access points. [[Amberburgh]] has none that the authorities acknowledge, though the [[Bonehands]] are widely believed to use an undocumented entrance for smuggling operations. The Gnaino use the tunnel network as their primary transportation infrastructure and have done so since before the Reconstruction era. Their knowledge of the full network extent — including where the tunnels lead beyond Duskfrost — is possibly the most valuable geographic intelligence in the region, and they treat it as a closely guarded trade secret.

The unmapped 80% is unmapped for reasons. Academy survey teams report that beyond the mapped perimeter, tunnels branch unpredictably, some sections show signs of deliberate collapse, and navigational instruments fail with increasing frequency the deeper one goes. Three survey teams have been lost entirely — walked in, did not walk out, no remains found. The Academy's current policy requires all survey expeditions to maintain a continuous rope line back to mapped territory, carry a minimum of two [[Speaking Stone]] pairs, and include at least one practitioner capable of emergency sigil work. These precautions are sensible. Whether they are sufficient is a question the Academy answers by not sending teams deeper than the current map boundary without compelling reason.
