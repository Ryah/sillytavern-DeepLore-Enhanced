# Phantom Bridge Legend

[[Highwallow]] residents have reported a recurring visual phenomenon near the Scar's eastern boundary: the apparent outline of a bridge that does not exist, visible at dawn and dusk in certain atmospheric conditions. The [[Duskfrost Academy]]'s official position is optical refraction caused by the [[Resonance Scar Properties|Scar]]'s arcane turbulence. The unofficial position of most Highwallow residents is that the bridge is a memory of something that stood there before the Calamity.

The phenomenon has been documented since at least Year 180, when a Highwallow district clerk included a note in the quarterly infrastructure report: "Bridge visible again at eastern Scar boundary, dawn, approximately fifteen minutes duration. No bridge exists at this location. Recommend investigation." The investigation was never conducted. The clerk's successors continued noting the appearances, creating an unintentional longitudinal record that the Academy's historical department now considers one of the most valuable data sets on Scar boundary behavior. The bridge appears most frequently during equinox periods — roughly twice per year at dawn, occasionally at dusk — and always at the same location, spanning a gap in the Scar boundary where the old district road used to cross from Highwallow into what is now uninhabitable Scar territory.

Several Academy researchers have attempted to study the phenomenon directly. The results are consistently strange. Arcane measurement instruments register a resonance signature during appearances that matches no known sigil pattern but is internally consistent — the same frequencies, the same harmonic structure, every time. Photographic capture via alchemical plates fails; the bridge appears to the eye but not to any recording medium. One researcher, in Year 304, walked toward the bridge during an appearance and reported that it maintained apparent distance — no matter how far she walked, the bridge remained the same distance away. She stopped when she realized she had crossed into Scar territory without noticing, having been drawn approximately two hundred meters past the boundary markers. She returned unharmed but declined to repeat the experiment.

The [[Zuvine Song Cycles]] contain a passage that the elder singers translate as "the road that remembers where it went." Whether this refers to the phantom bridge or to a general property of the Scar is debated. Highwallow children dare each other to watch for the bridge at dawn; parents discourage this not because the bridge is dangerous but because the Scar boundary, where one must stand to see it, very much is. The legend persists because it offers something the Academy's optical refraction explanation does not: the suggestion that the world before the Calamity is not entirely gone, that the land itself retains some echo of what it was. This is comforting. Whether it should be is another question.
