# Resonance Frequency Theory

The current [[Duskfrost Academy]] model holds that arcane energy operates in discrete frequency bands, similar to sound harmonics. The pre-Calamity resonance network achieved its extraordinary power by aligning the frequencies of all towers simultaneously — essentially creating a city-wide chord. The current research question is whether the discovered fragment beneath [[Rappiop Grove]] is still broadcasting its frequency, and whether anything nearby is listening.

The theory emerged from [[Ayre Waesphyra]]'s work in Year 289, building on earlier observations that sigils of similar design produce slightly different effects depending on location and time of year. Waesphyra demonstrated that these variations correlate with measurable fluctuations in the ambient arcane field — fluctuations that follow periodic patterns resembling acoustic harmonics. Her model proposes that [[Sigil Theory — Fundamentals|sigils]] work not by generating arcane energy but by resonating with specific frequency bands of energy already present in the environment, much as a tuning fork does not create sound but selects and amplifies a specific frequency from ambient vibration. The implications are significant: sigil geometry determines which frequency band is engaged, sigil precision determines the clarity of resonance, and sigil errors produce the equivalent of a badly tuned instrument — noise, distortion, and occasionally destructive interference.

The theory explains several previously puzzling phenomena. The [[Resonance Scar Properties|Resonance Scar]]'s behavior becomes interpretable as broadband arcane noise — the ley line convergence point radiating across all frequency bands simultaneously, drowning out the specific frequencies that sigils need to function cleanly. This is why sigils in the Scar zone have a 12% malfunction rate: they are trying to resonate with a specific frequency in an environment saturated with every frequency at once. The [[Ley Line Geography of Duskfrost|ley lines]] themselves, under this model, are natural waveguides — geological structures that channel specific frequency bands preferentially, creating corridors of enhanced resonance that the pre-Calamity civilization exploited for their tower network.

The fragment beneath Rappiop Grove is broadcasting on a frequency that Waesphyra has designated F-Prime — a fundamental tone that her instruments have detected nowhere else, at an energy level that suggests the fragment is not a passive relic but an active transmitter. The [[Oracles of Might]] have confirmed that sensitive instruments in [[Bellsummit]] can detect F-Prime as a faint background signal. The question of whether "anything nearby is listening" is not rhetorical. The [[Seven Seals]], if the Pallasys translations are correct, were designed to respond to specific resonance frequencies. If any Seal is within range of F-Prime's broadcast, and if the Seal's response mechanism still functions, then the fragment's continued operation is not merely an archaeological curiosity. It is a signal that may already have been answered.
