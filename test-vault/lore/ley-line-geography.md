# Ley Line Geography of Duskfrost

Three major ley lines converge beneath what is now the [[Highwallow]] district — this convergence is almost certainly why the pre-Calamity civilization built their primary resonance tower node there, and almost certainly why the Calamity did its most severe damage to that location. The [[Resonance Scar Properties|Resonance Scar]] marks the intersection point.

The three lines run roughly north-south, east-west, and diagonally from the deep harbor to the inland mountains. Each carries a measurable flow of ambient arcane energy — not enough to power sigil work directly, but enough to amplify it significantly. Practitioners working near a ley line report their sigils requiring less effort and producing stronger effects, a phenomenon the [[Duskfrost Academy]] has documented extensively but cannot fully explain. The current model, based on [[Resonance Frequency Theory]], holds that ley lines are natural conduits in the earth's arcane substrate — channels where energy flows preferentially due to geological composition. The pre-Calamity civilization appears to have mapped these conduits and built their resonance tower network to tap them, essentially using the ley lines as power cables for a city-scale arcane grid.

The convergence point beneath Highwallow is where all three lines intersect within a radius of approximately two hundred meters. Before the Calamity, this would have been the network's primary node — the point of highest energy concentration and the most logical location for the system's central control infrastructure. After the Calamity, it became the epicenter of the grid's catastrophic failure. The Resonance Scar is the wound that failure left behind: a zone where the ley line energies, no longer channeled by the tower network, bleed into the surface in chaotic, unpredictable patterns. The Scar's boundary fluctuations track the ley lines' own energy cycles, expanding when flow increases and contracting when it ebbs.

Secondary ley line intersections exist beneath [[Bellsummit]] (two lines crossing) and near [[Bayside Shushail]] (where the harbor line meets a minor tributary line). These secondary nodes show much milder effects — slightly elevated ambient arcane levels, minor instrument interference during high-flow periods — but they are monitored by the Academy as early warning indicators. If the Highwallow convergence point ever destabilizes further, the secondary nodes would likely be affected first. The [[Oracles of Might]] maintain measurement stations at both secondary points, staffed year-round. The data from these stations is classified. The fact that the stations were quietly doubled in staffing last year is not.
