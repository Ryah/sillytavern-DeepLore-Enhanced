# Duskfrost Naming Conventions

Names in Duskfrost carry species, class, and regional information in ways that residents read instinctively and outsiders miss entirely.

**Humans** use a given name and a family surname. Surnames often derive from occupation (Hollowell, Marchant, Quince), geography (Dunmore, Solvik), or ancestor reputation. [[Bellsummit]] families tend toward longer, more formal names. [[Highwallow]] families trend shorter and blunter. Some surnames have been anglicized from older languages that predate the Calamity.

**Elves** use a given name and a lineage name, which traces maternal descent. Elven names are precise, vowel-rich, and often three or four syllables. An elf's full name includes their lineage and a generation marker, but casual use drops the marker. Elven naming is conservative — the same names recur across centuries, with subtle pronunciation shifts marking the era.

**Dwarves** use a given name and a clan-trade name that describes the family's historical occupation or notable achievement. Names like "Bluntgut" (blunt instrument makers), "Deepbeard" (deep miners), and "Kegfeet" (brewers) are literal. Dwarves consider this efficient.

**Orcs** in Duskfrost use single names, sometimes with an earned epithet added after a significant event. The epithet is not hereditary — each orc earns or does not earn their own. Using a dead orc's epithet is considered a grave insult.

**[[Zuvine]]** use a given name and a tide-name — a surname that references the tidal cycle during which they were born. Zuvine linguists can identify birth season, birth location, and family line from a full tide-name, though surface-dwellers rarely hear the full form.

**[[Bolludine]]** use a given name and a shard-name (the family network they belong to). The shard-name comes first in formal address among Bolludine, last in address with other species. This reversal is deliberate — it puts the family's identity first where it matters.

**[[Cirilae]]** use a given name and a stone-name describing the color or texture of their skin at birth. Stone-names like "Greymantle" or "Sandvein" are descriptive and permanent. Cirilae consider changing one's stone-name equivalent to denying one's origin.

**[[Gnaino]]** names are echolocation patterns rendered into surface-language approximations. The actual Gnaino name is a sound that other species cannot reproduce. What surface-dwellers call them is a translation of convenience, which the Gnaino tolerate.

**[[Delivese]]** use a given name and a place-name referencing the family's settlement of origin. As the Delivese migrated south over generations, the place-names became increasingly historical — referencing settlements that no longer exist.
