# Bhissalae

The Bhissalae are an amphibious species native to freshwater estuaries, smaller than [[Zuvine]] and adapted to shallower, murkier waters. Roughly 250 live in Duskfrost, concentrated along the [[Redpond Waterfront]] and the silted canals feeding into [[Zuvine Harbor District]]. Their mottled green-brown skin shifts shade with water salinity — a useful trait in the brackish channels where they do most of their work.

Where Zuvines dominate the deep-water salvage trade and the open harbor, Bhissalae work the shallows — canal maintenance, silt dredging, underwater structural inspection, and the recovery of goods that fall off docks or wash into the drainage system. It is unglamorous work that the Zuvine consider beneath them, and the Bhissalae have built a quiet monopoly on it. The [[Salvagers Compact]] recognizes both species' territorial claims, though the boundary between "shallow" and "deep" is a subject of recurring, occasionally violent dispute.

Bhissalae culture centers on the tide-house — a communal gathering hall built half-submerged, where community decisions are made in water. The logic is practical: Bhissalae communicate emotional nuance through subtle changes in their skin patterns, which are most visible when submerged. Dry-land conversation is considered inherently imprecise, which is why Bhissalae have a reputation among surface species for being blunt to the point of rudeness. They are not being rude. They are compensating for a medium they consider inadequate.

Their relationship with the Zuvine is more complex than simple rivalry. The two species share ecological needs and historical grievances against a surface-centric reconstruction, but compete directly for salvage contracts and harbor access. Bhissalae elders maintain that their ancestors were in the estuary channels before the Zuvine arrived from the open coast, a claim the Zuvine dispute. The [[Triumvirate]] has declined to adjudicate, which both species interpret as a slight.

Bhissalae cooking — heavy on fermented kelp, pickled freshwater shellfish, and canal-grown herbs — has found an unexpected following among [[Bolludine]] couriers, who appreciate the high-salt, high-energy profile. Several Bhissalae-run food stalls in [[Redpond]] serve a mixed clientele, and these have become one of the few genuinely cross-species social spaces on the waterfront.
