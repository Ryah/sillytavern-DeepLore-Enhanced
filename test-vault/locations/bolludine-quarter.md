# Bolludine Quarter

Three adjacent streets in central [[Amberburgh]] where the three major [[Bolludine]] shard networks maintain their public-facing operations. The streets are narrow, cobbled, and perpetually busy with foot traffic that moves faster than anywhere else in the city. Couriers arrive and depart continuously — small, quick-footed Bolludine runners carrying message shards in leather pouches, weaving through the crowd with the practiced urgency of people for whom time is literally currency. The information exchange that happens here is the informal backbone of Duskfrost's commercial intelligence system.

The buildings are modest by Amberburgh standards — two and three stories, stone foundations with timber upper floors, their facades distinguished mainly by the shard-shaped signs that mark each network's office. Inside, the receiving halls are cramped and efficient: counters for intake, sorting rooms behind frosted glass partitions, and the constant crystalline click of message shards being catalogued, routed, and dispatched. The air smells of sealing wax, courier sweat, and the faintly metallic scent of fresh shard-work. Each network maintains its own coding system, its own courier routes, and its own fiercely guarded client lists. Cooperation between the three networks is limited to the shared understanding that a price war would destroy all of them.

The Quarter is loudest at dawn and dusk, when the commercial day's opening and closing messages flood through simultaneously. Between those peaks, the streets settle into a working rhythm — merchants checking for replies, couriers refueling at the two tea houses that serve the Quarter, and the occasional [[Merchants League]] representative making a conspicuously casual visit. The [[Solarguard]] patrols here lightly, partly because the Bolludine handle their own security and partly because the couriers know the patrol schedules better than the officers do.

What makes the Quarter politically significant is not what it does openly but what it knows incidentally. Every message that passes through a shard network leaves metadata — sender, recipient, urgency, routing. The networks insist this information is confidential. The Triumvirate, the Merchants League, and the [[Bonehands]] all operate on the assumption that it is not.
