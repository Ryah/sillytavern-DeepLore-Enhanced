# Duskfrost Daily Life

Duskfrost wakes early because the harbor does. Dock workers start before dawn. Amberburgh's markets open at first light. [[Bellsummit]]'s institutions begin at eighth bell, which is late enough to be a class signifier. [[Highwallow]] operates on its own schedule, which is to say continuously.

**Food:** The city eats fish, grain, and root vegetables. The harbor provides the first. The [[Harcyne Granary Authority|Harcyne Granaries]] control the second. The third grows in the marsh gardens outside Highwallow, tended mostly by [[Thyren]] cultivators. Meat is a luxury — livestock requires grazing land the city does not have. Preserved fish and dense bread are working-class staples. [[Bellsummit]] dining features imported spices and fresh harbor catches that arrive before the public markets open, a service the fishing guilds provide for a premium.

**Drink:** Ale is universal. Wine is imported and expensive. [[Amberburgh]] taverns serve a local spirit called brine-gin, distilled from grain and flavored with harbor kelp. It tastes exactly as bad as it sounds. People drink it anyway.

**Entertainment:** Tavern culture dominates. Storytelling, dice games, and amateur music fill the evenings in the harbor district. [[Bellsummit]] hosts formal salons where politics is conducted under the pretense of cultural events. The [[Night Market Guild|night market]] in Amberburgh is entertainment as much as commerce.

**Festivals:** Two major public holidays survive. The **Thaw Festival** marks the spring breakup of harbor ice — three days of markets, public drinking, and the controlled chaos of dock workers celebrating the end of winter unemployment. **Remembrance Night** in autumn marks the anniversary of the Calamity with lantern processions, silence at sunset, and private gatherings. It is the closest thing Duskfrost has to a sacred occasion.

**Social customs:** Duskfrost runs on favors, not friendship. People remember who helped them and who did not. The concept of "owing" is central to social relationships — a debt unpaid is a relationship unstated. The [[Cirilae]] take this furthest, but every species participates. Direct refusal is considered rude. Indirect refusal — "I'll consider it," meaning no — is considered polite. Asking twice after an indirect refusal is considered aggressive.

**Taboos:** Public displays of uncontrolled magic. Discussing someone's Calamity-era family losses uninvited. Entering a [[Zuvine]] residence without removing shoes. Touching a Bolludine's antennae. Interrupting a Cirilae mid-task. These are not laws. They are the things everyone knows and newcomers learn the hard way.
