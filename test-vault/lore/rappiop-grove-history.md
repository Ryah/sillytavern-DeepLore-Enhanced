# Rappiop Grove History

Rappiop Grove was established as a botanical research garden by the [[Duskfrost Academy]] in Year 180. For 132 years it was exactly what it appeared to be. The pre-Calamity resonance grid fragment beneath it was discovered during routine foundation inspection in Year 312 — the discovery that precipitated the current political crisis. The grove is now under joint Academy-[[Duskfrost Triumvirate|Triumvirate]] control. The original botanical research was discontinued and no one has watered the specimens.

The grove's founding was unremarkable: the Academy acquired a plot of land in [[Amberburgh]]'s eastern district to study regional flora, particularly species with arcane-reactive properties. The site was chosen for its soil quality and its distance from the [[Resonance Scar Properties|Resonance Scar]], which would have interfered with controlled experiments. For over a century, the grove produced steady, unglamorous research — cataloguing local species, testing cultivation techniques for alchemical reagents, and maintaining a seed bank that supplied the Academy's medical programs. The botanical staff numbered between four and eight researchers at any given time. Their annual budget was the smallest in the Academy. Nobody paid them much attention, which suited them.

The foundation inspection of Year 312 was triggered by subsidence in the grove's western beds — the ground sinking approximately two centimeters over a three-month period. A structural engineer discovered that the subsidence was caused by a void beneath the grove's foundations: a sealed chamber containing what subsequent analysis identified as a functioning fragment of the pre-Calamity resonance grid. Not a ruin. Not a relic. A piece of the network that destroyed the world, still active, still broadcasting its [[Resonance Frequency Theory|resonance frequency]] into the [[Ley Line Geography of Duskfrost|ley line]] beneath it. The botanical staff's reaction was to suggest monitoring the fragment's effect on plant growth. The Academy's reaction was to classify the discovery. The Triumvirate's reaction was to deploy the [[Wardenship]].

The fragment's existence is now the worst-kept classified secret in Duskfrost and the direct cause of the tensions being called the [[War of Glimmering Hope]]. Three Triumvirate houses want control of it for different reasons — research, military application, and economic leverage, respectively. The Academy wants to study it without political interference. The [[Oracles of Might]] want it sealed and forgotten, on the grounds that the last civilization that operated this technology no longer exists. The grove itself — once a quiet garden where researchers grew medicinal herbs and argued about soil composition — is now a fortified research site surrounded by Wardenship sentries, warded to Tier 3 standards, and visited weekly by representatives of all three Triumvirate houses who tour the facilities and take notes they do not share with each other.
