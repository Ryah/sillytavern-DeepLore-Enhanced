# Amberburgh

Amberburgh is Duskfrost's commercial center — the district where things are bought, sold, negotiated, and occasionally stolen. It sits between the wealthy [[Bellsummit]] heights and the working-class harbor districts below, and its character reflects that position: ambitious, busy, politically pragmatic.

<div class="meta-block">
[Type: District | Elevation: Mid-cliff | Watch Presence: Moderate | Social Class: Middle to upper-middle | Key Institutions: Merchants League, [[Marblesmiths]], Night Market | Population: ~18,000]
</div>

The district's central market — officially the [[Amberburgh Exchange]], colloquially "the Scramble" — runs six days a week and handles everything from grain futures to illegal arcane components, depending on the hour and who's watching. The [[Bolludine]] shard networks operate out of three adjacent streets and effectively function as the district's information exchange alongside the physical goods market.

The night market activates at sunset in the district's eastern quarter. Nominally unlicensed, practically tolerated. Majority-[[Orvathi]] operated. The [[Solarguard]] maintains a deliberate blind spot.

The Marblesmiths guildhouse anchors the western end of the district. The Merchants League occupies a purpose-built hall in the center. Three banking houses — licensed, regulated, and believed to be honest within the definition of that term — cluster around the Exchange.

Political character: Amberburgh is the closest thing Duskfrost has to a neutral faction. It does business with everyone and endorses no one. This makes it simultaneously the most useful and least trusted district by all three Triumvirate houses.
