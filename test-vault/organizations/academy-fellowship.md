# Academy Alumni Fellowship

The Academy Alumni Fellowship is the social and professional network for graduates of the [[Duskfrost Academy]]. It holds quarterly dinners, maintains an employment referral network, and lobbies the Academy administration on matters of alumni interest. Its political influence exceeds its formal importance — which is to say, its formal importance is modest and its political influence is considerable.

<div class="meta-block">
[Type: Professional Network | HQ: Borrowed rooms, Academy East Wing | Chair: Alderman Fossick Grenn | Members: ~180 active (of ~900 living graduates) | Founded: Year 156 | Meetings: Quarterly dinners + ad hoc committees]
</div>

The Fellowship's quarterly dinners are held in the Academy's East Wing reception hall, which the administration lends with the understanding that the Fellows will remember this generosity when budget petitions reach the Triumvirate. The dinners are precisely as dull as they sound — mediocre wine, speeches about tradition, polite competition over whose career has advanced furthest — but the conversations in the corridors afterward are where the real business happens. Committee chairs for Academy advisory boards are selected here. Contracts for arcane consulting work flow through the referral network. [[Bellsummit District Council]] members who happen to be Fellows hear about Academy priorities before they become official requests. The Fellowship is, in function, the Academy's unofficial lobbying arm, staffed by people who would be offended by the description and unable to refute it.

Several prominent alumni maintain dual membership in the [[Salvagers Compact]], which the Fellowship officially neither endorses nor discourages. This diplomatic neutrality conceals a genuine split: the Fellowship's older members view salvage work as beneath Academy graduates, while younger members see it as one of the few fields where an arcane education still guarantees employment. Chair Fossick Grenn, a retired Academy lecturer with a talent for strategic ambiguity, manages this tension by refusing to acknowledge it exists.

The Fellowship's current preoccupation is the Academy's declining enrollment — down fifteen percent over the last decade, as talented young people increasingly pursue commercial arcane work through the [[Oracles of Might]] rather than completing a full Academy program. Several Fellows have proposed subsidizing tuition. Others argue the problem is not cost but relevance. The argument has continued for two years without resolution, which Grenn considers progress because at least no one has resigned over it yet.
