# The Calamity Onset

Year Zero. The resonance tower network failed in a cascade that lasted approximately six hours from first failure to total collapse. No one alive witnessed it and survived to write about it coherently. What is known has been reconstructed from archaeology, geological evidence, [[Zuvine]] deep-water oral histories, and the fragments that Thornwick Pallasys translated from pre-Calamity sources over a lifetime of painstaking scholarship.

The failure began at the primary node — the largest tower, located at what is now the center of the Resonance Scar in [[Highwallow]], precisely where three major ley lines converge beneath the city. Whether the first failure was accidental, deliberate, or the result of the weaponization experiments referenced in [[War of Kroyhr]]-era correspondence is the central question of [[Calamity Theories|Calamity scholarship]], and it remains unanswered. What is known is that the primary node's failure sent a cascading resonance pulse through the connected tower network. Each tower that received the pulse amplified it before failing in turn, creating a chain reaction that expanded outward from the city at a speed survivors described as "the horizon catching fire."

The energy release reshaped the harbor — the previous coastline was approximately 200 meters further out than the current one, and the underwater cave networks the [[Zuvine]] now inhabit were created by the collapse of coastal bedrock. Approximately two-thirds of the existing city was destroyed outright. The Resonance Scar — a zone of permanently destabilized arcane energy covering roughly twelve city blocks — marks the epicenter. The population of the pre-Calamity city is estimated at 150,000 to 200,000. The population of the region one year after the Calamity is estimated at fewer than 8,000. The difference is not entirely accounted for by death; many fled, and their destinations are unknown.

The Calamity did not simply destroy a city. It destroyed a civilization's confidence in the technology that had defined it. The resonance grid had powered everything — heating, lighting, communication, transportation, manufacturing, and apparently warfare. Its loss was not like losing electricity; it was like losing the ability to think in a language that had been native for centuries. The survivors who eventually built what became Duskfrost did so in the ruins of a world they could see but no longer understand, surrounded by infrastructure they could not repair, reading inscriptions in a script they could not fully decipher.

The [[Duskfrost Academy]]'s Year Zero memorial — held annually, attended sparsely — includes a reading of the Pallasys Fragment, the only translated pre-Calamity text that appears to describe the moment of failure. It reads, in part: "The towers sang wrong. We knew. We knew and we listened anyway."
