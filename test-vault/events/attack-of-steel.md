# Attack of Steel

In Year 112, a fleet of fourteen northern pirate longships entered Duskfrost harbor at dawn under cover of a seasonal fog bank. The harbor at that time had minimal defenses — a chain boom that hadn't been maintained in years and a garrison of fewer than fifty militia. The pirates cut the chain, beached six ships on the inner quay, and spent the better part of three days burning, looting, and killing before a hastily assembled coalition of Triumvirate house guards, [[Champions of Patience]] volunteers, and armed dockworkers drove them back to the waterline.

The name "Attack of Steel" comes from the pirates' distinctive armament — full iron plate armor of a quality Duskfrost's smiths could not match at the time, likely salvaged or traded from a northern pre-Calamity weapons cache. The psychological impact was considerable. Duskfrost's defenders, accustomed to fighting lightly armored raiders, found their weapons ineffective against opponents who shrugged off sword strikes. The breakthrough came when an unnamed Academy practitioner — possibly a [[Wardenship]] affiliate, though records are sparse — used a heat-conduction sigil to superheat the pirates' own armor. The technique killed several pirates and caused the rest to retreat. It also killed the practitioner, who apparently had not accounted for the sigil's area of effect.

The attack killed approximately 200 residents and destroyed a quarter of the harbor district's warehouses. The Triumvirate's response was immediate and, for once, unified: they authorized the construction of the [[Scorchhelm]] fortification complex on the eastern cliff face, established a permanent harbor watch (predecessor to the harbor patrol later absorbed into the [[Solarguard]]), and invested in a proper chain-boom system with arcane reinforcement. The fortification project took eighteen years to complete and bankrupted two minor merchant families who had been contractually obligated to contribute. The pirates never returned in comparable force, though whether this was due to the fortifications or to the simple fact that the northern pirate confederation collapsed in Year 119 for unrelated reasons remains a matter of scholarly debate.

The attack's lasting legacy, beyond the physical fortifications, was the establishment of Duskfrost's doctrine that the harbor must be defensible at all times — a principle that has shaped every subsequent [[Harbour Expansion Project]] and trade negotiation involving the waterfront.
