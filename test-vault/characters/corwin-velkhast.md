# Corwin Velkhast

Corwin Velkhast is the second claimant to the Velkhast house seat, which makes him the candidate that the [[Solarguard]] quietly supports, the [[Bonehands]] actively opposes, and the Academy officially has no opinion about. He is thirty-one years old, the dead house head's nephew by a second marriage, and he has spent the last two years trying to claim a seat of power in a city that rewards patience he does not naturally possess.

His claim is legally sound — second in line by the house's own succession charter, elevated to first by the disappearance of the original heir eight months ago. The first claimant vanished without a body, which means Corwin cannot be formally confirmed until the Triumvirate council declares the first claimant dead. The council has declined to make that declaration, citing insufficient evidence. Whether this reflects genuine legal caution or deliberate obstruction depends on which house you believe is doing the obstructing.

Corwin is competent without being exceptional. He understands the [[Velkhast Trading Company]]'s operations because he worked in its accounting office for six years before the succession opened. He understands harbor politics because he grew up watching his uncle navigate them. He does not understand the Academy's institutional maneuvering, which is his most significant vulnerability — [[Archmage Tessavel Orindal]] could end the succession dispute with a public endorsement, and she has not, and he cannot determine why.

His relationship with [[Warden-Captain Orin Castellan]] is his strongest political asset. Castellan's quiet support gives Corwin effective control of the Solarguard's institutional weight without requiring him to make promises he might not keep. He is aware that Castellan is backing him because she considers the third claimant a [[Bonehands]] proxy, not because she considers Corwin the best candidate. He accepts this because the alternative is having no backing at all.

The third claimant — a distant cousin with harbor connections and no administrative experience — has the Bonehands' support, which means money, muscle, and the ability to disrupt Velkhast trade operations at will. Corwin considers this a temporary advantage. The Bonehands consider it leverage. Both are correct.
