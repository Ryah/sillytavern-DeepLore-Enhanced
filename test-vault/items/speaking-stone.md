# Speaking Stone

Paired stones that allow two-way communication between holders regardless of distance, within the same city. Pre-Calamity manufacture. The [[Duskfrost Academy]] produces weak imitations at significant expense. True Speaking Stones are rare and enormously valuable. Three verified pairs are known to exist in Duskfrost.

Each stone is a smooth, palm-sized oval of dark material — not precisely stone, not precisely metal, with a surface temperature that matches the holder's body heat within seconds of contact. When one stone of a pair is held and spoken to, the words emerge from its counterpart with perfect fidelity, no delay, and no degradation regardless of intervening distance or obstacles. The communication is bidirectional and simultaneous — both holders can speak and hear at the same time, a capability the Academy's imitations cannot replicate (the imitations require turn-taking, with a two-second switching delay that makes urgent conversation infuriatingly slow). The stones require no activation, no arcane energy input, and no maintenance. They have been functioning for an estimated four centuries with no sign of degradation.

The three verified pairs are distributed as follows: one pair is held by the [[Oracles of Might]], connecting their Bellsummit tower to a field research station outside the city; one pair is in Wardenship custody, used for emergency communication between [[Scorchhelm]] garrison and the central Watch command; and one pair is split between the [[Archive Master Key|Archive]] vault (where one stone sits next to the Master Key) and an unknown counterpart location somewhere in the [[Hollow Roads]] tunnel network. This third pair is the most intriguing, as the Academy has detected intermittent communication from the tunnel-side stone — brief transmissions, apparently automated, consisting of a repeating pattern of tones rather than speech. [[Gorduin Wynlar]] has recorded and analyzed these transmissions and believes they are status signals from a pre-Calamity automated system that does not know its operators are four centuries dead.

The communication principle appears related to the binding resonance used in the [[Mirror of Binding]] — both technologies create a persistent link between paired objects that operates independently of distance and physical barriers. The [[Bolludine Shard Politics|Bolludine communication shards]] are believed to use a variant of the same principle, though at greater range and with different operational characteristics. The Academy's imitation program has been running for sixty years and has produced approximately twenty functional pairs, each costing more to manufacture than most buildings cost to construct. The imitations work within a single building. The originals work across a city. The gap represents a manufacturing technique the Academy has not recovered and may never recover.
