# Gorbag Thrice-Scarred

Gorbag Thrice-Scarred runs the [[Bonehands]] the way a harbor pilot runs a channel — by knowing where every rock is and making sure other people hit them first. He has led the organization for fourteen years, inheriting it from his predecessor through a transfer of power that the Bonehands describe as a retirement and that the [[Solarguard]] describes as an unsolved homicide.

The three scars that earned his epithet came from three separate fights in three separate years — a pattern that Gorbag considers instructional rather than coincidental. The first was a dockside dispute when he was nineteen. The second was during the internal consolidation that followed his predecessor's "retirement." The third, the deepest, was from an assassination attempt four years ago that killed his lieutenant instead. He wears the scars openly because hiding them would suggest he considers them shameful, and an orc who hides scars is an orc who does not learn from them.

His leadership style is organizational rather than violent, which surprises people who expect an orc crime boss to operate through intimidation. He does intimidate — his size and reputation handle that — but his actual management involves careful territorial division, reliable tribute collection, fair (by Bonehands standards) dispute resolution, and the maintenance of arrangements with [[Solarguard]] officers in the harbor district that everyone knows about and no one can prove.

His position on the [[Velkhast Succession Crisis]] is with the third claimant — the distant cousin with harbor connections. This is not loyalty. It is investment. A Velkhast head who owes the Bonehands for their seat would provide the organization with political cover it has never had, elevating them from tolerated criminal enterprise to something closer to unofficial governance in the harbor districts. Gorbag considers this the natural evolution of an organization that already mediates disputes, collects taxes (he calls them "contributions"), and maintains order in districts the Solarguard has effectively abandoned.

He has never met [[Warden-Captain Orin Castellan]] in person. They communicate through intermediaries and shared understanding. She knows the harbor would be worse without him. He knows the city would not survive without her. This mutual recognition is not respect — it is structural awareness, which in Duskfrost amounts to the same thing.

[[Snugug]] reports to Gorbag directly. [[Kabugbu]]'s intelligence work — selling information to outside parties — is something Gorbag is aware of, tolerates within limits, and will terminate without hesitation if those limits are crossed.
