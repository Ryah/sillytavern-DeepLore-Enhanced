# Thalassa Velmorin

Thalassa Velmorin has pulled more pre-Calamity artifacts from the harbor floor than any other active member of the [[Salvagers Compact]], and she has opinions about what happens to them afterward. Specifically, she objects to the Academy buying everything the Compact recovers at prices the Academy sets, using expertise the Academy claims only it possesses, for research the Academy refuses to share. She has said this publicly. The Academy has not responded publicly. The prices have not changed.

Her bioluminescent markings are brighter than average — an inherited trait that her grandmother considered a gift and that Thalassa considers a professional inconvenience. Bright markings in dark water announce your presence to everything, including the things in the deeper harbor caves that the [[Duskfrost Academy|Academy]] insists are just currents and that every Zuvine salvager knows are not.

She serves as an unofficial spokesperson for the above-water [[Zuvine]] community, a role she took on because no one else was willing to attend [[Amberburgh]] district council meetings and because the council kept making decisions about harbor infrastructure without consulting the people who actually live in the harbor. Her council appearances are infrequent, blunt, and reported with some amusement by the [[Bolludine]] information networks.

Her working relationship with [[Dockmaster Bram Solvik]] is professional and occasionally contentious — he controls berth allocation, she controls which salvage teams work which sectors, and both of them understand that the other's cooperation is not optional. She trusts him more than she trusts the Academy, which is not the same as trusting him.

The [[Zuvine]] song-cycles include a verse about the harbor floor that Thalassa sings to junior salvagers before their first deep dive. It is, she says, not a warning. It is a map. The distinction matters.
