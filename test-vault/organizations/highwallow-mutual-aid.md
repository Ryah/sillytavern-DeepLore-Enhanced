# Highwallow Mutual Aid Society

The Highwallow Mutual Aid Society provides food, shelter mediation, and basic medical services to [[Highwallow]]'s most vulnerable residents, funded entirely by donations and small fees from those who can afford them. It operates out of the [[Highwallow Mutual Aid Hall]], a building that is technically condemned and chooses not to think too hard about that. The roof leaks in three places. The foundation settled two inches last flood season. The people inside keep the district from falling apart worse than the building is.

</div>

Maerith Colvane has run the Society for eleven years with the organizational skills of a quartermaster and the emotional reserves of someone who stopped being surprised by institutional neglect around year three. The paid staff includes two hedge-practitioners who provide basic medical care — wound treatment, fever management, the kind of low-level arcane healing that the [[Wardenship]] technically prohibits but practically ignores because arresting the only people treating infected wounds in Highwallow would be both monstrous and politically untenable. The volunteer network extends further: flood-season shelter coordination, food distribution from whatever the [[Harcyne Granary Authority]] deigns to deliver on time, dispute mediation between neighbors in a district where living space is measured in arm-spans, and the quiet work of checking on elderly residents who might not survive a cold night without someone noticing.

The Society's funding is its permanent crisis. Official donations from Bellsummit charity drives cover perhaps forty percent of operating costs. Small fees from residents who can afford them cover another twenty. The remaining gap is filled by money that arrives in unmarked envelopes, left at the Hall's back entrance on the first of each month, which everyone involved understands comes from the [[Bonehands]] and no one discusses because discussing it would require either accepting it officially (which would scandalize the Bellsummit donors) or refusing it (which would mean people going without medicine). Maerith deposits the money, records it as "anonymous community donations," and saves her moral energy for problems she can actually solve.

The Society's tension is with the [[Keepers of the Scar]], whose members overlap substantially with the Aid Society's volunteer base. The Keepers' spiritual mission — understanding and preserving the Resonance Scar — occasionally conflicts with the Aid Society's practical mission of keeping people alive near it. When Scar activity spikes and residents need evacuation, the Keepers want to observe and document. The Aid Society wants to move people to safety first and document never. Maerith and the Keepers' leadership maintain a working relationship built on the shared understanding that Highwallow cannot afford its two most useful organizations fighting each other.
