# Rimeborough Cold Market

The primary market of [[Rimeborough]], operating year-round in conditions that discourage casual visitors from other districts. Specializes in cold-climate goods, [[Delivese]] handicrafts and preserved foods, and the thermal equipment that makes Rimeborough's environment habitable for non-Delivese residents. The market's Delivese operators maintain consistent prices and do not negotiate, which other merchants find unsettling and customers find refreshing.

The market occupies an open square in Rimeborough's center, exposed to the persistent sea winds that keep the district's temperature well below comfortable for most species. Stalls are constructed from heavy timber and weighted canvas, designed to withstand gusts that would scatter a lighter market into the sea. The Delivese vendors work without gloves, their hands bare and unbothered by cold that turns non-Delivese customers' fingers numb within minutes. The air is sharp and clean, carrying the salt-wind smell of the northern cliffs mixed with the particular scents of the market itself: smoked fish, rendered fat, the waxy aroma of the thermal wraps sold at every third stall, and the dry, pleasant smell of Delivese grain bread baking in stone ovens that never fully cool.

The goods reflect both Delivese culture and practical necessity. Handicrafts include intricately carved bone tools, woven textiles in the muted blues and greys the Delivese favor, and the small carved-stone figures that serve some cultural purpose the Delivese explain to outsiders with varying degrees of patience. Preserved foods — smoked meats, fermented vegetables, dried fish compressed into dense blocks — sell to both Delivese residents and the growing number of non-Delivese who have acquired a taste for cuisine that prioritizes caloric density over presentation. The thermal equipment stalls do the most cross-district business: insulated cloaks, heated boot liners, and the small alchemical hand-warmers that the Delivese manufacture and everyone else in Duskfrost has come to consider essential during winter.

The market's fixed-price policy is enforced by collective refusal rather than posted rules. Delivese vendors simply do not respond to haggling — they state the price, wait, and if the customer objects, wait longer. The system is efficient, eliminates the adversarial theater of negotiation, and quietly drives [[Merchants League]] representatives to distraction. Visitors from [[Amberburgh]] or [[Bayside Shushail]] who attempt to bargain are met with a silence so patient and complete that most pay the asking price within thirty seconds rather than endure another moment of it.
