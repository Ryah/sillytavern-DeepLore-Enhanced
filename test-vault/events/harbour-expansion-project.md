# Harbour Expansion Project

The Harbour Expansion Project (Year 260-275) was the largest construction effort in Duskfrost's history, a fifteen-year undertaking that doubled the harbor's berth capacity from twenty-two deep-water slips to forty-four and established the breakwater system that still protects the inner harbor from northern storms. Funded jointly by House Velkhast and the [[Amberburgh Merchants League]], the project represented a rare alignment of political and commercial interests — Velkhast wanted to cement its dominance over trade revenue, and the Merchants League wanted berths that could accommodate the larger trade cogs then coming into use along the northern coast.

The engineering challenges were considerable. The harbor floor still contained pre-Calamity ruins — submerged foundations, collapsed resonance infrastructure, and in several locations, active arcane residue that interfered with conventional construction. [[Zuvine]] dive teams were contracted to clear the seabed, a job that took three years and produced a significant quantity of pre-Calamity artifacts that the [[Duskfrost Academy]] acquired at prices the Zuvine community leaders still consider insultingly low. The outer breakwater required sinking over 4,000 tons of quarried stone, much of it hauled from the cliff quarries above [[Scorchhelm]] on a purpose-built road that was dismantled after the project concluded.

The project's labor force peaked at approximately 1,200 workers, drawn from every district and species in the city. Working conditions were poor even by Duskfrost standards — the construction claimed sixty-seven lives over fifteen years, mostly from falls, drowning, and a structural collapse in Year 268 that killed twelve workers when a partially completed quay wall gave way. The [[Bonehands]] controlled labor recruitment for the harbor-side work crews, taking a percentage of wages in exchange for job placement — a practice that House Velkhast officially prohibited and unofficially tolerated because the Bonehands delivered workers reliably and on time.

The completed expansion transformed Duskfrost from a regional port into the dominant trade hub on the northern coast. Tax revenue from harbor fees tripled within a decade of completion, enriching House Velkhast disproportionately and creating the economic imbalance that other Triumvirate houses have resented — and exploited — ever since. The [[Harbour Expansion Project]] is cited in nearly every modern discussion of Duskfrost's economic structure, usually by whoever is currently losing the argument about trade taxation.
