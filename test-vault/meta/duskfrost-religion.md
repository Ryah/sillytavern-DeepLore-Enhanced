# Duskfrost Religion

Duskfrost is a secular city in the way that a city is secular after its gods failed to prevent the worst catastrophe in living memory — not by philosophical conviction, but by the quiet consensus that whatever was up there either could not or did not help, and either way, the rebuilding had to be done by hand.

**Pre-Calamity religion** is poorly documented. Fragments suggest multiple competing faith traditions tied to arcane practice — the resonance towers may have had a spiritual dimension as well as a functional one. Whatever those traditions were, they did not survive the Calamity intact.

**Post-Calamity pragmatism** replaced organized religion with institutional loyalty. The Triumvirate houses, the Academy, the guilds — these are the structures that rebuilt the city, and these are the structures that command allegiance. Oaths are sworn on contracts, not scriptures.

**The Champions' Oath** is the closest thing to a non-theistic creed in Duskfrost. The [[Champions of Patience]] swear to protect civilians without payment, without political allegiance, and without invoking any power beyond their own competence. The oath's explicit rejection of divine authority was radical when it was written. Now it simply reflects the city's default assumptions.

**The [[Keepers of the Scar]]** represent the nearest thing to spiritual practice. Their veneration of the Resonance Scar is not worship in any traditional sense — they do not pray to it, do not claim it has consciousness, and do not expect it to answer. They treat it as sacred in the way a surgeon treats an open wound as demanding respect: carefully, attentively, and with the understanding that carelessness has consequences.

**Folk superstitions** persist at the street level. Harbor workers knock twice on the dock before boarding. [[Highwallow]] residents leave salt at their doorsteps during Scar-active periods. [[Zuvine]] song-cycles include passages that function as blessings without naming any deity. The [[Bolludine]] avoid conducting business during the three days after the winter solstice, citing "bad air," though the actual reason has been lost to time.

The Academy's official position is that religious belief is a personal matter with no bearing on arcane theory. The Academy's unofficial position is that anyone who claims divine inspiration for their spellwork is either lying or dangerous, and possibly both.
