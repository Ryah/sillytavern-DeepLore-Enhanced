# Elspeth Dunvale

Elspeth Dunvale is what the [[Duskfrost Academy|Academy]] calls an "untrained instinctive practitioner" and what [[Highwallow]] calls a wild mage. She is twenty-three years old, has never drawn a sigil, and can shatter glass from across a room when she is angry. The Academy offered her a training placement at age twelve. She attended for four months before the binding exercises caused seizures. She has not been back.

Wild magic is not rare in Duskfrost — it manifests in perhaps one in a thousand children before formal training begins. What makes it rare is persistence. Most children either learn sigil discipline or their instinctive expression fades by adolescence. Elspeth's has not faded. It has intensified, which the Academy's medical faculty attributes to her proximity to the Resonance Scar and which Elspeth attributes to the Academy's training methods having broken something that was working fine before they touched it.

She lives in a converted warehouse in eastern Highwallow, where the Scar effects are strongest. The irony is not lost on her: the arcane turbulence that makes the district uninhabitable for trained practitioners actually stabilizes her wild magic, the way a noisy room can calm a person who finds silence unbearable. Her episodes — sudden, uncontrolled bursts of arcane energy — are less frequent and less destructive in the Scar zone than anywhere else in the city.

She makes a living doing odd jobs that benefit from someone who can heat metal without a forge, move heavy objects without touching them, and occasionally produce light strong enough to illuminate the Hollow Road access tunnels. The [[Keepers of the Scar]] consider her presence diagnostically useful — her episodes sometimes correlate with Scar activity spikes, making her an involuntary early warning system.

[[Hedge Mage Pruvven Alt]] sends her clients when their problems are beyond his skill set, which is another way of saying when their problems require raw power rather than careful sigil work. She does not advertise. She does not charge a standard rate. She accepts payment in food, materials, and the understanding that if something goes wrong, no one was here and nothing happened.

The [[Wardenship]] is aware of her. They have chosen not to act, which Elspeth interprets as pragmatism and the Wardenship interprets as ongoing assessment. Both interpretations are accurate.
