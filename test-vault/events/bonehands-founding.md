# Bonehands Founding

The [[Bonehands]] trace their organizational origin to Year 45, when a former Antecedent Culture harbor official named Drassik Vole reorganized the scattered criminal elements of the early harbor settlement into something that functioned less like a gang and more like a shadow government. Vole had survived the Calamity by being, by all accounts, deeply unpleasant and extremely competent — qualities that served him equally well in civilization and its absence. He recognized that the proto-Triumvirate families consolidating power in the upper districts had neither the interest nor the manpower to govern the harbor, and he filled the gap with a structured protection racket that offered genuine services alongside its threats.

The name comes from Vole's preferred method of communicating displeasure. Those who declined Bonehands protection would find a small package at their door containing finger bones — animal, usually, though the implication was clear enough. Those who continued to decline received human bones. Whether Vole ever actually killed anyone for refusing protection is debated; the bones may have come from Calamity-era remains, which were plentiful. The threat was effective regardless, because in the early Reconstruction period, the line between the living and the dead felt disturbingly thin.

Within a decade, the Bonehands controlled harbor taxation (unofficial but enforced), dispute resolution (faster and cheaper than the proto-Triumvirate courts), and the nascent smuggling trade. Vole's organizational structure — a hierarchy of "fingers" reporting to "hands" reporting to the "grip" — has survived largely intact for three centuries, though the current leadership would find Vole's methods crude. He died in Year 71 under circumstances his successors describe as "retirement" and everyone else describes as murder by his own lieutenant, a pattern the Bonehands have repeated with sufficient regularity to qualify as organizational tradition.

The Bonehands' early relationship with the proto-Triumvirate was pragmatic. The harbor needed order. The Triumvirate couldn't provide it. Vole could and did, in exchange for being left alone. This arrangement — the Triumvirate pretending the Bonehands don't exist while benefiting from their control of harbor crime — has persisted in various forms through the [[Watchers Treaty]] and into the present day. The [[Solarguard]], established nearly two centuries after the Bonehands, has never fully displaced them from [[Bayside Shushail]], and at this point, few people expect it ever will.
