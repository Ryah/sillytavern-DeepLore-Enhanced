# Zuvine Harbor Settlement

The [[Zuvine]] community's above-water presence in northern [[Bayside Shushail]] — a cluster of low buildings adapted for both surface and underwater access. Most Zuvine adults commute to and from the underwater cave networks daily via submerged access points in the harbor floor.

The settlement occupies a stretch of the harbor's northern edge where the water deepens sharply — a geographic necessity, since the submerged access points require depth to function. The buildings are low, single-story structures of dark stone, their rooflines barely above head height, with doors wide enough for two and ceilings that drip with condensation in warm weather. The interiors are cool, damp, and designed for comfort on a spectrum that non-Zuvine find alien: floors are slightly concave for water drainage, walls are unfinished stone that stays perpetually moist, and the furnishings prioritize surfaces that tolerate being wet. The air inside smells of brine, kelp, and the mineral tang of deep water brought up on Zuvine skin and clothing. Visitors describe it as walking into a tide pool. The Zuvine describe visitor discomfort as predictable.

The harbor-floor access points are visible at low tide as dark circles in the silted bottom, each roughly four feet across — stone-lined shafts that descend vertically into the underwater cave networks where the majority of Zuvine life takes place. Surface-dwellers have no way to use them. The Zuvine pass through these shafts as casually as other residents use doorways, transitioning between air and water with a fluidity that makes the surface settlement feel like what it is: a concession to the necessity of interacting with a city that was not built for aquatic people. Trade goods — Zuvine handicrafts, deep-water materials, items salvaged from the harbor floor — appear in the settlement's small market stall each morning, brought up from below through channels the Zuvine do not explain.

The settlement's relationship with the broader harbor district is functional but distant. Zuvine traders sell to [[Bayside Shushail]] merchants, Zuvine laborers work the docks with a strength and endurance that makes them valued employees, and Zuvine community leaders attend the harbor council meetings when the agenda concerns them. Beyond these points of contact, the community is opaque. The underwater cave networks have never been mapped by outsiders. The [[Duskfrost Academy]] has requested permission to survey them four times and been declined four times, with a politeness that made the refusal no less absolute. What happens below the waterline is Zuvine business, and the surface city has learned to accept that boundary — or at least to stop asking.
