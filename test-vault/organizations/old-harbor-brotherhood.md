# Old Harbor Brotherhood

The Old Harbor Brotherhood predates the [[Bonehands]] by sixty years. They once controlled the harbor district entirely — every dock, every warehouse, every sailor's purse that changed hands between ship and shore. Now they hold perhaps two square blocks of territory in the oldest part of [[Bayside Shushail]] and maintain it through a combination of stubbornness, local loyalty, and the fact that the Bonehands find eliminating them more trouble than it's worth.

Pellham "Grandfather" Voss has led the Brotherhood for twenty-two years, which in harbor criminal politics qualifies as geological time. He is seventy-one, walks with a cane that conceals a blade he hasn't needed in a decade, and conducts business from a corner table at [[The Drowned Anchor]] where the bartender keeps his chair reserved and his tea hot. The Brotherhood's remaining operations are modest: protection fees from the dozen businesses on their two blocks, a small-scale fencing operation for stolen goods that are too distinctive for the Bonehands' smuggling channels, and a decades-old agreement with the [[Zuvine]] harbor settlement to provide dockside security that the [[Solarguard]] won't. This last service is genuine — Brotherhood enforcers patrol the old harbor waterfront at night, and the Zuvine fishermen who work the early morning tides have not been robbed in the Brotherhood's territory in six years.

The Bonehands tolerate the Brotherhood for three reasons. First, Voss has connections and institutional memory that occasionally prove useful — he knows the harbor's infrastructure, its hidden spaces, and its tidal patterns better than anyone alive. Second, the Brotherhood's territory is small enough to be irrelevant and stable enough to not require attention. Third, [[Snugug]] of the Bonehands Council has a personal relationship with Voss that predates the current Bonehands leadership, and Gnaino do not abandon debts of respect lightly. The Brotherhood's younger members — there are perhaps eight under forty — understand that they are part of a dying institution. Some stay out of loyalty to Voss. Others stay because the Brotherhood's territory is the only place in the harbor where a small-time criminal can operate without Bonehands tribute.

The Brotherhood's tension is succession. Voss has no chosen successor, and the three most likely candidates represent incompatible futures: one wants to negotiate absorption into the Bonehands on favorable terms, one wants to expand into the [[Salvagers Compact]]'s underwater trade, and one wants to continue exactly as they are until the last member dies or retires. Voss listens to all three, commits to none, and drinks his tea. He has outlived every prediction of the Brotherhood's demise. He intends to outlive a few more.
