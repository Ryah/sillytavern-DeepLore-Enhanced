# Severance Dagger

A dagger with a blade that appears to cut arcane connections as readily as physical ones. A single cut from it will sever a [[Blood Binding Contracts|binding contract]], break an active ward, or disrupt an enchantment — permanently, with no restoration possible. The [[Wardenship]] classified it as an Extreme Hazard artifact and lost track of it in Year 318.

The blade is approximately fifteen centimeters long, double-edged, with a handle wrapped in leather that has not degraded despite unknown centuries of age. The metal is dark — not black iron but something that absorbs light slightly, giving the blade a visual quality of being less present than the space it occupies. The [[Duskfrost Academy]]'s metallurgy department examined it during its period of Wardenship custody and found the metal's composition to be, like so many pre-Calamity artifacts, unidentifiable. Unlike most pre-Calamity artifacts, the dagger actively resisted examination: diagnostic sigils directed at the blade were severed on contact, exactly as the blade's reputation promised. The Academy's examination lasted four hours and cost them three diagnostic instruments and one researcher's favorite enchanted magnifying glass.

The dagger's cutting effect operates on contact with any arcane structure — a ward, a binding, an enchantment, a sigil's active energy pattern. The cut is clean and irreversible: the severed arcane structure does not degrade or fade but simply ceases to exist, as though it had never been created. This makes the dagger catastrophically dangerous in the wrong hands. A single slash across a warded door does not just break the ward but removes any possibility of re-warding the same structure. A cut applied to a blood-binding contract negates the binding entirely — both parties are freed, and the sigil that enforced the contract becomes inert. The applications for contract fraud, security breach, and targeted sabotage of arcane infrastructure are obvious and extensive.

The Wardenship lost custody of the dagger during a facility transfer in Year 318, when the transport convoy arrived at its destination with the dagger's containment case empty and no record of when or how the removal occurred. The containment case's own wards — designed to alert if the case was opened — had been severed. The investigation lasted eight months, implicated no one, and generated a four-hundred-page report whose conclusions amount to "the dagger is gone and we do not know who took it." The Wardenship's Artifact Response Unit maintains the case as an active priority. The dagger surfaces in rumor every few years — used in a contract dispute in [[Amberburgh]], spotted in the [[Nightmarket Customs|night market]], reportedly offered to a debtor trapped in an exploitative blood-binding. Whether these sightings reflect a single holder using the dagger selectively or multiple holders passing it along is unknown. The Wardenship would very much like to find out.

The most recent credible lead places the dagger in [[Dhymma Dherignol]]'s private collection. A former Bonehands courier, interviewed by the [[Wardenship]] under immunity, claimed to have transported a wrapped blade matching the Severance Dagger's description to Dhymma's residence in [[Highwallow]] roughly two years ago. Dhymma has denied this, and the Wardenship lacks sufficient evidence for a search warrant — Dhymma's connections to the [[Bonehands]] Council make a forced entry politically inadvisable. The courier has since left Duskfrost.
