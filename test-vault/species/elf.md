# Elf

Elves in Duskfrost are a long-lived minority species with natural affinity for preservation and binding magic. They occupy positions of institutional power disproportionate to their numbers — roughly 5% of the population, but present in senior roles across the Academy, the [[Oracles of Might]], and [[Bellsummit]] governance.

</div>

The elven community's long lifespan creates a political dynamic unique to their species: institutional memory. An elf in a senior position has personally witnessed two or three generations of human politics, which gives them a strategic patience that younger species find either reassuring or infuriating. [[Archmage Tessavel Orindal]] is ninety years old — middle-aged for an elf — and has already outlasted four Triumvirate leadership changes.

Their preservation and binding affinity makes elves the natural practitioners of ward magic, containment sigils, and long-duration enchantments. The Academy's restricted section relies heavily on elven preservation techniques. The wards on the [[Archive Building]] have been maintained by the same family of elven practitioners for over a century.

Elven culture in Duskfrost is formal, hierarchical, and deeply concerned with lineage. Marriages are political events. Family alliances span centuries. Grudges do the same. The elven community in Bellsummit maintains social structures that predate the Calamity — or at least claims to, since verifying pre-Calamity social records is effectively impossible.

Half-elves occupy an uneasy space between elven formality and human pragmatism. The elven community officially recognizes them as lineage members. Unofficially, their shorter lifespans and mixed affinities place them at the margins of elven social networks. [[Warden-Captain Orin Castellan]] and [[Tybellan Hercyne]] both navigate this boundary in different ways — Castellan by ignoring it, Hercyne by making himself indispensable enough that people stop asking.

Relations with other species are cordial and carefully maintained. Elves work alongside humans and dwarves without difficulty. They are polite to [[Cirilae]] and [[Zuvine]]. They tend to find [[Bolludine]] conversation speeds exhausting, a weakness the Bolludine exploit in trade negotiations.
