# Brother Emvel Crayne

Chaplain of the [[Champions of Patience]], responsible for the organization's civilian outreach work in [[Bellsummit]] and [[Amberburgh]]. Where Paladin Serevath waits for the right moment to act, Emvel spends his time ensuring that when the Champions need community support, they have cultivated the relationships that make it available. He is warm, genuinely interested in people, and has a politician's memory for names and family connections. He considers this a spiritual gift.

Emvel is the Champions' public face in a way that [[Paladin Serevath Dul]] cannot be — he goes where armed paladins would cause alarm, sits in kitchens, attends funerals, remembers which grandmother is ill and which child just started apprenticing. This is not performance. He genuinely cares about every person he speaks with, which is both his greatest strength and the source of a grief he carries quietly: he knows too many people, and in a city with Duskfrost's mortality rate, he buries too many of them.

What the Champions do not know is that Emvel's faith has been faltering for three years. Not in the divine — in the doctrine of patience itself. He has watched situations deteriorate while the order waited for the right moment, and he has begun to wonder whether patience is sometimes indistinguishable from cowardice. He has not spoken this doubt aloud. He prays about it nightly and receives no answer he can interpret, which he considers an answer in itself.

He shares tea regularly with [[Cecilie Sorensen]] at the Bayside clinic, where they trade community intelligence disguised as gossip. She is the only person outside the order he would call a friend without qualification.
