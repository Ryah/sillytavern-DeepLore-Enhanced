# Reconstruction Era Oral Histories

The most reliable accounts of the early Reconstruction period come not from written records (most were destroyed or never created) but from oral histories preserved by [[Zuvine]] elder song-cycles and several human community organizations in [[Highwallow]]. These accounts consistently describe a period of extreme scarcity, surprising cooperation across species lines, and rapid deterioration of cooperation as scarcity eased and political competition resumed.

The Highwallow community organizations — collectively called the Memory Houses — maintain their histories through a structured practice of communal recitation. Once per season, the senior members of each Memory House gather to recite the canonical accounts of the Reconstruction in sequence, correcting each other's lapses and debating disputed passages. The practice was established in approximately Year 40, when the first generation of Reconstruction survivors realized they were aging out and their children did not remember the early years clearly. The canonical accounts cover roughly Year 0 through Year 80, with the most detailed coverage of the first three decades — the period of greatest hardship and, not coincidentally, the period the survivors most wanted remembered.

The accounts describe a Duskfrost unrecognizable from its current form. No districts, no Triumvirate, no Academy — just clusters of survivors occupying whatever structures remained standing. Water came from two functioning wells and a rain-collection system assembled from scavenged materials. Food came from subsistence farming on plots cleared of arcane contamination through trial and error (the error rate, in the early years, was approximately one death per week). The [[Gnaino]] provided critical assistance during this period, trading food and clean water from their tunnel-network reserves in exchange for surface materials they could not produce underground. This trade relationship is documented in both the Memory House accounts and the [[Zuvine Song Cycles]], making it one of the most reliably attested facts of the early Reconstruction. The Gnaino do not discuss it, which the Memory Houses interpret as humility and the Zuvine interpret as the Gnaino considering the debt long since paid.

The deterioration of cooperation is the accounts' most consistent and most bitter theme. As infrastructure stabilized and scarcity eased — roughly Year 30 to Year 50 — the cooperative networks that had sustained survival began fracturing along lines of species, district, and access to resources. The formation of the proto-Triumvirate in Year 45 is described in the Memory House accounts not as a founding but as a seizure: three families with military capability and stored resources claiming authority over communities that had governed themselves cooperatively for decades. The [[Binding Compact]], established a century later, is viewed by the Memory Houses as an attempt to formalize the power the three families had already taken. This perspective is not taught in the Academy's history curriculum. The Memory Houses consider this omission deliberate.
