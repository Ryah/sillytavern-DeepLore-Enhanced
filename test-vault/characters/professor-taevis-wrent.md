# Professor Taevis Wrent

Professor of theoretical magic at the [[Bellsummit]] Academy of Arcane Arts, specializing in the interaction between Duskfrost's ambient magical field and conventional arcane practice. He has published fourteen monographs and is working on a fifteenth that will, he hopes, resolve an argument he has been having in the literature with a Stormhaven researcher for six years. He is popular with advanced students and somewhat frightening to first-years.

Wrent teaches the way other people argue — with intensity, conviction, and a willingness to follow a tangent into territory that terrifies his students. His lectures on ambient field interactions are legendary at the Academy, not because they are clear (they are not) but because the occasional moments of clarity reveal something genuinely profound, and students learn to chase those moments through the surrounding chaos. He has set his own desk on fire three times during demonstrations. He considers this an acceptable pedagogical cost.

What Wrent will not admit publicly is that his fifteenth monograph is stalled because his conclusions point toward something he cannot explain within the Academy's theoretical framework. The ambient magical field in Duskfrost is not behaving as established theory predicts — it is intensifying in patterns that suggest an external influence rather than natural variation, and his models cannot identify the source. He has reviewed [[Kieran Heixisys]]'s notes (without attribution, which he tells himself he will correct eventually) and found that the junior lecturer's data confirms his own anomalous readings, which means either both of them are wrong or something significant is changing.

He has a grudging respect for [[Gorduin Wynlar]], who dismissed his first three monographs as "theoretically irresponsible" and praised the fourth as "marginally competent," which is the closest thing to a compliment Gorduin has issued in living memory. Wrent keeps the letter.
