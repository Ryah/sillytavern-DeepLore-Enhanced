# Sword of Enigmas

The Sword of Enigmas is a longsword of apparent pre-Calamity manufacture with an unusual property: it compels honest answers. Not from its target — from its wielder. A person holding the Sword cannot lie in response to a direct question. They can refuse to answer. They can change the subject. They cannot state a known falsehood.

<div class="meta-block">
[Type: Weapon/Artifact | Era: Unknown | Current Holder: Classified | Danger Level: Moderate | Side Effect: Compulsory honesty]
</div>

The Sword has changed hands fourteen times in recorded Duskfrost history. Most holders give it up within a year. The two who kept it longest — a magistrate (12 years) and a spy (7 years) — have both written extensively about the experience. The magistrate found it clarifying. The spy found it professionally catastrophic.

The Sword is currently in the custody of a party whose identity the [[Oracles of Might]] declined to record officially. It surfaces in circulation periodically, usually when its holder becomes inconveniently honest in a negotiation.

The compulsion applies only to statements, not to actions. A holder cannot lie about what they are about to do, but they can simply not announce it.
