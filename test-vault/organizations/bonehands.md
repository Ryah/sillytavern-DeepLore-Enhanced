# Bonehands

The Bonehands control organized crime in the harbor and marsh districts of Duskfrost. They collect tribute, mediate disputes, and run the largest smuggling operation in the city. Their relationship with the [[Solarguard]] is an open secret — officially hostile, practically a cold war with mutually understood boundaries. The organization takes its name from an old dockworkers' tradition: bare-knuckle oath-swearing over a pile of fish bones, which new members still perform in the back room of the [[Bonehands Counting House]] on the primary dock street.

The Bonehands operate under a Council of Five, though calling it democratic would be generous. [[Snugug]], a [[Gnaino]] fixer who has survived three leadership purges, handles logistics and tunnel access — the organization's real competitive advantage. [[Harvel Quince]] runs debt collection across [[Bayside Shushail]] with meticulous records and a professional distaste for unnecessary violence. [[Zoughat]], a [[Vorruk]] enforcer, manages the harbor protection racket and is widely considered the most dangerous person in the organization, though he would describe himself as "motivational." [[Kabugbu]], an [[Orvathi]] smuggler, controls the routes through the marsh channels that connect the harbor to inland drop points. And [[Gorbag Thrice-Scarred]], a [[Bolludine]] who earned his name honestly, oversees the Highwallow operations — territory extraction, gambling dens, and the quiet business of making problems disappear into the marsh.

The rules of operation are simple and enforced without sentimentality. No killing without Council approval. No stealing from anyone under Bonehands protection — and protection is not optional for businesses in their territory. No cooperation with the [[Solarguard]] beyond the understood arrangement. No involvement with the [[Scourge of Vitality]] or any blood-magic operation. That last rule exists because Snugug insisted on it after the cult nearly brought a Wardenship raid down on the entire harbor district eight years ago, and nobody argued because Snugug is usually right about things that keep people alive.

Current operations center on three revenue streams: the harbor protection racket (stable, predictable), smuggling through the marsh channels (profitable, seasonal), and loan services to businesses that the [[Amberburgh Merchants League]] won't touch (growing, increasingly political). The Bonehands also take a cut from the [[Salvagers Compact]]'s underwater hauls — not officially, but every salvager knows that certain artifacts found in the harbor never make it to the Academy's purchasing office. The tension within the organization runs along the obvious fault line: Kabugbu and Gorbag want to expand into [[Amberburgh]]'s night market territory, while Snugug and Quince argue that visibility is the enemy of longevity. Zoughat, characteristically, says he will enforce whatever the Council decides and means it.

Beyond the Council, the Bonehands maintain a network of specialists and affiliates. [[Dhymma Dherignol]], a fence and artifact dealer operating out of [[Highwallow]], handles the organization's more delicate acquisitions — items too identifiable or too dangerous for the standard smuggling channels. Her discretion and her personal collection of confiscated curiosities make her valuable enough that the Council tolerates her occasional independence.
