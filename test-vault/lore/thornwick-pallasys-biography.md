# Thornwick Pallasys Biography

Thornwick Pallasys (Year 31-118) spent the majority of his life on a single project: translating the [[Antecedent Script]]. He succeeded well enough that his translations remain the authoritative reference four centuries later. He failed enough that subsequent scholars have spent those four centuries correcting his errors. His personal journals, held in the Archive, suggest he knew his translations were flawed and considered publishing anyway preferable to letting the knowledge die with him. He was correct.

Pallasys was born in the early Reconstruction era to a family of subsistence farmers in what would later become the [[Highwallow]] district. His arcane affinity was identified at age nine by a traveling practitioner — one of the informal teachers who served communities too small and too poor to support an institution. The [[Duskfrost Academy]] did not yet exist; Pallasys's early education was a patchwork of apprenticeship, self-study, and access to whatever pre-Calamity texts the Reconstruction-era communities had salvaged. He encountered his first Antecedent Script inscription at age sixteen, carved into the wall of a [[Hollow Roads]] tunnel entrance near his family's settlement. He spent the next seventy years trying to read it.

His method was brute-force pattern analysis — comparing every known inscription against every other, cataloguing character frequencies, positional patterns, and contextual associations across thousands of fragments. He worked alone for the first twenty years, then attracted a small circle of students who became the nucleus of the Academy's linguistics department. His great insight, achieved around Year 75, was recognizing that the script's administrative documents used a restricted character set with standardized combinations — a simplified register, like a bureaucratic shorthand. By cracking this register first, he established a foothold from which to attack the full script. His great error was assuming the simplified register's grammar applied to the full script, which it does not. Approximately half of his translation errors stem from this assumption.

His personal journals reveal a man who understood the weight of what he was doing. An entry from Year 96 reads: "I am guessing. I have always been guessing. But an educated guess, recorded and published, can be corrected by those who come after me. An unrecorded guess dies with the guesser." This philosophy — publish the flawed work so others can improve it — defined the Academy's approach to Antecedent studies for generations. The [[Forbidden Sigil Catalog]] is his most dangerous legacy: eighty translated sigils, twelve of which still function, several of which he specifically marked in his journals as "patterns I believe were forbidden for reasons I cannot yet determine." His caution was prescient. His translations were incomplete. The [[Oracles of Might]] vault holds his original notes alongside [[Gorduin Wynlar]]'s corrections, and the corrections are, in places, more alarming than the originals.
