# Scrivener Tulla Nance

Public scrivener in [[Bellsummit]]'s civic quarter — she writes letters, petitions, contracts, and the occasional document that occupies a creative relationship with official certification. She does not forge; she assists clients in producing documents that are accurate to their needs and ambiguous on details that would complicate official review. This distinction is legally defensible and has been tested twice. She won both times.

Tulla's shop is barely wider than a doorway — a writing desk, a stool for clients, and shelves of ink, parchment, and reference materials organized by the kind of document rather than the kind of client. She writes everything from love letters to land petitions, and she charges the same rate for both, because she considers all communication equally important and equally difficult to do well. Her handwriting is so precise that it has been mistaken for print, which is a professional asset in a city where official documents are evaluated partly on their visual authority.

The legal challenges she survived were brought by the [[Duskfrost Triumvirate]]'s Office of Records, which took issue with her interpretation of "public notarization" — specifically, whether a scrivener can notarize a document she also drafted. Tulla argued that the act of writing is separate from the act of witnessing, and prevailed, establishing a precedent she knows the Office intends to revisit. She is already drafting her defense for the third challenge, which she considers a reasonable use of her evenings.

She has an unofficial referral arrangement with [[Registrar Morna Pelwick]] — when Academy applicants need documentation that the Registrar cannot formally help them produce, Pelwick sends them to Tulla with a specific turn of phrase that means "help this person but do not create anything I would have to reject." Tulla finds this arrangement elegant.
