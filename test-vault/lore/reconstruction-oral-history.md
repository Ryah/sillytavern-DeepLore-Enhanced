# Reconstruction Era Oral Histories

The most reliable accounts of the early Reconstruction period come not from written records (most were destroyed or never created) but from oral histories preserved by [[Zuvine]] elder song-cycles and several human community organizations in [[Highwallow]]. These accounts consistently describe a period of extreme scarcity, surprising cooperation across species lines, and rapid deterioration of cooperation as scarcity eased and political competition resumed.
