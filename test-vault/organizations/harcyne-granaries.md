# Harcyne Granary Authority

The Harcyne Granary Authority controls food storage and distribution for Duskfrost and the surrounding agricultural regions. Their monopoly on grain storage gives [[Duskfrost Triumvirate|House Harcyne]] a political lever no other house can match — they can cause food shortages without technically doing anything illegal. The granary system was built as civic infrastructure during the Reconstruction. That it ended up as a private monopoly controlled by a single noble house is the kind of outcome that surprises no one who has lived in Duskfrost longer than a season.

<div class="meta-block">
[Type: Commercial Monopoly | Owner: [[Duskfrost Triumvirate|House Harcyne]] | Director: Overseer Brenn Harcyne | Facilities: 4 granary complexes, 12 distribution depots | Founded: Year 67 (as civic authority), Year 189 (privatized) | Employees: ~450]
</div>

The Authority operates four massive granary complexes — two on the inland plateau, one in [[Amberburgh]]'s warehouse district, and one adjacent to [[Bayside Shushail]]'s northern docks for grain imports. Every bushel of grain, every sack of flour, every barrel of preserved meal that enters or leaves Duskfrost passes through Harcyne storage, where it is weighed, taxed, stored, and released on a schedule that the Authority describes as "demand-responsive" and everyone else calls "whatever House Harcyne needs this week." Overseer Brenn Harcyne, the current director and a Harcyne cousin close enough to the main line to matter, manages the operation with the efficiency of someone who genuinely believes the system works well and the indifference of someone who has never been hungry.

The political implications are straightforward and ugly. When House Harcyne wants something from the Triumvirate — a favorable ruling, a commercial concession, a convenient delay in proceedings — grain shipments to contested districts slow. Not stop. Stopping would be an act of war. Slowing is just logistics. The [[Highwallow Mutual Aid Society]] tracks granary delivery schedules obsessively because a three-day delay in flour distribution to [[Highwallow]] translates directly into people going hungry. The [[Redpond Fishers Cooperative]] exists in part because freshwater fish is the one protein source that doesn't pass through Harcyne hands, which makes eighteen lake fishermen more strategically important than their scale suggests.

The Authority's internal tension is between the Harcyne family members who run the operation as a political tool and the career employees who actually keep the grain moving. The warehouse managers, logistics clerks, and distribution drivers are working people who take professional pride in feeding a city of 80,000 and growing resentment at being instruments of a noble house's leverage games. Overseer Brenn has noticed the resentment. He has addressed it with a modest pay increase and the quiet transfer of three employees who talked too loudly about it to the inland plateau facility, where they can complain to the wheat.
