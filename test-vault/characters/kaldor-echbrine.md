# Kaldor Echbrine

Kaldor Echbrine knows more about the [[The Hollow Roads|Hollow Roads]] than the [[Duskfrost Academy|Academy]]'s mapping expedition has learned in thirty years of trying, and he has no intention of sharing. This is not hostility — it is professional judgment. The Roads are not a map problem. They are a listening problem. And surface-dwellers do not listen well enough.

The [[Gnaino]] navigate by echolocation — a series of clicks and hums that map the tunnel walls, identify air currents, detect structural instability, and locate other living things in the dark. Kaldor's echolocation range is wider than most, a fact he demonstrates by describing objects behind corners before he reaches them and by stopping mid-step when a tunnel floor is about to give way.

He guides paying clients through the upper Hollow Roads — the mapped sections that connect [[Highwallow]] to [[Scorchhelm]]'s sub-basements and the harbor undercroft. He charges five silver marks per trip, does not negotiate, and refuses to guide anyone into the deeper tunnels. When asked why, he produces a clicking sound that Gnaino linguists have translated as either "because you would die" or "because it would be your fault," depending on the inflection.

His relationship with surface Duskfrost is transactional. He buys supplies at the [[Rimeborough Market]], pays his taxes to the [[Amberburgh]] authorities, and otherwise maintains the Gnaino preference for being left alone. The [[Keepers of the Scar]] have attempted to consult him about subterranean Scar effects three times. He answered their questions with other questions. They have not asked a fourth time.

He sleeps underground. He eats underground. He surfaces because the economy requires it and returns below because everything else does. Most surface-dwellers who meet him remember the eyes — pale, clouded, and pointed at nothing in particular, because he is not looking at them. He is listening.
