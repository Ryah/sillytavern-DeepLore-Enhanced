# Kethvali

Kethvali are nomadic traders who pass through Duskfrost twice yearly — once in early spring, once in late autumn — with caravans from regions most Duskfrost residents could not locate on a map. They do not settle, have never settled, and regard the concept of permanent residence with a polite bewilderment that borders on pity. At peak caravan season, perhaps 150 Kethvali occupy the designated trade camps outside [[Scorchhelm]]. Within a week, they are gone.

Kethvali caravans are family operations — extended kinship networks of forty to eighty individuals traveling with pack animals, wagons, and a portable market infrastructure they can assemble in under two hours. Their goods command premium prices because they are genuinely irreplaceable: spices from arid southern plateaus, textiles woven from fibers that do not grow within five hundred miles of Duskfrost, preserved foods with flavors the local palate has no framework for, and occasional arcane curiosities whose provenance the Kethvali decline to discuss.

Their trade route enters Duskfrost through [[Scorchhelm]], where the [[Velkhast Trading Co]] handles bulk purchases before the caravans move on to the [[Amberburgh Exchange]] for retail sales. The Kethvali tolerate the Velkhast markup as a cost of doing business and maintain their own direct relationships with preferred customers — the Academy, several [[Bellsummit]] households, and a handful of [[Orvathi]] night-market dealers who have earned trust over decades of reliable payment.

Kethvali culture is organized around the route itself. Children are educated during travel, learning geography, languages, and trade mathematics from the landscape they pass through. Their oral histories are structured as journey narratives — not "in the year of the flood" but "three days past the salt flats, when the wind changed." Time is measured in distances. A Kethvali saying: *A promise kept in one city is forgotten by the next horizon* — meaning that obligations are place-bound, and the road wipes the slate clean.

The [[Triumvirate]] has attempted several times to tax Kethvali trade more aggressively or require permanent commercial licenses. Each attempt has resulted in the Kethvali simply skipping Duskfrost for a season, routing their caravans to competing cities until the policy is reversed. They have done this three times in living memory, and the economic pain was sufficient each time that the Triumvirate now negotiates seasonal trade terms rather than imposing them. The Kethvali are polite about this leverage. They do not need to be otherwise.
