# Resonance Grid Discovery

Approximately two years ago, a junior [[Duskfrost Academy]] researcher named Talesse Morvane — a distant descendant of the pre-Calamity scholar whose work was lost in the [[Academy Fire of Year 211]] — was conducting routine geological surveys beneath [[Rappiop Grove]] when her resonance detection equipment registered an active signal. Not residual. Not decaying. Active. A functioning fragment of the pre-Calamity resonance grid, buried beneath twelve meters of soil and post-Calamity sediment, still drawing power from the ley line convergence and still maintaining whatever operational state it had been in for over three centuries.

The discovery was reported to the Academy's Senior Council within hours. The Council's response was immediate and total: Morvane was placed under academic gag order, the survey site was sealed, and the [[Wardenship]] deployed a containment team to establish a permanent monitoring station disguised as a groundskeeping facility. The Academy has suppressed public knowledge of the discovery because the implications are, by any reasonable assessment, terrifying. If one grid fragment survived and remained functional, others may have as well. The technology that destroyed the Antecedent Culture is not entirely dead — it has been sleeping.

All three Triumvirate houses are aware of the discovery. None have publicly acknowledged it. Each has drawn different conclusions. House Velkhast sees economic potential — a functioning resonance node could power harbor infrastructure, reducing dependence on expensive arcane practitioners. House Harcyne sees military potential — a resonance weapon, if reconstructable, would decisively end any future conflict in their favor. House Morveth sees validation — their family's long monopoly on arcane knowledge was built on the belief that the old technology could be recovered, and they want credit for being right. These three incompatible visions are the engine driving the [[War of Glimmering Hope]].

Morvane herself has been sequestered in an Academy research facility, where she continues to study the fragment under heavy security. Her preliminary reports — classified, but widely leaked in summary form — suggest the fragment is not simply surviving but actively communicating with something beneath the harbor. The [[Zuvine]] elders have been asked if they know what that might be. Their answer, delivered through formal diplomatic channels, was a single word in the old Zuvine tongue that translates roughly as "Don't."
