# Siege of Am — Background Lore

The Siege of Am was not primarily a military action — it was a negotiation by other means. The proto-[[Duskfrost Triumvirate|Triumvirate]] needed Am's harbor access. Am's leaders knew this. The siege was how the Triumvirate demonstrated they were serious. The failure was deliberate — a face-saving retreat that let Am accept the subsequent trade treaty without appearing conquered.

The siege lasted forty-three days, from early autumn through the first frost of Year 62. The Triumvirate deployed approximately four hundred soldiers — most of their available military force at the time — to surround Am's landward approaches while leaving the harbor conspicuously unblockaded. This was the tell: a force genuinely intent on conquest does not leave the supply route open. Am's leaders, who were experienced enough to read the signal, settled in for a siege that both sides understood would end in negotiation rather than assault. The forty-three days were spent in increasingly theatrical displays of military capability: the Triumvirate parading troops along the siege line, Am's garrison conducting drills on the walls in full view, and both sides sending diplomatic envoys who returned with progressively more reasonable counteroffers.

The treaty that emerged — the Am Harbor Accord — gave the Triumvirate guaranteed harbor access, preferential trade rates, and the right to station a customs officer at Am's main dock. In exchange, Am retained full self-governance, received military protection guarantees, and extracted a clause preventing the Triumvirate from building a competing harbor within fifty leagues. This clause has shaped Duskfrost's maritime economics ever since: it is why [[Bayside Shushail]] developed as a fishing port rather than a commercial harbor, and why the [[Amberburgh Merchants League]] maintains a stranglehold on maritime import routes. The Accord has been renegotiated six times, each renegotiation producing progressively more favorable terms for the Triumvirate and progressively less meaningful self-governance for Am, which is now effectively an administrative district of Duskfrost in all but name.

The [[Reconstruction Oral Histories|Memory Houses]] in [[Highwallow]] preserve a version of the Siege that differs from the official account. In their telling, the siege was not a diplomatic theater but a genuine military action that failed when Am's defenders employed pre-Calamity defensive sigils that the Triumvirate's forces could not breach. The deliberate-failure narrative, in this version, was invented afterward to preserve the Triumvirate's reputation. The Academy's military history department considers the Memory House version "unsubstantiated but not implausible," which is the most diplomatically dangerous assessment an academic institution can produce.
