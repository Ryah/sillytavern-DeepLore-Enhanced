# Thornwick Society

The Thornwick Society is a scholarly organization dedicated to the study of the pre-Calamity Antecedent Culture, named after Archmage [[Thornwick Pallasys Biography|Thornwick Pallasys]] who founded [[The Archive Building|the Archive]]. They have no formal affiliation with the [[Duskfrost Academy]] but substantial overlap in membership — roughly half their fellows hold Academy positions, and the other half left Academy positions under circumstances they describe as "principled departure" and the Academy describes as "not renewed."

The Society meets monthly in a reading room at the Archive that Thornwick Pallasys designated for scholarly use in his founding charter — a provision the Academy cannot revoke without admitting that the Archive's charter supersedes Academy administrative authority, which it does, and which the Academy would rather not acknowledge publicly. The meetings combine research presentations, artifact analysis, and arguments about translation methodology that have been running longer than some of the members have been alive. Professor Emerita Dunhallow Vess, an Elf whose personal memory extends to within living distance of the Reconstruction era, chairs the Society with the patience of someone who has watched academic fads come and go for centuries and finds them all equally temporary.

The Society's work is the closest thing Duskfrost has to systematic Antecedent scholarship. While the Academy treats pre-Calamity artifacts primarily as objects of arcane study — what do they do, can they be replicated, what is their commercial potential — the Thornwick Society asks historical questions: who built them, why, what kind of civilization produces resonance towers, and what can the answers tell Duskfrost about avoiding the same mistakes. This distinction matters more than it sounds. The Academy's approach has produced practical results — working reconstructions, new binding techniques, commercial applications. The Society's approach has produced uncomfortable questions about whether Duskfrost's current trajectory of expanding arcane commerce and resonance exploitation looks uncomfortably similar to the patterns they've documented in pre-Calamity records.

The Society's tension is with its own relevance. The [[Salvagers Compact]] brings up artifacts faster than the Society can study them. The Academy's commercial research programs attract the funding and the talent. The Society's annual *Proceedings* are respected and under-read. Younger fellows push for public engagement — lectures, museum exhibitions, collaboration with the [[Keepers of the Scar]] on Highwallow's living connection to the Antecedent era. Senior fellows, Vess included, worry that popularization means oversimplification, and oversimplification of the Antecedent Culture's mistakes is how you get people repeating them. In the meantime, the Society continues its quiet work in the Archive reading room, cataloguing the fragments of a civilization that destroyed itself and hoping someone is paying attention.
