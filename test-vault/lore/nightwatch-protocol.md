# Nightwatch Protocol

The Nightwatch Protocol governs [[Scorchhelm]]'s after-dark patrol rotation — four overlapping shifts, each responsible for specific districts and gate positions. The protocol was established after the Third Consolidation when a single unguarded night allowed a rival faction to plant incendiaries in the lower armory. The garrison has not had a fully unguarded night since.

The four shifts are designated by cardinal direction: North Watch, South Watch, East Watch, and the Wall Watch. The first three cover Scorchhelm's interior districts in rotating patterns that change weekly according to a schedule known only to the Watch Commander and the shift leads. The Wall Watch covers the outer fortifications and gate positions continuously — two soldiers at each gate, four on the wall walks, and a roving reserve of six who respond to signals from any position. Shift overlap is thirty minutes, during which both the departing and arriving watch patrol simultaneously. This redundancy was added after a Year 276 incident in which an infiltrator exploited the five-minute gap between shifts to enter through the eastern postern gate. The infiltrator was caught inside the armory. What they intended to do there remains classified.

Each patrol carries a signal horn, a light source (non-arcane — Scorchhelm's protocol prohibits sigil-based illumination on night patrol, as the light interferes with dark-adapted vision and announces the patrol's position), and a sealed dispatch tube for written reports. Patrols report to the Watch Commander's station at the central keep every two hours. A patrol that fails to report triggers an automatic response: the reserve deploys to the patrol's last known position, and the adjacent shift extends coverage. In the protocol's three-century history, this response has been triggered forty-seven times. Thirty-nine were communication failures (lost dispatch tubes, broken horns, a patrol that fell asleep). Six were genuine security incidents. Two have never been explained — the patrols returned safely but had no memory of the missing reporting period.

The [[Scorchhelm Fortifications|fortification]] design integrates with the protocol: sight lines from the wall walks cover every approach within arrow range, and the gate positions include concealed observation points that allow the watch to monitor visitors before they become aware of surveillance. The protocol's effectiveness is a point of institutional pride for the garrison and a persistent irritant for the [[Wardenship]], whose own urban patrol operations in [[Highwallow]] and [[Amberburgh]] are frequently compared unfavorably to Scorchhelm's military discipline. The comparison is unfair — Scorchhelm patrols a fortress, not a sprawling city — but the Wardenship officers find this argument less comforting than it should be.
