# Breaking of the Morveth Arcane Monopoly

Year 147. For nearly a century and a half after the Calamity, House Morveth had maintained exclusive control over the largest surviving collection of pre-Calamity arcane texts, artifacts, and practitioner training methods. This was not accidental — the Morveth family had been arcane practitioners before the Calamity, and they had spent the Reconstruction era systematically acquiring every scrap of pre-Calamity knowledge they could find, buy, or confiscate. By Year 140, they controlled an estimated 80% of all known arcane manuscripts in the region, trained the only certified practitioners, and charged accordingly for both access and services.

The other two Triumvirate houses tolerated this monopoly for decades because they had more pressing concerns — territorial consolidation, the [[Attack of Steel]], the ongoing struggle to feed a growing population. But by Year 140, the city was stable enough that the imbalance had become intolerable. House Morveth's ability to deny arcane services to political rivals — or to price those services beyond reach — gave them leverage that no amount of military or economic power could counter. The breaking point came when House Morveth refused to provide ward-maintenance for the Velkhast granary complex without a renegotiation of trade tariffs. The grain nearly spoiled. The other houses decided the monopoly would end.

The negotiation took seven years, involved four attempted compromises and one assassination attempt (on a Morveth negotiator, unsuccessful, perpetrator never identified), and concluded with the founding charter of the [[Duskfrost Academy]]. The charter transferred control of the Morveth private archive to a jointly governed institution with representatives from all three houses and an independent academic council. House Morveth received significant agricultural concessions — guaranteed grain supplies at below-market rates for fifty years — along with a permanent seat on the Academy's governing board and first right of refusal for the position of Archmage. These concessions were generous enough that House Morveth could claim they had been compensated rather than defeated, a fiction everyone maintained because the alternative was admitting that the most powerful family in the city had just been collectively robbed.

The resulting institution became the [[Duskfrost Academy]], which has outlasted the political circumstances of its creation by nearly two centuries. House Morveth's influence within the Academy has waned as the institution developed its own institutional culture and power base, a development the Morveth family anticipated but could not prevent. The [[Wardenship]], the Academy's enforcement arm, was established within a decade of the founding — originally staffed primarily by former Morveth practitioners, now thoroughly independent. The agricultural concessions expired in Year 197 and were not renewed. The permanent board seat remains, occupied currently by a Morveth cousin who attends meetings irregularly and votes with the academic majority on most issues.
