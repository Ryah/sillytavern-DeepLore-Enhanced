# Hollow Road Entrance — Highwallow

A partially collapsed building in [[Highwallow]] conceals the most accessible known entrance to [[The Hollow Roads]] network. The structure was a warehouse once — the remaining walls still bear faded merchant signage in a script no one has used in fifty years. The roof caved in two decades ago, and the rubble was never cleared because doing so would have exposed what lies beneath: a stone-cut passage descending at a steep angle into darkness. The Academy has mapped the first kilometer. The [[Gnaino]] community knows precisely how far the network extends and has not shared this information with anyone.

The entrance itself is a rectangular gap in the warehouse's cellar floor, roughly six feet wide and lined with stonework that is conspicuously older and finer than anything else in Highwallow. The air rising from it is cold and carries a mineral smell — wet rock, iron deposits, and something faintly organic that defies identification. Sound behaves strangely near the opening: voices from below arrive distorted, stretched into vowels that last too long. The first few meters of the descent are lit by bioluminescent moss that the Academy's researchers have failed to cultivate anywhere else. Beyond the moss, the passage is absolute dark.

The [[Highwallow Mutual Aid Society]] keeps an unofficial watch on the building, less out of civic duty than practical concern — things occasionally come up from below. Nothing dangerous, so far. Rats with odd coloring. Insects that don't match any catalogued species. Once, a ceramic jar of unknown origin that the Academy confiscated before anyone could examine it properly. The Gnaino pass through the entrance freely, navigating the tunnels without light or hesitation, and return with trade goods whose provenance they decline to discuss.

The [[Solarguard]] has proposed sealing the entrance three times. Each proposal was quietly withdrawn after consultations with parties whose names do not appear in the official minutes. Whatever is down there, someone with authority has decided that access — however controlled, however dangerous — is preferable to ignorance.
