# The Majestic Boulder

A mid-range tavern in [[Amberburgh]] named ironically for its complete lack of any boulder-related decor. Popular with off-duty [[Solarguard]] officers who appreciate the moderate prices and the understanding that no one will ask about work.

The Boulder sits on a corner lot in Amberburgh's commercial district, its exterior distinguished only by a wooden sign depicting a rock wearing a crown — the only concession to the name, and one the owner commissioned as a joke that has outlasted whatever humor it originally contained. Inside, the room is wide and well-lit, with solid wood furniture built to withstand the kind of use that off-duty officers provide: heavy tables, chairs with reinforced joints, and a bar counter of lacquered oak that bears the ring stains of a thousand mugs placed with more force than necessary. The air smells of ale, roasted meat from the kitchen, and the leather-and-iron scent that clings to Solarguard officers even when they leave their armor at home.

The food is honest and abundant — meat pies, roasted vegetables, bread with butter, and a stew that changes daily depending on what the kitchen received that morning. The ale selection is broader than the wine selection, which tells you everything about the clientele. Prices are moderate enough that a patrol officer's salary covers a comfortable evening without calculation, and the portions are sized for people who spend their days on their feet in armor. The kitchen closes at midnight. The bar does not.

The Boulder's unspoken function is decompression. Officers who spend their shifts enforcing order in a city that resents them come here to sit among people who understand the work without needing to discuss it. Conversations are loud, jokes are coarse, and the occasional argument is settled with the efficiency of people trained in conflict resolution, even if the methods employed at the Boulder are less formal than those in the [[Solarguard Headquarters|headquarters]] manual. Civilians drink here too — Amberburgh merchants, guild workers, the occasional Academy administrator — and the mix works because the Boulder's primary rule is the same as its appeal: this is where you come to not be on duty.
