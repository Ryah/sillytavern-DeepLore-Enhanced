# Gnaino

The Gnaino are a subterranean species who have lived in the deep tunnel networks beneath Duskfrost since before the city existed above them. Their population is genuinely unknown — estimates range from 300 to over 1,000 — because the Gnaino do not participate in surface censuses and most of their settlements are in tunnels no surface-dweller has ever mapped. What is known: they navigate [[The Hollow Roads]] with an ease that surface species find unsettling, and they consider the tunnels their sovereign territory regardless of what the [[Triumvirate]]'s maps claim.

Gnaino echolocation works through a series of clicks and low-frequency hums produced in a specialized throat structure. In enclosed spaces, a Gnaino can map the dimensions of a room, count the people in it, and detect structural weaknesses in the walls — all with their eyes closed. On the surface, this ability degrades rapidly. Open air scatters their signals, and the ambient noise of a city street renders echolocation nearly useless. Surface-dwelling Gnaino describe the experience as being perpetually half-deaf, which explains why so few choose it.

Those Gnaino who do work above ground — perhaps sixty or seventy in Duskfrost at any given time — are predominantly tunnel surveyors, mining consultants, and structural engineers. The [[Wardenship]] contracts Gnaino inspectors to assess building foundations, a service the Gnaino provide at steep rates and with undisguised condescension toward surface architecture. Their reports are terse, accurate, and occasionally insulting. A Gnaino assessment that reads "adequate for surface standards" is understood to mean "this would not pass in a real structure."

Underground, Gnaino culture revolves around resonance. Their settlements are carved to specific acoustic profiles — living quarters tuned for intimacy, gathering halls tuned for community, worship spaces tuned for awe. A Gnaino who enters a new chamber can read its social purpose from the way sound moves through it. Their oral tradition is not spoken but *shaped* — stories told through the modulation of echo patterns in purpose-built narrative chambers, where the walls themselves become part of the telling.

The Gnaino maintain a complicated relationship with the surface city. They trade in rare minerals, deep-earth fungi, and geological surveys. They accept payment in metal, preserved food, and — increasingly — Academy-produced light sources, which younger Gnaino have adopted despite elder disapproval. They do not attend council meetings, respond to [[Solarguard]] jurisdiction, or acknowledge surface property law as it applies to anything below the foundation line. The Triumvirate has learned to work around this rather than challenge it, because the alternative is attempting to enforce authority in tunnels where the enforcers cannot see and the Gnaino can hear them coming from a quarter mile away.
