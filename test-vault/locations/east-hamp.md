# East Hamp District

East Hamp is a working-class residential district of dense housing, communal wells, and three markets that serve the district's significant [[Cirilae]] and [[Vorruk]] populations. The [[Marblesmiths]] guild recruits heavily here, and the guild's influence shows in the district's infrastructure — the streets are better paved than comparable neighborhoods, the communal wells are lined with cut stone rather than brick, and the guild hall on Millward Street is the largest non-residential building in the area.

The housing is dense and practical: rows of narrow stone-and-timber buildings, three stories tall, with shared walls and shared courtyards. Laundry lines cross the alleys at every level, and the sound of the district is domestic — children, arguments conducted through open windows, the rhythmic clang of the Marblesmiths' apprentice workshops that operate in ground-floor units throughout the eastern blocks. The air smells of cooking fires, the mineral dust that Marblesmiths track home on their boots, and the particular earthiness of communal wells that serve a hundred households each. The three markets are daily affairs — fresh food, household goods, and the inexpensive clothing that working families require — and they operate with the efficient informality of merchants who know every customer by name.

The Cirilae and Vorruk populations give East Hamp a cultural texture that distinguishes it from Duskfrost's other working districts. Cirilae feast-day decorations appear on buildings weeks before the actual celebrations, paper lanterns and woven garlands that transform the grey streets into something briefly vivid. Vorruk family compounds — identifiable by their distinctive arched doorways and the smell of their particular spiced tea — anchor the southern blocks. The two communities coexist with the pragmatic tolerance of neighbors who share wells and market stalls, which is not the same as friendship but is more durable.

The [[Ye Olde Guinea Pig Inn]] serves as East Hamp's informal community hub, and the [[Solarguard]] maintains a small substation on the district's western edge that is staffed during daylight hours and theoretically available at night. East Hamp's residents handle most of their own disputes internally and involve the watch only when property damage exceeds what the neighborhood's informal mediation network can resolve.
