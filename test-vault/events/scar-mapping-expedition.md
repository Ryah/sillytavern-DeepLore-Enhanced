# Scar Mapping Expedition

In Year 198, the [[Duskfrost Academy]] and the [[Solarguard]] mounted a joint expedition to map the full extent of the Resonance Scar in [[Highwallow]] — the zone of permanently destabilized arcane energy that marks the epicenter of the [[Calamity Onset|Calamity]]. The expedition was prompted by the [[Great Census of Year 180]]'s discovery that people were living significantly closer to the Scar boundary than anyone in authority had realized, and the uncomfortable question of whether those people were being slowly poisoned by proximity to uncontained arcane residue.

The expedition fielded twelve Academy researchers (three arcane surveyors, four geologists, five general scholars), a Solarguard escort of twenty officers, and a team of [[Gnaino]] tunnel specialists contracted to map the subsurface extent. The Gnaino were the first to report problems. Their instruments — sensitive to arcane fluctuations in ways that surface equipment was not — began registering anomalous readings within a hundred meters of the Scar boundary. At two hundred meters, compasses spun freely. At three hundred meters, sigil-based equipment ceased functioning entirely, and two Gnaino surveyors reported hearing sounds from below the ground that they refused to describe in their written reports.

The surface team mapped approximately 70% of the Scar boundary before the combined equipment failures forced withdrawal. The [[Wardenship]] researcher leading the arcane survey suffered a seizure on the thirteenth day and was evacuated to the Academy infirmary, where she recovered physically but reported persistent visual disturbances — specifically, the ability to see sigil patterns on surfaces where no sigils had been inscribed. This condition persisted for eight months. She declined to participate in subsequent expeditions.

The remaining 30% of the Scar boundary, concentrated in the southeastern zone where the Scar interfaces with the flooded areas created by the [[Great Flood of Highwallow]], remains officially unmapped. Unofficial maps exist — the Highwallow residents who live near the boundary have their own understanding of where it is safe to walk and where it is not, knowledge passed down through generations and maintained with considerably more practical accuracy than the Academy's survey. The Academy has proposed three follow-up expeditions since Year 198. All three have been approved in principle and cancelled in practice, because no one wants to fund the cost and no one wants to explain what the Gnaino heard underground.
