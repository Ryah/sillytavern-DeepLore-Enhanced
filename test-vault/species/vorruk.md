# Vorruk

Vorruk are large, heavily built humanoids — averaging six and a half feet tall, thick-boned, with dense grey skin that resists cuts and abrasions the way other species resist sunburn. Roughly 700 live in Duskfrost, concentrated in [[Scorchhelm]] and the dock wards, where they form the backbone of the physical labor economy. They are, by the numbers, the species most likely to be employed, most likely to be injured at work, and least likely to hold a position of institutional authority. These facts are related.

The narrative that Vorruk are simple — strong backs, small ambitions — is the most persistent and most convenient lie in Duskfrost. It is convenient because it justifies paying them less, housing them worse, and excluding them from the civic structures that might change either condition. The reality is that Vorruk culture, shaped by a lifespan of roughly seventy years, prioritizes immediacy and practical skill in ways that longer-lived species misread as a lack of sophistication.

Vorruk craftsmanship is functional, durable, and deeply undervalued. Their stonework — load-bearing walls, foundation reinforcement, and the heavy structural elements that keep Scorchhelm's industrial buildings standing — is the best in Duskfrost by a margin that embarrasses the [[Wardenship]]'s own engineers. A Vorruk mason apprentices at twelve, masters the trade by twenty, and spends the remaining fifty years building things that will outlast them by centuries. They take this seriously. The Scorchhelm quarter's oldest structures, the ones that survived the Calamity when everything around them collapsed, were Vorruk-built. The plaques do not mention this.

Their community in Scorchhelm is organized around work-crews — groups of eight to fifteen adults who contract collectively, share housing, pool wages, and function as extended family units regardless of blood relation. A Vorruk who loses their crew to injury or economic downturn does not become an individual looking for work; they become a displaced person looking for a community. The [[Bonehands]] understand this vulnerability perfectly, which is why their recruitment pipeline targets crewless Vorruk with offers of belonging as much as wages.

Vorruk political engagement is direct and frustrated. They vote in Scorchhelm district elections at higher rates than any other species, support candidates who promise labor protections, and watch those promises dissolve in [[Bellsummit]] committee sessions with a regularity that has bred deep cynicism. The [[Highwallow Mutual Aid]] network includes a Vorruk contingent that operates its own injury fund and widow's pension, parallel to and more reliable than the official Wardenship programs.

What outsiders rarely see is Vorruk art. Their oral poetry tradition — performed in a deep, rhythmic cadence that uses the chest cavity as a resonating chamber — is structured around work rhythms. Hauling songs, cutting songs, setting songs. The poetry is not about the labor; it is composed during the labor, shaped by the physical tempo of the task, and performed afterward as both record and memorial. A Vorruk work-song about laying foundation stones is also, always, about the people who laid them. The names are in the rhythm. The dead are in the pauses.

The [[Solarguard]] maintains a disproportionate presence in Scorchhelm's Vorruk neighborhoods, a fact justified by crime statistics that reflect policing patterns rather than actual criminal behavior. Vorruk elders have raised this issue with the Triumvirate formally four times. The Triumvirate has acknowledged the concern formally four times. Nothing has changed formally four times.
