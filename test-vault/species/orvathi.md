# Orvathi

Orvathi are nocturnal humanoids whose biology is locked to an inverted cycle — fully alert from dusk to dawn, functionally impaired in daylight. Roughly 800 live in Duskfrost, making them one of the larger minority species, and their influence on the city's night economy is disproportionate to their numbers. When the sun goes down, Duskfrost does not sleep. The Orvathi make sure of it.

Orvathi eyes contain a reflective layer that amplifies available light — moonlight is as clear to them as full sun is to a [[Human]]. The tradeoff is severe light sensitivity. Direct sunlight causes pain, disorientation, and after prolonged exposure, temporary blindness. Orvathi who must function during daylight hours wear tinted lenswork commissioned from [[Delivese]] craftsmen, though most simply avoid the issue by sleeping through the day in windowless rooms.

Their community centers on the [[Amberburgh]] night market quarter, where the [[Night Market Guild]] — an Orvathi-majority organization — coordinates a parallel economy that operates from sundown to sunrise. Night-market stalls sell everything the day markets sell, often at slightly better prices, because Orvathi merchants pay lower rent for nocturnal-only leases. The taverns, food stalls, and entertainment venues of the night quarter draw a mixed-species clientele: shift workers, insomniacs, [[Bolludine]] on their third sleep cycle, and anyone whose business prefers darkness.

Orvathi culture values discretion the way other cultures value honesty — not as the opposite of truth, but as its more sophisticated form. An Orvathi proverb: *The eye that sees in darkness learns what daylight hides.* Their social negotiations are layered, indirect, and conducted with a patience that daytime species sometimes mistake for passivity. The [[Crennish]] who work as Orvathi translators in multi-party negotiations have learned to read the pauses as carefully as the words.

Politically, the Orvathi are pragmatic. They maintain a seat on the [[Amberburgh Merchants League]] council and vote as a reliable bloc for policies that protect night-commerce rights. Their relationship with the [[Triumvirate]] is functional — the Orvathi pay taxes, observe curfew exemptions they negotiated decades ago, and in return expect the [[Solarguard]] to actually patrol the night quarter rather than treating it as a dead zone after dark. This expectation is met inconsistently, which is why the Night Market Guild funds its own security force: a discreet, efficient operation that handles problems before sunrise and rarely involves paperwork.

The Orvathi maintain an old rivalry with the [[Bonehands]], whose nighttime operations compete for the same shadows. The difference, as Orvathi elders are fond of noting, is that the Orvathi built the night economy. The Bonehands merely exploit it.
