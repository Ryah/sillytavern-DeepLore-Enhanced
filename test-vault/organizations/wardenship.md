# Wardenship

The Academy's enforcement arm, with jurisdiction over registered arcane practitioners anywhere in Duskfrost. Small — twenty-two wardens at full strength, rarely at full strength — but each is an accomplished practitioner capable of subduing most threats without backup. They operate parallel to the [[Solarguard]] with deliberately ambiguous overlap in jurisdiction, a design choice that benefits the Academy and frustrates everyone else.

</div>

Wardens operate in pairs, always. The standard deployment is one containment specialist and one investigator, though the distinction blurs in practice because every warden is trained in both. Their authority covers any crime involving arcane practice — unlicensed spellwork, prohibited binding rituals, resonance manipulation, and the broad category of "arcane public endangerment" that gives them jurisdiction over essentially anything they want. The [[Solarguard]] handles mundane crime; the Wardenship handles magical crime; and the substantial grey area between them is resolved through institutional ego, personal relationships, and occasionally shouting matches in the [[Scorchhelm]] liaison office. Commander Castellan of the Solarguard and Warden-Provost Drath maintain a functional professional relationship built on mutual respect and mutual suspicion.

Notable operations in recent memory include the raid on the [[Scourge of Vitality]]'s second safehouse in [[Highwallow]] — which succeeded tactically and failed legally when key evidence was ruled improperly obtained — and the quiet shutdown of three unlicensed practitioners in [[Highwallow]] who were selling resonance-tainted charms to desperate residents. That second operation is more typical of the Wardenship's actual work: unglamorous, necessary, and invisible to anyone living above the marsh line. Highwallow presents the Wardenship's most persistent problem. The district's proximity to the Resonance Scar means ambient arcane energy is higher, unlicensed practice is more common, and the line between "folk remedy" and "prohibited ritual" is genuinely unclear. The Wardenship's official position is that all unlicensed practice is illegal. Their practical position is that they lack the numbers to enforce that in a district where the [[Highwallow Mutual Aid Society]] openly employs hedge-practitioners for basic medical care, and cracking down would cause more problems than it solves.

The Wardenship's internal tension is generational. Senior wardens, trained in the decades after the [[Oracles Schism]], view their role as protecting the public from arcane harm. Junior wardens, recruited from a generation that grew up with the Academy's expanding commercial interests, increasingly view the role as protecting the Academy's regulatory monopoly. Warden-Provost Drath is aware of this drift and has not yet decided what to do about it.
