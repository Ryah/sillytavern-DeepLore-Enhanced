# Hedge Mage Pruvven Alt

Unlicensed hedge mage operating in [[Bayside Shushail]] — providing minor healing, warding charms, and practical enchantments to a client base that cannot afford Academy-trained practitioners and cannot always wait for [[Solarguard]]-approved magical services. His license situation is technically illegal under Triumvirate arcane regulation and practically tolerated because the alternative is people trying to self-enchant. He has an informal arrangement with [[Cecilie Sorensen]] to refer cases that exceed his competence.

Pruvven's workspace is a cluttered back room that smells of dried rosemary, chalk dust, and something faintly sulfuric. He works by candle and magelight, and his clients find him by word of mouth — there is no sign, no advertisement, and no address that appears on any official register. He learned his craft through apprenticeship, stolen glances at Academy texts, and forty years of trial and error. His healing is rough but effective. His wards are ugly but hold. His enchantments occasionally do something he did not intend, which he considers an acceptable margin.

The Academy pin he wears on his collar is genuine — he attended for one year before being expelled for "unsanctioned experimentation," which meant he was trying to develop practical applications for magic that the curriculum considered too crude for academic attention. He has never forgiven the institution, and he has never stopped teaching himself. His theoretical knowledge has gaps the size of lecture halls, but his practical knowledge exceeds that of most licensed practitioners, and he knows it.

He refers overflow work to [[Cecilie Sorensen]] and receives referrals in return — a symbiotic relationship that the Triumvirate's Arcane Regulatory Office would find extremely interesting if they ever looked closely at Bayside Shushail's healthcare infrastructure, which they have not done in eleven years.
