# Bolludine Shard Politics

The Bolludine family controls the only working set of long-distance communication shards in Duskfrost — seventeen paired devices that allow near-instant message relay across the city and to three external locations. The family's pricing, access policies, and occasional refusal of service have become a recurring political crisis. Three [[Duskfrost Triumvirate]] attempts to nationalize the shards have failed on the same legal technicality, which most legal scholars believe the Bolludines wrote into the relevant statute themselves.

The shards operate on a principle related to [[Speaking Stone]] resonance but at significantly greater range and clarity. Each shard is paired to exactly one counterpart; messages spoken into one emerge from the other within seconds. The Bolludines maintain eleven intra-city pairs connecting [[Bellsummit]], [[Amberburgh]], [[Scorchhelm]], and [[Highwallow]], plus three long-range pairs linking Duskfrost to trading partners along the coast. The long-range shards are the family's true leverage — without them, urgent communication with external cities requires a fast horse and three days of travel. The Bolludines charge accordingly: a single message costs between five and twenty crowns depending on distance, urgency, and how much the sender has annoyed the family recently. The "urgency" surcharge is notoriously subjective.

The political dimension extends beyond pricing. The Bolludines read every message that passes through their shards — they must, as the relay process requires a family member to physically handle each shard. This gives them an intelligence network that rivals the [[Wardenship]]'s, assembled passively and legally. The family insists they do not exploit this information. Their consistently excellent investment returns suggest otherwise. The Triumvirate's Commerce Board has proposed privacy regulations four times; the Bolludines have defeated each proposal by threatening to suspend service during the legislative debate, which would paralyze the city's commercial communication for weeks.

The [[Duskfrost Academy]] has attempted to replicate the shard technology for decades. Their best efforts produce paired stones that work reliably within a single building — useful for internal communication, useless for replacing the Bolludine network. The gap between Academy replicas and the original shards suggests pre-Calamity manufacturing techniques that have not been successfully reverse-engineered. The Bolludines maintain that the shards are family heirlooms of great sentimental value. The Academy maintains that the shards are pre-Calamity artifacts of immense research significance. Both claims are technically accurate and entirely beside the point, which is that whoever controls the shards controls the information flow, and the Bolludines have no intention of sharing.
