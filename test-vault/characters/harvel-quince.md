# Harvel Quince

Human debt collector for the [[Bonehands]] in [[Bayside Shushail]] — the person who visits when payments are overdue and has a conversation about the importance of keeping agreements. He is not violent unless the conversation exhausts other options, which he considers a professional standard rather than a moral position. He keeps detailed notes on every account and has never lost a debt to statute of limitations.

Quince dresses like a mid-tier clerk — pressed collar, clean boots, no weapons visible. The absence of visible threat is the threat. He sits down when he talks to people, accepts offered tea, compliments the household. He takes notes openly, which makes debtors more nervous than a knife would. Every detail goes into the ledger: who was home, what they were eating, what condition the furniture was in. He explains that this is standard recordkeeping. It is also an inventory.

The thing Quince will not admit — not to [[Snugug]], not to anyone in the Bonehands hierarchy — is that he waives interest on debts owed by families with sick children. Not formally. He adjusts the numbers in his ledger so the totals come out close enough, and covers the difference from his own cut. He has done this eleven times. He tells himself it is risk management: dead debtors pay nothing. But he knows it isn't that.

He maintains a cordial professional relationship with [[Dockmaster Bram Solvik]], who sometimes provides advance notice of which ships are carrying which cargo — useful for knowing when a debtor's fortunes are about to change.
