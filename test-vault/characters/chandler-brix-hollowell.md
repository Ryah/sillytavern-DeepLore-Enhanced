# Brix Hollowell

Human chandler and wax merchant supplying candles, lamp oil, and specialist preservation wax to three major institutions: the [[Oracles of Might]], the [[Bellsummit]] Academy, and the Triumvirate administrative compound. The breadth of his institutional relationships gives him incidental exposure to purchasing records across all three, and purchasing records are surprisingly informative about institutional priorities. He does not analyze this information deliberately. He notices things, which is different.

Brix's workshop is a warm, low-ceilinged room that smells like honey and rendered fat. He has been making candles for thirty-one years, and his hands can judge wax temperature by touch within two degrees. He talks constantly while he works — to customers, to apprentices, to himself — and the talking is how he processes what he sees. People dismiss chandlers as tradesmen. Brix has learned that tradesmen who enter through service doors see more than dignitaries who enter through the front.

The thing Brix will not acknowledge, even to himself, is that his noticing has become deliberate. He tracks which institutions increase their candle orders (more late-night work), which request preservation-grade wax (new acquisitions or document handling), and which change their delivery schedule (altered security rotations). He has never sold this information. He has, twice, mentioned observations to [[Steward Pela Forthwick]] in a way that could be mistaken for idle conversation. She did not mistake it.

He considers [[Gorduin Wynlar]] his most interesting client — the Oracles' wax orders have tripled in the past year, suggesting either a major preservation project or a significant volume of new documents entering the collection. Brix has not asked which. He is waiting to see if Gorduin volunteers the information, which will tell him more than the answer itself.
