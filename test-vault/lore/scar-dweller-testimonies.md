# Scar Dweller Testimonies

People who live adjacent to the [[Resonance Scar Properties|Resonance Scar]] report consistent effects: tools occasionally refuse to function, dreams near the boundary are unusually vivid, and animals avoid the area. Children born in the Scar-adjacent tenements show slightly elevated [[Arcane Bloodline Inheritance|arcane affinity]] rates. The [[Duskfrost Academy]] considers this data; the residents consider it their landlords' problem.

The testimonies, collected systematically since Year 240 by the Academy's social research division, paint a picture of adaptation rather than suffering. Scar-adjacent residents have developed practical workarounds for the Scar's interference: non-arcane tool alternatives for the two or three days per month when the boundary shifts close enough to cause failures, sleep routines that account for the vivid dreams (many residents simply avoid sleeping near exterior walls facing the Scar), and a informal warning system — children stationed at boundary markers who shout when the Scar's visible shimmer intensifies, signaling residents to secure arcane equipment. The tenements closest to the boundary charge lower rent, attracting the city's poorest residents, who then develop the most expertise in living with the Scar's effects. This expertise has commercial value: Scar-adjacent residents hire out as guides for Academy researchers, [[Wardenship]] patrols, and the occasional foolish tourist. The going rate is five crowns per hour, non-negotiable, payment in advance.

The dream phenomenon is the testimony detail that interests the Academy most. Residents report not merely vivid dreams but shared ones — multiple people in the same tenement building dreaming the same imagery on the same night. The imagery is consistent: geometric patterns, sounds described as "singing stone," and a pervasive sense of being inside something very large and very old. The Academy's oneirology specialist has catalogued over three hundred shared dream reports and concluded that the dreams correspond, in broad structure, to descriptions of the pre-Calamity resonance tower network found in the [[Antecedent Script]] translations. Whether the Scar is broadcasting memories of the network it destroyed, or whether the residents are simply absorbing ambient arcane patterns that their sleeping minds interpret as architecture, is an open question with implications the Academy finds "premature to publish."

Most documented testimonies come from the eastern districts closest to [[Bellsummit]], where the boundary is most active and the tenement density highest. The western Scar boundary, which abuts less populated areas, produces fewer testimonies but more unusual ones — residents there report not dreams but sounds, heard during waking hours, emanating from the ground. The sounds are described as rhythmic, metallic, and purposeful, as though something beneath the surface is working. The Academy attributes this to subsurface water movement through pre-Calamity infrastructure. The residents attribute it to the [[Resonance Scar Tunnels|tunnels]]. Neither explanation is entirely satisfying.
