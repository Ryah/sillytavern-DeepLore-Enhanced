# Zuvine Harbor Treaty

Year 189. For two decades prior to the treaty, [[Zuvine]] communities and Duskfrost's surface authorities had been locked in a dispute that both sides recognized was unsustainable. The Zuvine had been living in the underwater cave networks beneath the harbor since before the Calamity — they were not immigrants but original inhabitants of a territory that the surface city had expanded over without consultation. As Duskfrost's harbor grew and dive salvage became economically significant, the Zuvine found their ancestral waters being claimed by surface enterprises that had neither the capability to work them safely nor the legal standing to exclude those who could.

The disputes turned violent twice: in Year 171, when a surface salvage crew's equipment damaged a Zuvine nursery cave and killed three juveniles, and in Year 183, when Zuvine foragers sank a salvage vessel whose crew had been dumping toxic waste into an inhabited cave system. The Triumvirate's initial response to both incidents was to treat them as crimes rather than political conflicts — an approach that the Zuvine elders rejected with a diplomatic coldness that their song-cycles describe as "the silence before the current turns." The threat was implicit: the Zuvine controlled the harbor's underwater infrastructure, and if they chose to make that infrastructure unavailable, Duskfrost's maritime economy would collapse within months.

The treaty, negotiated over eight months with the [[Duskfrost Academy]] serving as a nominally neutral mediator, granted Zuvine communities legal personhood under Duskfrost law (they had previously been classified as "resident non-citizens," a category that afforded fewer protections than it sounds like), surface residency rights in designated harbor-adjacent zones, and exclusive salvage licenses for the inner harbor's submerged ruins. In exchange, the Zuvine shared navigational charts of the underwater cave networks — maps that revealed passages extending far beyond the harbor, connecting to the deep ocean and to submerged chambers that the Academy had not known existed. These charts transformed the Academy's understanding of the pre-Calamity coastal geography and remain among the most valuable documents in the restricted archive.

The Academy added an undisclosed rider to the treaty that Zuvine community leaders have never publicly discussed. The rider's contents are classified at the highest level — access requires authorization from both the Academy Senior Council and the Zuvine Elder Circle simultaneously. Speculation about its contents ranges from the mundane (exclusive Academy access to certain archaeological sites) to the alarming (knowledge of pre-Calamity technology surviving in the deep caves). The Zuvine elders, when asked, respond with a phrase that translates as "the deep water keeps its own counsel," which is either a cultural proverb or a very specific warning, depending on who is interpreting it.
