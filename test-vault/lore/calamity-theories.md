# Theories of the Calamity

Three major theories compete to explain the Calamity. The accident theory holds it was a cascading engineering failure. The attack theory holds it was a deliberate weapon. The transcendence theory holds it was intentional — that the Antecedent Culture triggered the Calamity deliberately to achieve something they considered worth the cost. The [[Scribe Fragment — Lost Translation]] (if authentic) supports none of them cleanly and all of them partially.

The accident theory is the most widely accepted among mainstream scholars and the most politically comfortable. It holds that the resonance tower network — the pre-Calamity civilization's primary infrastructure for arcane power distribution — experienced a cascading harmonic failure. One tower fell out of alignment, the neighboring towers attempted to compensate, the compensation destabilized further nodes, and within hours the entire network collapsed in a chain reaction that released centuries of stored arcane energy simultaneously. The theory explains the damage pattern (radiating outward from ley line convergence points) and the [[Resonance Scar Properties]] observed in [[Highwallow]]. Its weakness is that it requires the Antecedent Culture to have built a world-spanning energy network with no failsafe against exactly this kind of cascade — and the [[Antecedent Script]] translations suggest they were not that careless.

The attack theory holds that an external or internal faction weaponized the resonance network deliberately. Proponents point to the [[Seven Seals]], which appear to be containment mechanisms placed at key network nodes — why build containment for a system you trust? The theory requires a motive, and here proponents diverge: some argue invasion by an unknown external force, others argue civil war between factions of the Antecedent Culture itself. The [[Pre-Calamity Species Records]] suggest the civilization was multispecies, and inter-species conflict is a perennial candidate for the attack motive. The theory's weakness is the absence of any identifiable attacker or weapon in the archaeological record.

The transcendence theory is the most controversial and the one that keeps [[Gorduin Wynlar]] up at night. It holds that the Antecedent Culture triggered the Calamity as a deliberate act — not as destruction but as transformation. The Scribe Fragment's reference to towers "warning" rather than "malfunctioning" is the theory's strongest evidence. The transcendence theorists argue that the civilization attempted to use the resonance network to achieve something beyond current understanding — ascension, dimensional travel, communion with something — and that the Calamity was either the attempt's failure or its success, depending on definitions of success that most people find deeply uncomfortable. The [[Oracles of Might]] archive holds the most complete collection of competing theoretical frameworks, and the transcendence materials are kept under Tier 2 restriction — not because they are dangerous, but because the Oracles consider public discussion of the theory "unhelpfully destabilizing."
