# Oracles Schism

In Year 271, the [[Oracles of Might]] fractured over a question that sounds academic but carried lethal implications: should research into pre-Calamity resonance theory be published openly or restricted to the guild's internal membership? The immediate trigger was a paper by Oracle Reshen Khault titled "Harmonic Frequencies of the Antecedent Grid: A Reconstruction," which demonstrated — theoretically — how a functioning resonance tower could be activated using modern sigil techniques. Khault wanted to publish. The Oracle leadership wanted the paper sealed. Khault argued that suppressing knowledge was what had caused the [[Academy Fire of Year 211]]. The leadership argued that publishing weaponizable knowledge was what had caused the [[Calamity Onset|Calamity]].

The debate lasted three months and was, by all accounts, vicious. Twenty-seven of the Oracles' ninety-one members sided with Khault. When the leadership voted to seal not only Khault's paper but all ongoing resonance research, the twenty-seven walked out of the guild hall and did not return. They established themselves as the [[Mages of the Patient]] — the name a deliberate rebuke, implying that the Oracles lacked the patience to let knowledge do its work in the open. The Oracles responded by revoking the splinter group's access to the guild archive, a punishment that the Mages circumvented within a year by establishing their own research program funded by sympathetic [[Duskfrost Academy]] faculty.

Relations between the two organizations have been chilly ever since, which is to say: they attend the same academic conferences, cite each other's work only when absolutely unavoidable, and maintain a mutual pretense of professional respect that fools no one. The practical consequence of the schism is that Duskfrost's arcane research community is permanently divided on the fundamental question of knowledge security. The Oracles guard what they know. The Mages publish what they find. Both believe the other's approach will eventually get people killed.

Khault's original paper was never published. The [[Wardenship]] confiscated the Mages' copy in Year 274 under an Academy security directive. Khault herself died in Year 289 during the [[War of the Broken Mountain]], defending the Academy against Harcyne forces — an irony that both organizations claim supports their position.
