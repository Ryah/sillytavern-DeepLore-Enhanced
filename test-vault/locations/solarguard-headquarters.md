# Solarguard Headquarters

The Solarguard's administrative core sits at the center of the [[Scorchhelm]] fortress complex — command offices, holding cells, records vault, and the watch commander's suite. All district patrol officers report here for shift changes. Officially open to the public for complaints and missing person reports. In practice, the public finds reasons to avoid it.

The building is a squat, functional block of grey stone that makes no attempt at architectural beauty. The front entrance opens into a receiving hall that smells permanently of old paper, lamp oil, and the particular variety of sweat produced by people waiting to explain themselves to authority. Wooden benches line the walls, their surfaces carved with generations of bored graffiti that no one has bothered to sand away. A duty sergeant sits behind an iron-banded counter, processing complaints with the mechanical patience of someone who has heard every possible lie and several impossible ones. Behind the counter, corridors branch into the records rooms — floor-to-ceiling shelves of case files, patrol logs, and incident reports dating back decades, maintained by clerks who can locate any document within minutes and guard their filing system with territorial ferocity.

The holding cells occupy the building's eastern wing, below ground level. They are clean, cold, and deliberately uncomfortable — stone floors, iron cots bolted to walls, and a ventilation system that carries sound from the interrogation rooms with suspicious clarity. The cells are rarely full. The [[Solarguard]] prefers to process arrests quickly: charges filed, fines levied, or suspects transferred to the Triumvirate courts within forty-eight hours. Extended detention requires paperwork that no duty sergeant wants to complete.

The watch commander's suite occupies the top floor, offering a view of the [[Scorchhelm Training Grounds]] through narrow windows that were designed for defense and have never been widened. The current commander keeps the room sparse — a desk, a map table, two chairs, and a locked cabinet that contains the district intelligence summaries the [[Solarguard]] officially does not compile. The corridor outside the suite is where patrol captains wait for their monthly performance reviews, and the bench there is noticeably more worn than any other in the building.
