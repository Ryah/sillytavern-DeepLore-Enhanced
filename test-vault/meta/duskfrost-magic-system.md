# Duskfrost Magic System

Magic in Duskfrost is channeled through sigils — geometric patterns drawn or inscribed that serve as conduits for arcane energy. Sigils must be learned, practiced, and maintained. They do not work for those without bloodline affinity unless powered by an external source (a costly and unreliable process).

**Bloodline affinities** vary by lineage. Elves typically have affinity for preservation and binding magic. Dwarves for earth-shaping and metallurgical enhancement. Humans show the widest variance — some have strong affinity, most have none.

**The Academy** regulates arcane practice within Duskfrost city limits. Unlicensed spellwork is technically illegal but enforcement is inconsistent. Practitioners in [[Highwallow]] operate openly despite this.

**Costs:** All significant spellwork requires either time (preparation, ritual), materials (reagents, anchors), or life-force (blood, years, vitality). The larger the effect, the higher the cost. Catastrophic violations of this principle are the most common explanation offered for the Calamity.

**Wild magic** is untrained, instinctive arcane expression. It exists primarily in children before formal training and in rare individuals who cannot be trained at all. Wild mages are viewed with a mix of pity and fear.
