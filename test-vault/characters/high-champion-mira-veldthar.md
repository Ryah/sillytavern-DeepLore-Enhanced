# High Champion Mira Veldthar

Mira Veldthar is the last true believer, or the last fool, depending on whom you ask in [[Scorchhelm]]. She has led the [[Champions of Patience]] for nine years, inheriting an order at its lowest membership since Year 180 and maintaining it through force of conviction in a city that runs on force of leverage.

The Champions' code prohibits accepting payment for protection, which means Mira leads an organization of eighty warriors on Academy stipends and public donations. She manages this by being extraordinarily good at two things: inspiring loyalty in people who have other options, and making institutional poverty look like moral clarity rather than organizational failure.

She fought at the [[Battle of Vryhs]] as a field champion — one of the last large-scale engagements the Champions were involved in. Three scars on her left forearm are from blocking a blade meant for a civilian who had refused to evacuate. She does not discuss the battle. The scars speak for themselves, and she lets them.

Her relationship with [[Warden-Captain Orin Castellan]] is cordial and slightly wary. Both women lead institutions that protect Duskfrost's citizens. Castellan does it with four hundred officers and a realistic view of corruption. Mira does it with eighty Champions and a code that dates from the Reconstruction era. They respect each other. They do not entirely understand each other.

She considers [[Tybellan Hercyne]] the Champions' most effective field agent and their most unreliable one, which are related qualities. She has declined to formally discipline him for his Oracles affiliation on the grounds that his competence serves the order's mission even when his methods do not serve its traditions.

The Champions are declining. Mira knows this. She responds by maintaining standards rather than lowering them to attract recruits, a decision that is either the order's salvation or its epitaph. She has not decided which. She does not intend to find out during her tenure.
