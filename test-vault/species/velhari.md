# Velhari

Velhari are humanoids with a natural ability to bend and shape ambient light — producing flickering images, color shifts, and localized visual distortions without any arcane training or external tools. The ability is biological, not magical, a distinction the Academy has confirmed and the general public persistently ignores. Approximately 300 Velhari live in Duskfrost, with their largest community in the lower districts of [[Bellsummit]] and a significant presence in [[Amberburgh]]'s entertainment quarter.

Velhari light manipulation operates through specialized cells in their hands and forearms that emit and redirect photons in controlled patterns. The range is limited — most Velhari can project stable images no more than ten feet away — and the complexity ceiling is real. A skilled Velhari can produce a convincing flame, a shifting pattern of color, or a brief visual distraction. They cannot produce a convincing duplicate of a person, sustain an illusion for more than a few minutes without concentration, or affect anything other than visible light. No sound, no touch, no smell. The limitations matter, because the assumptions about what Velhari *can* do have shaped how other species treat them far more than the reality.

The performance industry is the community's economic backbone. Velhari stage performers, street entertainers, and visual artists command premium rates in Amberburgh's theater district, where their abilities turn a simple storytelling performance into an immersive light show. The annual Festival of Lamps — originally a Velhari cultural celebration — has become one of Duskfrost's most popular public events, drawing crowds from every district to watch Velhari artists create collaborative light sculptures above the Amberburgh rooftops.

The less celebrated side of Velhari life is the persistent suspicion that follows them. A species that can create visual deceptions — even minor, limited ones — attracts distrust. The [[Solarguard]] maintains an informal (and technically illegal) registry of Velhari residents, justified by a string of confidence schemes in which Velhari illusions were used to misdirect marks during street-level fraud. The schemes were real. The registry's scope is not proportionate. Most Velhari in Duskfrost are performers, shopkeepers, and artisans who find the association with criminality exhausting.

Velhari culture places high value on what they call *clarity* — the deliberate choice to present oneself honestly despite the ability to deceive. A Velhari who uses their ability to mislead in personal relationships is subject to community censure that is swift and absolute. Ironically, a species that other cultures associate with deception holds truthfulness as its highest social virtue, precisely because the temptation to lie is so readily available.

The community's relationship with the [[Triumvirate]] is cautious. Velhari leaders lobby consistently for anti-discrimination protections, with limited success. The [[Bellsummit Council]] is sympathetic. The [[Bonehands]] recruit Velhari for obvious reasons and create exactly the kind of criminal associations the community is trying to escape. The [[Selaveth]], who can perceive the non-arcane nature of Velhari abilities directly, are the community's most reliable character witnesses — a small, quiet alliance between two species that understand what it means to be defined by a single trait.
