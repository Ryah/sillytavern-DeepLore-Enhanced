# Corporal Thresh Dunmore

[[Solarguard]] corporal assigned to gate duty at [[Bellsummit]]'s eastern approach. He has been in this posting for three years and knows every regular face that passes his gate. He has a prodigious memory for faces and a principled objection to being bribed — not on moral grounds, but because he calculated once that the risks outweigh the income. He will, however, accept bribes in the form of good information, which he considers a different category of transaction.

Thresh keeps a notebook — not an official log, a personal one — where he records faces, times, and patterns. He's noticed things the duty officer hasn't: that certain merchants arrive on specific days that correlate with [[Bonehands]] shipment schedules, that one particular half-elf researcher enters Bellsummit every third week carrying bags heavier than books would explain. He hasn't reported any of this formally because he's still assembling the picture, and because a corporal who makes accusations above his station tends to stop being a corporal.

What Thresh won't admit is that he requested this posting. Everyone assumes gate duty is a dead end — the assignment you get when nobody wants you elsewhere. Thresh asked for it specifically because the eastern gate sees every traveler between the districts, and he wanted to watch. He grew up in Highwallow where you learned to read a street by who walked it, and he's applied that skill to a chokepoint where the whole city passes through.

He has an informal arrangement with [[Mivi]] — she brings him coffee and district gossip from Bayside Shushail, and he lets her through the gate without the standard sixteen questions. He tells himself this is an intelligence relationship. It is also the closest thing he has to a friendship.
