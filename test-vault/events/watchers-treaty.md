# Watchers Treaty

In Year 301, [[Solarguard]] Commander Elia Vayne and [[Bonehands]] leader Thick Margol met in a private room above a tavern in [[Bayside Shushail]] and spent four hours negotiating what became the most consequential agreement in Duskfrost's criminal history — and one that neither party has ever admitted exists. No document was signed. No witnesses were present. The terms were agreed verbally and have been transmitted through organizational leadership on both sides as institutional knowledge ever since.

The treaty's terms are simple: the Solarguard holds primary law enforcement authority in the upper districts — [[Bellsummit]], [[Scorchhelm]], and the Amberburgh commercial zone. The Bonehands hold primary authority (the treaty does not use the word "control," because that would imply criminality) over the harbor district and the [[Highwallow]] marshes. [[Bayside Shushail]] is contested but governed by an unwritten understanding that the Bonehands handle everything below the third pier and the Solarguard handles everything above it. Disputes in the buffer zone are resolved by whoever gets there first, a system that has produced remarkably few conflicts because both sides are careful about where they send patrols.

The treaty was prompted by three years of escalating violence between Solarguard officers and Bonehands enforcers in the harbor district — a period known informally as "the red tides" because bodies kept washing up at the harbor mouth. Commander Vayne, a pragmatist who had risen through the ranks on competence rather than ideology, calculated that the Solarguard was losing the harbor fight: her officers were outnumbered in the district, their intelligence was worse than the Bonehands', and every enforcement action produced reprisals that damaged the civilian population she was theoretically protecting. Margol, for his part, was tired of losing enforcers to Solarguard raids that accomplished nothing except raising his recruitment costs. Both drew on the lesson of the [[Battle of the Vanguard Crossing]], which had demonstrated two centuries earlier that open conflict over the harbor benefited neither the governing authority nor its rivals.

Both sides have violated the treaty repeatedly — the Solarguard conducts periodic "operations" in the harbor to maintain appearances, and the Bonehands expand into upper-district protection rackets whenever they think they can get away with it. Both sides pretend the treaty does not exist, because acknowledging it would mean the Solarguard publicly admitting it negotiated with criminals, and the Bonehands publicly admitting they accepted limits on their territory. The current Solarguard commander, Warden-Captain Orin Castellan, inherited knowledge of the treaty from her predecessor and has honored it with the same pragmatic resignation that characterized its creation. Thick Margol is dead — his successor, whose identity the Bonehands do not publicly confirm, has maintained the arrangement. The tavern where the treaty was negotiated burned down in Year 308. No one has suggested this was deliberate, and no one has suggested it was not.
