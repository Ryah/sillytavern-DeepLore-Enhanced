# Resonance Scar Tunnels

[[The Hollow Roads]] pass beneath the [[Resonance Scar Properties|Resonance Scar]], and the tunnels in that section are unlike the rest of the network. The walls emit low-level light that varies with the Scar's activity cycle. Navigation instruments fail. Three [[Duskfrost Academy]] survey teams have entered; two returned with complete data, one returned with incomplete data and no explanation for the missing six hours. Access is currently restricted.

The light is the tunnels' most studied and least understood property. In the standard [[Hollow Roads]] sections, the walls are inert stone — smooth, dark, unremarkable. Beneath the Scar, the same stone emits a pale luminescence that pulses in slow cycles, brightening and dimming over periods of approximately forty minutes. The light's color shifts across the spectrum in patterns that Academy researchers have mapped exhaustively: blue during the Scar's quiescent periods, shifting through green and amber during active periods, and on three recorded occasions turning a deep red that coincided with significant arcane events on the surface — one being a major Scar boundary expansion, one being an earthquake, and one being nothing anyone has been able to identify. The light does not behave like bioluminescence or chemical phosphorescence; spectrographic analysis shows it matching the [[Resonance Frequency Theory|resonance frequency]] profile of the Scar itself, as though the stone is converting arcane energy directly into visible light.

The navigation failures are more concerning than the light. Standard compasses spin continuously. Arcane wayfinding instruments produce readings that are internally consistent but do not correspond to any known direction or distance. The third survey team — the one that lost six hours — reported that their instruments showed them traveling in a straight line for approximately two kilometers, which should have taken them well beyond the Scar's surface boundary. When they emerged, they were less than three hundred meters from their entry point, in a location their instruments said was impossible. The team members disagreed on basic details of their experience: two recalled the tunnel branching left, one recalled it branching right, and the team leader recalled no branch at all. Their written notes, taken in real-time, contradicted all four accounts.

The [[Gnaino]] use these tunnels. This is known because Gnaino trade goods occasionally emerge from [[Highwallow]] access points that connect only to the Scar tunnel section, and because Gnaino traders have been observed entering these same access points and reappearing at distant locations faster than surface travel would allow. When asked how they navigate tunnels that defeat Academy instruments, the Gnaino response is typically a variation of "we listen." The Academy has deployed acoustic monitoring equipment in the Scar tunnels and confirmed that the walls produce sound — a low, subsonic hum that shifts frequency in patterns the Gnaino apparently find meaningful and that Academy acousticians find indistinguishable from random noise. The current access restriction was imposed jointly by the Academy and the [[Wardenship]] after the third survey team's incident, but enforcement at the Highwallow access points is inconsistent, and the Gnaino ignore it entirely.
