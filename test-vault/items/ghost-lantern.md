# Ghost Lantern

A lantern that burns with a pale blue flame fed by ambient spiritual energy rather than fuel. When held in locations with strong emotional history, it illuminates residual impressions — not ghosts precisely, but echoes of significant past events. Useful for investigation; deeply unsettling in prolonged use.

The lantern is a standard iron-and-glass construction of Reconstruction-era manufacture, unremarkable in every respect except that it contains no wick, no oil reservoir, and no fuel mechanism of any kind. The pale blue flame ignites when the lantern is picked up and extinguishes when it is set down, responding to the holder's grip rather than any physical ignition source. The flame produces no heat. It produces light that behaves oddly — casting shadows in directions inconsistent with the lantern's position, as though illuminating the room from a different angle than the one it occupies. The [[Duskfrost Academy]] classifies this as a "perceptual distortion artifact," meaning the light shows the user things that are real but not currently present.

In emotionally neutral environments — empty rooms, unused corridors, open fields — the lantern produces ordinary pale blue illumination and nothing more. In locations where significant emotional events occurred — violence, grief, terror, occasionally intense joy — the light reveals translucent impressions of those events playing out in the space. The impressions are silent, incomplete, and disorienting: figures that walk through walls, actions that begin mid-motion, faces that are almost but not quite recognizable. The impressions do not interact with the viewer or respond to their presence. They are recordings, not entities. This distinction is important because the experience of watching a murder replay itself in pale blue light in an empty room is sufficiently disturbing that users frequently forget the distinction.

One was recovered from the ruins near [[Scorchhelm]] and is used occasionally by the city watch for crime scene investigation, with strict operational limits: no more than thirty minutes of continuous use, no solo operation, and mandatory debriefing afterward. The thirty-minute limit was established after a watchman used the lantern for four hours straight in a house where a family had been killed, and spent the following week unable to distinguish between living people and the lantern's impressions. He recovered. He refused to touch the lantern again. A second Ghost Lantern is rumored to exist in private hands in [[Highwallow]], used by an unlicensed investigator who charges premium rates for cases the watch cannot solve. The [[Wardenship]] has neither confirmed nor denied this, which in Wardenship protocol language means they know exactly who has it and have decided not to interfere.
