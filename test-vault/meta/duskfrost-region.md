# Duskfrost Region

Duskfrost is not alone, but it sometimes acts like it. The city sits on a coastal bluff at the intersection of two trade routes and one navigable river, which makes it a natural commercial hub and a natural target.

**Northern coast:** Rocky, cold, and sparsely settled. Fishing villages cling to the shoreline at irregular intervals. The [[Delivese]] originally migrated from settlements further north along this coast. Trade is possible but seasonal — winter ice closes the northern sea routes for two to three months.

**Southern lowlands:** Agricultural land controlled primarily by [[Duskfrost Triumvirate|House Harcyne]]. Grain farms, root vegetable cultivation, and the modest livestock operations that supply Duskfrost's limited meat market. Several villages depend on Harcyne contracts for survival, which gives the house leverage that no one in those villages would describe as benevolent.

**Eastern hills:** Mining territory. Iron, copper, and trace quantities of arcane-reactive minerals. The hills are home to several independent mining settlements that trade with Duskfrost through [[Amberburgh]] merchants. The [[Hollow Roads]] extend in this direction, though how far is unknown.

**Western sea:** Open ocean. Duskfrost's primary long-distance trade routes run westward to a chain of coastal cities. The [[Velkhast Trading Company]] controlled three of these routes before the succession crisis. Two remain functional. The third has been claimed by competitors.

**The nearest significant settlement** is Thornmark, a mining town three days east by road. It has a population of approximately 4,000 and a relationship with Duskfrost best described as dependently resentful — Thornmark needs Duskfrost's market, and Duskfrost's merchants set prices accordingly.

**Threats:** Banditry on the eastern road is persistent but manageable. Piracy on the western sea routes fluctuates with the strength of the Velkhast navy, which is currently at a low point. Creatures from the deeper Hollow Roads occasionally surface in the eastern hills. The Academy funds bounties for their capture or destruction, depending on the creature.

The region beyond a week's travel is poorly mapped and largely irrelevant to Duskfrost's daily politics, which is another way of saying that the city has not invested in exploration because it has enough problems within its own walls.
