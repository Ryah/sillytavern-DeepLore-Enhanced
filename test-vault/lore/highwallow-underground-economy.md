# Highwallow Underground Economy

[[Highwallow]] hosts a thriving informal economy in arcane services that the [[Wardenship]] is technically required to suppress and practically unable to. Unlicensed practitioners offer diagnosis, minor warding, and sigil work at rates the licensed [[Duskfrost Academy]] clinics cannot match. The [[Resonance Scar Properties|Scar]]'s interference makes accurate measurement difficult, providing convenient deniability for all parties. The Wardenship makes approximately three dozen arrests per year; the number of active unlicensed practitioners is estimated in the hundreds.

The economics are straightforward. An Academy-licensed practitioner charges between eight and twenty crowns for basic diagnostic work — identifying curses, measuring arcane exposure, assessing ward integrity. The fee reflects six years of Academy training, licensing costs, and the practitioner's obligation to maintain insurance against malpractice claims. An unlicensed Highwallow practitioner charges two to five crowns for the same work, carries no insurance, and learned their craft through apprenticeship to another unlicensed practitioner. The quality varies enormously — some unlicensed practitioners are Academy washouts with genuine skill and bad attitudes, while others are barely competent hedge-workers running on confidence and the placebo effect. The Scar's ambient interference means that even competent work occasionally produces unexpected results, which makes it impossible to distinguish malpractice from environmental effects. This ambiguity is the ecosystem's life support.

Beyond basic services, Highwallow's underground economy trades in materials that the Academy regulates heavily: unregistered sigil templates, raw resonance-reactive minerals scraped from the Scar boundary, and occasionally fragments of pre-Calamity artifacts pulled from the [[Resonance Scar Tunnels|tunnel network]] beneath the district. The mineral trade is particularly lucrative — Scar-boundary minerals exhibit unusual resonance properties that licensed alchemists prize but cannot legally source. A fist-sized chunk of Scar-boundary quartz sells for forty crowns in the [[Nightmarket Customs|night market]], four times what it would cost through Academy-regulated channels if those channels existed, which they do not because the Academy prohibits extraction from Scar-adjacent sites.

The Wardenship's enforcement strategy amounts to managed tolerance. The three dozen annual arrests target practitioners who cause visible harm, sell dangerously unstable sigil work, or — the real trigger — begin operating at a scale that threatens licensed practitioners' revenue in adjacent districts. Small-time hedge-workers who serve their immediate neighborhood are left alone. The arrangement is not formalized and would be denied by all parties if described publicly, but it has been stable for decades. The Academy's periodic calls for a crackdown are met with the Wardenship's periodic requests for additional funding and personnel, and neither side follows through.
