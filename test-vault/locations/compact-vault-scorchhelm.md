# Compact Vault

Three levels below the [[Scorchhelm]] fortress, accessible only with three Triumvirate keys plus Academy authorization. The descent takes you through cut stone that predates the fortress itself — the walls of the second sub-level bear chisel marks in a pattern no living stonemason recognizes, and the air grows colder with each flight of stairs. By the third level, breath clouds visibly, and the silence is the kind that presses against the ears. The vault stores the original [[Binding Compact]] document and several other foundational treaties. The [[Solarguard]] guards the entrance but is not permitted to enter.

The vault door is a slab of reinforced iron eight inches thick, set into a frame of warded stone. Three separate keys are required to open it: one held by the Triumvirate's civilian chair, one by the [[Champions of Patience]] Knight Commander, and one by the Academy Archmage. All three keyholders must be physically present. There is no override, no emergency protocol, and no exception. The last time the vault was opened — seven years ago, for a treaty verification — the process took four hours of scheduling alone. The locking mechanism is not mechanical. It responds to the keys in a way the Academy has studied and the Solarguard prefers not to think about.

Inside, the chamber is small and dry, the air preserved by wards that have not been refreshed in living memory yet continue to function. The original Binding Compact sits under glass on a stone pedestal, the ink still sharp after centuries. Surrounding it in smaller cases are the foundational documents of Duskfrost's governance: the Merchants League charter, the original [[Wardenship]] mandate, the [[Oracles of Might]] guild articles, and three sealed documents whose contents are known only to the Triumvirate members who last read them. The walls are bare stone, undecorated. There is nothing in this room that exists for comfort.

The vault's political significance outweighs its practical function. No one needs to read the Binding Compact — its terms are well known. But its physical existence beneath Scorchhelm is a daily reminder of who holds the foundational authority in Duskfrost, and every faction that has ever proposed moving the document to a "more appropriate" location has been quietly reminded that the vault was built first, and the fortress was built around it.
