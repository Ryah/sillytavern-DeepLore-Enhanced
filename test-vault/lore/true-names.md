# True Names — Theory and Danger

The pre-Calamity civilization believed that every sufficiently complex entity possesses a true name — a fundamental identifier that grants significant power over the entity to any speaker. True names for humans are theoretically derivable but practically inaccessible. True names for certain non-human entities are different: they are fixed, discoverable, and extraordinarily dangerous.

The theoretical basis, as reconstructed from the [[Antecedent Script]] translations, holds that a true name is not a word in any conventional sense but a [[Resonance Frequency Theory|resonance frequency]] — the specific harmonic pattern that defines an entity's fundamental arcane signature. Speaking a true name is not vocal; it is resonant. The speaker must produce the exact frequency pattern through sigil work, vocalization, or both, creating a harmonic lock that binds the named entity to the speaker's will. The binding is not metaphorical. A named entity cannot disobey a direct command from the holder of its name, cannot conceal itself from the holder, and cannot act against the holder's interests as the holder defines them. The cost is equally literal: holding a true name creates a resonance link that works in both directions. The holder gains power over the entity; the entity gains awareness of the holder. For minor entities, this awareness is inconsequential. For major ones, it is an invitation.

The [[Duskfrost Academy]]'s historical records document three confirmed instances of true name use in the post-Calamity era. The first, in Year 88, involved a Reconstruction-era practitioner who discovered the true name of a minor infernal entity trapped in the [[Hollow Roads]] tunnel network. She used it to bind the entity as a guardian for her community's food stores. The entity served faithfully for twelve years, then the practitioner died, and the binding dissolved. The entity's departure was not violent, but the community relocated within the week. The second instance, in Year 167, involved an Academy researcher who derived a partial true name for a class of arcane construct and used it to safely deactivate a pre-Calamity defense mechanism blocking a tunnel survey. The third instance is classified by the [[Oracles of Might]] and known only through its consequences: a sealed research wing, a permanent ward barrier, and a standing order that no one speak a specific sequence of syllables within the Bellsummit tower.

The Oracles classify all true name research as restricted material — Tier 3 minimum, with certain materials held at an unofficial Tier 4 that does not technically exist in the Academy's classification system. [[Gorduin Wynlar]]'s position is that true name research is the single most dangerous field of arcane study, not because the knowledge is inherently destructive but because the entities most likely to have discoverable true names are the ones most capable of punishing the attempt. The [[Forbidden Sigil Catalog]] contains several sigil patterns that appear to be components of a true-name derivation methodology. These are among the Wynlar Exclusions — redacted from the Academy's copy and held only in the Oracles vault.
