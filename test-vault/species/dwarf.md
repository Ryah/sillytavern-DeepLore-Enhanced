# Dwarf

Dwarves in Duskfrost are the commercial and industrial backbone of the city. They dominate the guilds, run the mining supply chain, and maintain a cultural emphasis on craft, contract, and completion that makes them simultaneously the city's most reliable partners and its most stubborn negotiators.

</div>

Dwarven earth-shaping magic is distinct from the Academic sigil tradition — it operates through direct material contact rather than drawn patterns, and it cannot be taught to those without the bloodline affinity. A skilled dwarf earth-shaper can assess stone quality by touch, identify stress fractures in metal, and perform minor reshaping of raw materials without tools. Master-level practitioners are rare but extraordinary — the [[Marblesmiths]] guild's founders reportedly shaped the guild hall's cornerstone from a single boulder in an afternoon.

The dwarven community centers on guild membership. A dwarf without guild affiliation is socially anomalous — not outcast, but visibly incomplete, like a sentence missing its verb. The [[Marblesmiths]] are the largest dwarven guild, but the metalworkers, brewers, and mining cooperatives all maintain significant dwarven membership.

Dwarven culture values the contract — spoken or written — as the fundamental unit of social organization. A deal made is a deal kept. A debt owed is a debt paid. This is not mere preference; dwarves consider broken contracts a form of theft, and clan-trade reputations built over centuries can be destroyed by a single default. [[Grolgrurim Bluntgut]]'s four-thousand-crown lawsuit is not eccentricity. It is cultural obligation.

Their relationship with elves is one of mutual professional respect and mutual social incomprehension. Elves think in centuries and speak in careful ambiguities. Dwarves think in contracts and speak in specifics. They work together productively because both species understand the value of patience, even if they define patience differently.

Dwarves are concentrated in [[Amberburgh]] and [[Scorchhelm]], with smaller communities in every district except [[Rimeborough]] (too cold, insufficient commercial density) and the Zuvine harbor settlement (wrong element entirely).
