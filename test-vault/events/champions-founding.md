# Founding of the Champions of Patience

The [[Champions of Patience]] were established in Year 34 by a coalition of twenty-three former soldiers who refused to attach themselves to any Triumvirate house after the wars of consolidation ended. Their leader, a human shield-bearer named Kael Veldthar, had spent the previous decade fighting in three different house militias and had concluded — correctly, as it turned out — that none of the factions vying for power in post-Calamity Duskfrost had any interest in protecting the people they claimed to govern. He gathered soldiers who shared this assessment and swore them to an oath that has defined the order ever since: service to the civilian population, refusal of political allegiance, and the acceptance that this would make them poor.

The founding oath was specific in ways that have proven both admirable and crippling. Champions may not accept payment for protection. They may not engage in commerce. They may not swear loyalty to any Triumvirate house, any guild, or any faction. They may accept donations and institutional stipends, but they may not solicit them. The oath was designed to ensure absolute neutrality — and it has, at the cost of ensuring the order is perpetually dependent on the goodwill of institutions that would prefer the Champions owed them something. The [[Duskfrost Academy]] provides their largest stipend, a fact that the Academy's political opponents periodically use to question the Champions' neutrality despite three centuries of evidence to the contrary.

The founding twenty-three established their barracks in what would become [[Scorchhelm]], separate from the Triumvirate house compounds by deliberate choice. Their first decade was spent doing unglamorous work — escorting refugees, guarding food stores, breaking up street violence between faction supporters — while the Triumvirate houses fought for territory and influence. This period established the Champions' reputation: they were the people who showed up when no one else would, and they asked for nothing in return except the right to keep showing up.

Kael Veldthar served as the first High Champion for thirty-one years, dying in Year 65 of injuries sustained defending a Highwallow grain store from looters. He is buried in the Champions' barracks courtyard beneath a stone marker that reads only "He waited." The Veldthar family name persists in the order — current High Champion Mira Veldthar is a direct descendant, a fact she considers a responsibility rather than a privilege.
