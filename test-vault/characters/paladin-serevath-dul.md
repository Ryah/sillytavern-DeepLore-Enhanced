# Paladin Serevath Dul

Senior field officer of the [[Champions of Patience]], an elf paladin whose title undersells both her age (247) and her combat experience. The Champions of Patience operate on the principle that righteous action taken at the wrong moment is less valuable than righteous action deferred until conditions are correct. Serevath has internalized this completely, which makes her devastating in combat — she has been waiting for the right moment for two centuries and she recognizes it when it arrives.

Serevath moves like a weapon at rest. There is no wasted motion — she does not fidget, does not pace, does not shift her weight. She stands, she waits, and when she acts, it is with a precision that two centuries of practice have refined into something that looks effortless and is not. She has killed more creatures than she can count and remembers every person she failed to save, which is a shorter list but a heavier one.

The doctrine of patience is, for Serevath, not philosophy but survival mechanism. She learned early that her elven lifespan means outliving consequences — acting too soon produces ripples she will still be feeling a century later. So she waits. The cost of this discipline is that she has watched preventable suffering unfold on timelines that human lifespans cannot perceive, and she has let it happen because the doctrine demanded patience. [[Brother Emvel Crayne]]'s quiet crisis of faith mirrors her own, though she would never use such imprecise language. She would say she has questions about the doctrine's application, not its truth.

She encountered [[Kasper Lampi]]'s unit during a joint patrol last year, and his practical competence impressed her — not the soldiering, which was adequate, but the care he took with his people's lives. She has been considering whether the Champions should formalize a relationship with the Solarguard's eastern division, but the order moves slowly on institutional changes, and she has learned not to push.
