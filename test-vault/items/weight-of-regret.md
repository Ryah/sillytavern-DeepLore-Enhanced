# Weight of Regret

A smooth black stone that becomes heavier — physically, measurably heavier — in proportion to the carrier's strongest regret. A person with no significant regrets finds it weightless. A person with deep guilt finds it immovable. The stone cannot be discarded by its carrier until the regret is resolved or accepted. It has been in continuous circulation in Duskfrost for over a century.

The stone is approximately the size and shape of a river pebble — smooth, oval, slightly warm to the touch regardless of ambient temperature. Its surface is featureless black, absorbing light without reflection, and its measured weight varies from zero grams (in the hands of the genuinely unburdened) to an observed maximum of forty-seven kilograms (recorded during the [[Duskfrost Academy]]'s controlled testing, when a volunteer who had not disclosed the nature of their regret found themselves unable to lift the stone from the table). The weight is genuine — it deflects scales, strains shelves, and on one memorable occasion collapsed a desk drawer that a carrier had casually placed it in. The Academy's physics department has verified that the stone's mass genuinely changes, not merely its perceived weight, which violates conservation of mass in ways that the physics department prefers not to discuss in professional settings.

The "cannot be discarded" property is the stone's most psychologically punishing feature. A carrier who attempts to put the stone down, give it away, throw it, or destroy it finds the stone back in their possession — usually in their pocket, occasionally in their hand, once (reportedly) manifesting inside a locked chest that the carrier had buried in their garden. The return is not instantaneous; the stone typically reappears within an hour of the attempted discard, in a location the carrier will naturally encounter. The only way to pass the stone to another person is for the regret to be "resolved or accepted" — terms the stone appears to define for itself. Several carriers have reported the stone suddenly becoming weightless and transferable after experiences they describe variously as confession, confrontation, or simply giving up the fight against their own guilt. The stone then finds its way to a new carrier, through mechanisms that appear coincidental but statistically are not.

It was found in the same excavation site as the [[Angel Crown]], suggesting a shared origin, and the two artifacts share several properties: construction from unidentifiable materials, apparent autonomous behavior, and a focus on the carrier's inner state rather than the external world. Where the Crown warps probability and perception, the Weight warps mass in proportion to conscience. If the same maker created both, the maker's interest was not in power or utility but in the relationship between objects and the people who hold them. The stone is currently in the Archive's physical artifact collection, stored alongside the [[Heartwood Staff]] in a shelf that has been reinforced twice. Its current measured weight — four kilograms — is attributed to the ambient regret of the building itself, which is either a joke by the archivists or a deeply unsettling observation about the emotional residue of a four-century-old institution that stores dangerous secrets for a living.
