# Great Flood of Highwallow

In Year 234, eleven days of unbroken rainfall combined with ongoing Scar-zone ground subsidence to produce catastrophic flooding across the lower three-quarters of [[Highwallow]]. The Resonance Scar had been slowly destabilizing the district's bedrock for over two centuries, creating a network of subsurface voids that the rain filled and then collapsed. Streets that had been stable in the morning became sinkholes by afternoon. Entire blocks of tenement housing, already structurally questionable, dropped into the mud.

Approximately four hundred residents died — drowned, crushed in collapses, or killed by the toxic runoff that the Scar zone produces when saturated. Thousands more were displaced into [[Bayside Shushail]] and the lower reaches of [[Bellsummit]], where they were received with the enthusiasm typically reserved for contagious disease. The [[Champions of Patience]] organized the only effective evacuation effort, deploying their entire active roster of forty-two members to pull survivors from flooded buildings for seventy-two continuous hours. Three Champions died in the effort. The [[Solarguard]], by contrast, sealed the district perimeter to prevent "disorder from spreading" — a decision that Commander Castellan's predecessor defended publicly and, according to reliable accounts, regretted privately for the rest of his career.

The Triumvirate's response arrived eight days after the flooding began. Emergency funds were allocated, reconstruction was authorized, and a commission was established to study Scar-zone geological stability. The commission produced its report in Year 237. The report recommended evacuating all residents within four blocks of the Scar perimeter. The Triumvirate read the report, noted that implementing the recommendation would require relocating approximately 6,000 people at enormous expense, and filed it. The residents were not relocated. The subsidence continues.

The flood permanently lowered the elevation of Highwallow's southern quarter by approximately two meters, creating the semi-permanent marsh conditions that now define the district's character. It also permanently lowered whatever trust Highwallow's residents had placed in Triumvirate governance, which was not substantial to begin with. The [[Bonehands]], who operated an informal but effective relief operation during the crisis — distributing food, providing shelter, asking for nothing immediately in return — significantly expanded their influence in the district as a result. The debt was collected later, quietly, and over decades.
