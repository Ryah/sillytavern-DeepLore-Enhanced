# Echo Lens

A monocle-sized lens that, when pressed against a surface, replays the last significant visual event experienced by that surface — specifically, the last thing that moved past it rapidly. Useful for crime scene reconstruction. Limited to approximately a 48-hour lookback window. Evidence obtained via Echo Lens is inadmissible in [[Duskfrost Triumvirate]] courts.

The lens is a disc of clear material — not glass, not crystal, not any substance the [[Duskfrost Academy]] can identify — approximately four centimeters in diameter and slightly concave on one side. When the concave side is pressed firmly against any solid surface, the user sees, overlaid on their normal vision, a ghostly replay of the last rapid-motion event the surface "witnessed." The replay is silent, monochromatic, and lasts between three and fifteen seconds. The definition of "rapid" appears to be relative: a wall near a street shows passing foot traffic, while a wall in a quiet room might show a door opening. The lens prioritizes violence — a surface that has witnessed both a passing cart and a stabbing within the 48-hour window will show the stabbing. Whether the lens is detecting physical impact energy, emotional intensity, or something else entirely is debated.

The inadmissibility ruling dates to Year 273, when a magistrate excluded Echo Lens evidence on the grounds that the device's mechanism was not understood, its selectivity criteria could not be verified, and the replay could not be independently corroborated. The ruling was pragmatic rather than principled: the magistrate privately noted that admitting evidence from a device no one could explain would open the courts to challenges from every charlatan with a mysterious object. The [[Wardenship]] uses the lens informally — crime scene investigators consult it for leads, then build their cases through conventional evidence. This is technically a procedural violation. No one has raised the issue, because the alternative is not using the one tool that can show you who was at a murder scene forty hours ago.

The only known working example is held by a private collector in [[Bellsummit]] who loans it to the Wardenship on request in exchange for unspecified considerations that neither party discusses. The Academy has examined the lens twice and produced two contradictory reports: the first concluded it operates on a resonance-memory principle related to [[Speaking Stone]] technology, the second concluded it operates on a principle with no known analog. A second lens was reportedly recovered from a [[Hollow Roads]] excavation in Year 306 but was damaged during extraction and has not functioned. The Academy is attempting repair. The private collector has offered to purchase the damaged lens "for study purposes" at a price that suggests the collector knows something about Echo Lens technology that the Academy does not.
