# Marblesmiths

The Marblesmiths guild controls most licensed construction work in Duskfrost, with a particular monopoly on stone and masonry. The guild is majority-[[Cirilae]] by membership — roughly seventy percent — a demographic reality that shapes everything from internal politics to contract negotiations. Their guildhouse occupies a solid three-story building on the western edge of [[Amberburgh]], built from the same grey granite they quarry from the cliffs north of the city.

Guild politics run along species lines with a thin veneer of professionalism. The Cirilae majority dominates the master-rank positions, controls apprenticeship admissions, and sets the contract bidding rules in ways that — while technically neutral — consistently favor Cirilae-owned firms. Non-Cirilae members, mostly [[Human]] and [[Dwarf]] masons, have petitioned the [[Duskfrost Triumvirate]] twice in the last decade for oversight of guild admissions. Both petitions were acknowledged, filed, and forgotten, because the Triumvirate needs the Marblesmiths more than the Marblesmiths need the Triumvirate. Guildmaster Torvasse Ingren maintains that the guild's practices are merit-based, which is true in the narrow sense that Cirilae stonemasons are genuinely excellent, and false in the broader sense that merit has a way of being defined by the people already in charge.

The Marblesmiths trace their founding to Year 48 of the Reconstruction, when the sheer scale of rebuilding after the [[The Calamity Onset|Calamity]] required organized labor coordination. That legacy gives them a civic weight that transcends their commercial function — the guild literally rebuilt the city, and they have never let anyone forget it. Major current projects include the ongoing reinforcement of [[Highwallow]]'s flood barriers (perpetually underfunded, perpetually critical), expansion of the [[Amberburgh Exchange]] market hall, and the politically sensitive restoration of the [[Scorchhelm]] east wall, which requires working alongside the [[Solarguard]] in a fortress district where construction delays are treated as security concerns.

The guild's internal tension centers on Highwallow contracts. Cirilae guildmasters consider marsh-district work low-prestige and low-margin, and consistently underbid it to non-Cirilae subcontractors. The subcontractors, in turn, cut corners because the margins are impossible. The result is that Highwallow's infrastructure — flood walls, drainage channels, foundation repairs — is maintained by the people least equipped to do it properly, while the guild's best talent builds market halls in Amberburgh. Guildmaster Ingren is aware this is unsustainable. She has not yet found a way to make it someone else's problem.
