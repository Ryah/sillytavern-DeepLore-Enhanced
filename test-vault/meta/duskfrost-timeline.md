# Duskfrost Timeline

## Pre-Calamity (Before Year 0)

The civilization that preceded Duskfrost is referred to collectively as the Antecedent Culture by modern scholars, though this name covers what was likely a dozen or more distinct societies linked by shared arcane infrastructure. What little survives — fragments of the Pallasys Translations, architectural remnants beneath [[Scorchhelm]] and [[Highwallow]], and a handful of functioning artifacts in the Archive's restricted section — suggests a civilization of extraordinary arcane sophistication.

The Antecedent Culture apparently solved the problem of large-scale arcane energy distribution using a network of resonance towers. These towers, the foundations of which still exist beneath [[Bellsummit]] and the old harbor, channeled ambient arcane energy from deep ley-line nodes and distributed it through a city-wide grid that powered everything from public lighting to military fortifications. The existence of this grid meant that arcane practice was not limited to those with bloodline affinity — anyone who learned the correct sigil protocols could tap the grid for minor effects.

This democratization of magic appears to have had profound social consequences, though scholars disagree on their nature. Some argue it created a relatively egalitarian society. Others point to evidence of a rigid caste system based not on birth but on the complexity of sigils one could work, with grid-tapping peasants at the bottom and full resonance-shapers at the top.

## The Calamity (Year 0)

The Calamity is poorly understood even now, three centuries later. The most widely accepted theory holds that the resonance tower network experienced a cascading failure — one tower destabilized, which caused a resonant overload in adjacent towers, which caused further destabilizations in an exponentially growing chain reaction. Within hours, the entire network had collapsed, releasing energy equivalent to what modern theorists estimate as several hundred simultaneous high-tier binding failures.

The physical effects were catastrophic. Large portions of what is now Highwallow simply sank into the marsh as the arcane supports maintaining the ground stability failed. The harbor was reshaped by a series of massive waves. Fires burned for weeks. Perhaps one in three residents of the proto-city survived.

The arcane effects were, if anything, worse. The collapse created what scholars call the Resonance Scar — a persistent low-level arcane turbulence that still affects magic in certain parts of the city, particularly in Highwallow. Sigils drawn in the Scar zone have a measurable chance of behaving unexpectedly, which is one of several reasons why the Academy refuses to officially recognize practitioners from that district.

Several of the original resonance towers survived partially intact. The Academy has spent centuries studying them. Three remain structurally sound enough to enter. What the researchers have found inside them is classified at the Archive's highest restriction level.

## Reconstruction (Years 1-200)

The two centuries following the Calamity were characterized by survival and slow rebuilding. The early settlement that would become Duskfrost proper coalesced around the ruins of the old harbor, which remained functional despite the wave damage. Fishing and coastal trade kept the population alive through the first brutal decades.

The three families that would eventually form the Triumvirate — the Harcyne lineage, the Velkhast lineage, and the Morveth lineage — rose to prominence during this period through a combination of genuine organizational competence and ruthless elimination of rivals. Each controlled a different critical resource: the Harcynes dominated food production and distribution, the Velkhasts controlled the harbor and trade routes, and the Morveths held a monopoly on functional arcane knowledge (most of the surviving pre-Calamity scholars had attached themselves to Morveth protection in exchange for resources).

The Academy was formally founded in Year 147 as a compromise institution — a jointly governed body that gave all three families nominal oversight while in practice creating an independent scholarly power base. This arrangement has been renegotiated, fought over, and re-established at least seven times since its founding.

## Medieval Era (Years 200-Present)

The medieval era saw Duskfrost stabilize into its current form: a city of approximately 80,000 residents, governed by the Triumvirate under a complex web of charters, covenants, and tacit agreements, with the Academy serving as both a fourth power and a moderating influence.

The major guilds — the [[Marblesmiths]], the [[Mages of the Patient]], the [[Champions of Patience]], and others — formed during this period, each carving out economic and social territory under the protection of one or more Triumvirate houses. The [[Solarguard]] was established as the official city watch in Year 243, though its relationship with the [[Bonehands]] thieves' organization has been complicated from the beginning.

The most significant medieval-era conflict was the [[War of the Broken Mountain]] (Year 289), in which the Harcyne house attempted to seize direct control of the Academy following the death of the previous Archmage. The war lasted four years and ended with a negotiated settlement that further constrained Triumvirate influence over the Academy while giving the Harcynes several significant trade concessions.

## Renaissance Period (Years ~340-Present)

The current renaissance period is defined by two overlapping crises: the discovery of pre-Calamity resonance technology that may be reactivatable, and a succession dispute within the Velkhast house that has destabilized the Triumvirate balance.

The resonance discovery was made by a junior Academy researcher who stumbled onto a functional fragment of the original grid beneath the [[Rappiop Grove]] district. The Academy has suppressed public knowledge of this discovery, but word has leaked to all three Triumvirate houses, each of which is now quietly maneuvering to control whatever emerges from the subsequent research.

The Velkhast succession dispute arose from the death of the previous house head with no clear legitimate heir. Three claimants exist, each supported by different factions within the city. The Solarguard has quietly aligned with one claimant. The Bonehands have aligned with another. The third claimant has disappeared, which most observers interpret as assassination rather than withdrawal.

Against this backdrop, ordinary life in Duskfrost continues more or less as it always has — which is to say, at a low level of organized chaos that the city's residents have long since stopped noticing.
