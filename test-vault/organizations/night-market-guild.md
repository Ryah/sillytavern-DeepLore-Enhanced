# Night Market Guild

The Night Market Guild is an informal trade organization managing the unlicensed night market in [[Amberburgh]]. Majority-[[Orvathi]] operated, though "operated" implies a level of formal structure that would make the actual organizers laugh. The city watch maintains a deliberate blind spot about their operating license status in exchange for the market's cooperation with crime reporting — a gentleman's agreement between people who are not, strictly speaking, gentlemen.

The market sets up every evening in the Lower Terrace alleys behind the [[Amberburgh Exchange]], where the permanent market stalls close at sundown and the real commerce begins. Orvathi vendors — who make up roughly two-thirds of the stalls — bring the distinctive sensory character: braziers of spiced coal that turn the alley air sweet and acrid, strings of bioluminescent kelp lanterns imported from coastal [[Orvathi]] settlements, and the low rhythmic chanting that Orvathi merchants use instead of shouting prices. The remaining third are a mix of everyone else who can't afford [[Amberburgh Merchants League]] licensing fees or doesn't want their inventory examined too carefully. Goods range from the entirely legitimate (street food, mended clothing, salvaged tools) to the technically illegal (unlicensed alchemical preparations, resonance-touched curiosities from [[Highwallow]], information) to the genuinely dangerous (occasionally, and the Guild's enforcers shut those stalls down fast because nothing kills a market like a [[Wardenship]] raid).

The arrangement with the [[Solarguard]] is practical rather than principled. The six watch officers who patrol Amberburgh's evening shift know the market exists, know it is unlicensed, and know that reporting it would create paperwork, political complications with the Orvathi community, and the loss of the market's single most useful function: its vendors see everything and will share what they see with officers who don't ask inconvenient questions about licensing. Reshva Orvathi-Keln, the market's unofficial coordinator, manages this relationship personally. She keeps a mental ledger of which officers prefer coin, which prefer information, and which simply prefer being left alone with a cup of spiced wine on cold nights.

The Guild's tension is existential. The [[Amberburgh Merchants League]] has been pushing the Triumvirate to enforce licensing in the Lower Terrace for three years, framing it as a public safety issue rather than the competitive threat it actually is. The Orvathi community views the night market as cultural infrastructure — not just commerce but a gathering space, a social institution, the closest thing to a community hall they have in a district that wasn't built for them. If the League succeeds, the market doesn't just close. It takes a piece of Orvathi life in Duskfrost with it. Reshva knows this. She is not the type to let it happen quietly.
