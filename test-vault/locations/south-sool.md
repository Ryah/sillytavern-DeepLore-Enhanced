# South Sool District

South Sool is a residential district of modest townhouses, small shops, and the kind of quiet that comes from being neither poor enough for drama nor rich enough for politics. Its residents commute to [[Amberburgh]] for work and return for sleep. Nothing significant has happened in South Sool in thirty years.

The district's streets are clean, its buildings maintained to the standard of people who take pride in their homes without having the budget to renovate them. Stone facades with painted shutters, flower boxes in season, and front stoops where neighbors sit in the evening trading gossip that is, by Duskfrost standards, aggressively mundane. The shops — a baker, a greengrocer, a cobbler, two taverns that compete on atmosphere rather than price — serve the immediate neighborhood and close early. The smell of South Sool is bread in the morning, chimney smoke in the evening, and the faint dusty scent of sun-warmed stone in between. It is the kind of place that exists to be lived in quietly, and it fulfills that purpose without ambition or apology.

The district's population is mixed by species but uniform by class: working families, retired tradespeople, young professionals saving for something better, and elderly residents who remember when South Sool was considered the edge of the city and prefer it that way. The [[Solarguard]] patrols here infrequently because there is nothing to patrol for. The crime rate is low not because of enforcement but because the return on criminal activity in a district of modest means does not justify the effort. The most heated dispute in recent memory involved a property line between two gardens, which was resolved by a surveyor and three months of neighbors choosing not to speak to each other.

South Sool's greatest asset and greatest limitation are the same thing: it is boring. Young people leave for [[Amberburgh]]'s opportunities. Families arrive for the affordable rent and the schools. The cycle has repeated for decades without altering the district's essential character, which is the character of a place that has decided exactly what it wants to be and has no interest in becoming anything else.
