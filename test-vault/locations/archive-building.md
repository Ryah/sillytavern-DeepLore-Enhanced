# The Archive Building

The Duskfrost Archive occupies three city blocks in [[Bellsummit]]'s institutional quarter — a chaotic collection of architectural styles representing seven construction phases spread across three centuries of additions. The oldest wing is dark basalt, the newest is pale limestone, and between them lies a progression of compromises that no architect would willingly claim. The public collection fills the ground floor. Restricted materials occupy the upper levels. The Black Shelf is somewhere above that, in a section that does not appear on any publicly available floor plan.

The ground floor is open to any resident of Duskfrost, and the smell of it is unmistakable: old paper, binding glue, dust that has had generations to settle into the stone, and the faint chemical tang of the preservation wards that keep the oldest materials from crumbling. Long reading tables fill the central halls, scarred and ink-stained, occupied by a permanent population of students, researchers, and elderly residents who come for the quiet and the warmth. The shelves extend in rows that seem to violate the building's external dimensions — a quirk of the third construction phase that the Academy has studied and the archivists consider unremarkable. Finding anything in the public collection without assistance requires either a working knowledge of the cataloguing system or three free hours, and the cataloguing system was last fully reorganized during Head Archivist Brimstone Kettleback's first year, thirty-one years ago.

Kettleback runs the Archive with the possessive authority of someone who has outlasted six Academy Archmages and considers the institution more permanent than any of them. His staff of fourteen archivists and twenty-three apprentices maintain the collection, process acquisitions, and manage access to the restricted floors with a bureaucratic thoroughness that the [[Solarguard]] openly envies. Access above the ground floor requires written authorization from a department head, a stated research purpose, and the patience to wait while an archivist verifies both. The restricted sections contain original historical documents, pre-Compact records, and arcane texts that the [[Duskfrost Academy]] considers too sensitive for general circulation. The Black Shelf — whose existence the Archive neither confirms nor denies — allegedly holds materials that even the Archmage cannot access without Triumvirate authorization.

The [[Oracles of Might]] maintain that their private collection in the [[Oracles of Might Tower|tower]] surpasses the Archive's holdings. Kettleback has been heard to respond to this claim with a single word that archivists are not supposed to use in professional settings.
