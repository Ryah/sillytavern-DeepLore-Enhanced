# Divine Tiara

A tiara of silver and pale blue stone that confers minor blessings on the wearer — slightly improved luck, marginally better health, and a persistent sense of being watched benevolently. Current whereabouts unknown; last seen in the [[Champions of Patience]] chapel before their relocation.

The blessings are subtle enough to be dismissed as placebo by skeptics and consistent enough to resist that dismissal under controlled testing. The [[Duskfrost Academy]] conducted a blind trial in Year 284, during the tiara's last period of institutional custody: twenty subjects wore the tiara for one week each, without being told what effects to expect. Eighteen reported improved sleep quality. Fifteen reported that minor injuries (cuts, bruises, headaches) healed faster than usual. Twelve reported a subjective sense of "rightness" — a feeling that things were going slightly better than they should. And all twenty, without exception, reported the sensation of being observed by something benevolent. The Academy's psychology department attributed this last effect to the tiara's design aesthetic (the blue stones, the delicate silver work, the general appearance of something that ought to belong to something holy). The Academy's arcane research department noted that the tiara emits a resonance pattern unlike any documented sigil but structurally similar to the [[Angel Crown]]'s probability-warping field — similar in kind, vastly smaller in magnitude.

Some scholars believe it is a lesser artifact from the same maker as the Angel Crown, and the structural resonance similarity supports this. If correct, the tiara and the Crown represent two points on a spectrum of the same technology — the Crown operating at a scale that drives its holders mad, the tiara operating at a scale that makes them slightly happier. The question of whether the maker intended this difference or whether the tiara is simply a degraded version of the Crown's full capability has not been resolved.

The tiara disappeared from the Champions of Patience chapel during their relocation from [[Bellsummit]] to their current site outside the city walls. The Champions' official statement is that the tiara was "entrusted to a worthy keeper" and that its location is "known to those who need to know." Pressed for details, the Champions' Prior has said only that the tiara went where it wanted to go. The [[Wardenship]] investigated the disappearance as a theft, found no evidence of forced entry or unauthorized access, and closed the case as "unresolved, no further leads." [[Gorduin Wynlar]] has expressed concern that the tiara, like the Angel Crown, may exhibit autonomous behavior — moving itself to locations of its own choosing. If the two artifacts share a maker, and if that maker built self-directing capability into the Crown, there is no reason to assume the tiara lacks it.
