# Erlathan Ercyne

Elf historian attached to the Triumvirate's cultural records office, specializing in pre-Compact territorial boundaries and the disputes that led to the current administrative arrangement. He is politely requested to be present at most significant territorial negotiations, and politely ignored at approximately half of them. He keeps notes on which half.

Erlathan has the elf's curse of outliving his own relevance. He remembers the political conditions that produced the Binding Compact because he was alive for them — not as a participant, but as a junior scholar recording proceedings that the participants considered temporary. Three centuries later, those temporary arrangements are foundational law, and the only living person who remembers what was said informally in the margins of those negotiations is an archivist that politicians invite for decorative historical credibility.

His current research focuses on territorial disputes predating the [[Binding Compact]], and what he has found disturbs him. The current political tensions between the districts follow a pattern that is structurally identical to the pre-Compact disputes, which means either history is repeating through coincidence or someone is deliberately reconstructing the conditions that made the Compact necessary. He has written a briefing document to this effect. It was received, acknowledged, and shelved.

He shares his concerns occasionally with [[Sarros Sylren]], who is studying the same period from the military history angle. They have reached similar conclusions independently, which Erlathan finds validating and terrifying in equal measure. He is too old to be afraid for himself, but he is very afraid for the city.
