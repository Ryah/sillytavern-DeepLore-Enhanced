# Bellsummit District Council

The Bellsummit District Council manages local administration for the high district — street maintenance, licensing, minor disputes, and the general business of ensuring that [[Bellsummit]]'s cobblestones remain cleaner and its lamp-oil better funded than anywhere else in Duskfrost. Technically subordinate to the [[Duskfrost Triumvirate]]; in practice, the Council does what it wants because its members are all Triumvirate-adjacent nobility who can make a single dinner party more politically consequential than a month of official proceedings.

The seven councillors are drawn from Bellsummit's landed families — minor nobility, successful merchants who married into names, and the occasional Academy administrator who retired to the heights with enough social capital to earn a seat. Lord Alderman Cauvren Harcyne-Moss, a distant cousin of the Harcyne main line, chairs the Council with the practiced ease of someone who has never had to worry about whether dinner would be on the table. Three current councillors maintain active membership in the [[Academy Alumni Fellowship]], which gives the Council an informal channel to the Academy administration that bypasses the Triumvirate entirely. This arrangement benefits both parties and irritates the Triumvirate, which is an additional benefit neither party acknowledges.

The Council's actual power is soft but real. They control Bellsummit's licensing fees, which means they control who opens businesses in the wealthiest district. They manage the district's private security supplement — a polite term for the additional [[Solarguard]] patrols that Bellsummit's residents fund directly, ensuring that the high district receives approximately three times the watch coverage of [[Highwallow]] for approximately one-tenth the crime. They arbitrate property disputes among families who would rather settle quietly than involve the Triumvirate's formal courts.

The Council's tension is the same tension that runs through all of Bellsummit: the Velkhast succession dispute. Two of the seven councillors have financial ties to the first claimant; one is married to a supporter of the second. The remaining four are performing elaborate neutrality while privately preparing for whichever outcome protects their property values. Council meetings have become longer, less productive, and more carefully worded. Lord Alderman Harcyne-Moss manages this by ensuring the wine is excellent and the agenda is confined to street repair schedules, which is the governance equivalent of pretending the house is not on fire by discussing the curtains.
