# Rimeborough

A northern clifftop district kept cold by persistent sea winds that funnel through the cliff channels with enough force to make exposed skin ache within minutes. The [[Delivese]] community has settled here in significant numbers, finding the temperature comfortable. Other species find it unpleasant. The district is quiet, clean, and largely self-sufficient.

Rimeborough's architecture reflects its climate and its primary residents. Buildings are constructed from heavy dark stone with thick walls, small windows, and doors that seal against the wind with the precision of ship hatches. The streets are wide — wind-management, not aesthetics — and kept scrupulously clean by a community that considers clutter a moral failing. Delivese residences are identifiable by their blue-painted door frames, a cultural tradition whose significance the Delivese explain differently depending on who is asking and how much they respect the questioner. Non-Delivese residents, a small minority, live in the district's southern blocks where the wind is partially broken by the terrain, and their buildings are noticeably more insulated, with the thermal wraps and alchemical heating systems that the [[Rimeborough Cold Market]] sells in quantity.

The district's self-sufficiency is not ideological but practical. Rimeborough produces its own food — cold-climate crops grown in sheltered terraces, livestock breeds adapted to the temperature, and preserved goods that the Delivese manufacture with a skill that comes from cultural memory of environments far harsher than this. The [[Rimeborough Cold Market]] handles most internal commerce. The Delivese maintain their own dispute resolution processes, their own cultural institutions, and their own quiet understanding with the [[Solarguard]] that amounts to: we will handle our own affairs, and you are welcome to patrol our streets if you dress warmly enough.

Outsiders who visit Rimeborough — merchants, Solarguard officers on rotation, the occasional [[Duskfrost Academy]] researcher studying Delivese cold-adaptation — describe it as eerie. The combination of silence, cleanliness, and cold produces a district that feels uninhabited until you notice the faces in the windows, watching you pass with an attention that is not hostile but is unmistakably evaluative. The Delivese are slow to trust, slower to share, and entirely willing to wait out anyone who arrives with expectations of quick rapport. Rimeborough rewards patience. It has no use for anything else.
