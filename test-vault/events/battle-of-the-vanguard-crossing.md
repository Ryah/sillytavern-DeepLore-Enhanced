# Battle of the Vanguard Crossing

The Battle of the Vanguard Crossing (Year 67) was the decisive conflict of the early Reconstruction era, fought over control of the primary bridge connecting the upper cliff settlements to the harbor district below.

The proto-Triumvirate coalition — the three families that would eventually formalize their power-sharing as the Triumvirate — faced a rival faction of harbor merchants who controlled the bridge and the trade flowing through it. The battle lasted three days and ended with the bridge in Triumvirate hands.

The harbor merchants who survived were incorporated as the founding membership of what eventually became the [[Amberburgh Merchants League]] — a negotiated peace that gave the merchants formal recognition in exchange for accepting Triumvirate governance. This pattern of co-opting defeated rivals would define Triumvirate political strategy for the next three centuries.

The bridge itself was destroyed in Year 289 during the [[War of the Broken Mountain]] and rebuilt in its current form (wider, with incorporated defensive positions) shortly thereafter.

Historians cite the [[Binding Compact]] as context for this event.
