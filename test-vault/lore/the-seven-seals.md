# The Seven Seals

The Seven Seals — referred to in some [[Thornwick Pallasys|Pallasys]] translations as the Seventeen Seals, a discrepancy that has not been resolved — are pre-Calamity arcane constructs placed at what appear to be key nodes in the resonance network. Their purpose is not fully understood; the translations describe them variously as "locks," "governors," and "the memory of the agreement." At least two Seals are believed to be located within or near Duskfrost. Neither has been found. The [[Oracles of Might]] consider this a priority research area and do not discuss it publicly.

The seven-versus-seventeen discrepancy is not a translation error in the conventional sense. The [[Antecedent Script]] character for the number is unambiguous in its base form — it clearly means "seven." But the compound character used in the longer administrative documents includes a modifier that Pallasys read as "complete set" and later translators have read as "active subset." If the second reading is correct, the Seals number seventeen total, of which seven were active at any given time. This interpretation implies the Seals could be rotated or swapped, which implies they were maintained, which implies someone or something was responsible for maintaining them. Whether that maintenance function survived the Calamity is a question that has kept [[Gorduin Wynlar]] awake for the better part of two decades.

The translations' descriptions of the Seals' function are contradictory in ways that may be informative rather than confusing. "Locks" suggests containment — the Seals hold something in place or prevent something from acting. "Governors" suggests regulation — the Seals moderate a process, keeping it within acceptable parameters. "The memory of the agreement" suggests something else entirely: that the Seals encode the terms of an arrangement between parties, functioning as the arcane equivalent of a [[Blood Binding Contracts|blood-binding contract]] at civilizational scale. If this last interpretation is correct, the Seals are not infrastructure but law — the physical manifestation of a compact between the six (or more) populations of the [[Pre-Calamity Species Records|Antecedent Culture]], enforcing cooperation through consequences rather than trust. The Calamity, under this reading, was what happened when the compact was violated.

The two Seals believed to be near Duskfrost have been the subject of systematic but secret searches by the Oracles for over a century. The search is complicated by not knowing what a Seal looks like — the translations describe them as "stones that remember" and "the weight of the word," which could describe anything from a mountain to a pebble. The [[Resonance Frequency Theory|resonance frequency]] work suggests the Seals would emit a distinctive arcane signature, but the [[Resonance Scar Properties|Resonance Scar]]'s broadband noise drowns out any signal within its radius, and one of the two Seals is believed to be located within or beneath the Scar itself. The second is believed to be somewhere in the [[Hollow Roads]] tunnel network, based on a set of [[Antecedent Script]] directional markers that Gorduin translated personally and has not shared with the broader research community. His notes on the translation are stored with the [[Forbidden Sigil Catalog]] materials in the Oracles vault, behind wards that he resets monthly.
