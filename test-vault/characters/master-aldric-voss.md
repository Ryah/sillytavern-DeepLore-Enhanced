# Master Aldric Voss

Master of the [[Amberburgh]] Merchant Consortium — the coordinating body above individual guilds that manages inter-guild disputes, sets common trade standards, and represents commercial interests to the Triumvirate administration. He has held this role for twelve years through the force of his relationships, his memory for favors, and his willingness to absorb short-term losses for long-term positioning. He and [[Grolgrurim Bluntgut]] have a productive working relationship that neither would describe as friendly.

Aldric did not inherit his position — he built it by being the person everyone owed a favor to. He started as a textile factor's apprentice, moved into commodity brokering, and spent two decades constructing a web of obligations that made his appointment to the Consortium chair feel inevitable rather than contested. He understands that power in Amberburgh is not held by those who make money but by those who decide how money moves, and he has positioned himself at every significant junction.

What Aldric conceals behind the professional charm is a growing anxiety about the Consortium's relevance. The [[Bonehands]] are moving into legitimate trade channels in ways that undermine guild pricing structures, and the Triumvirate's regulatory response has been sluggish enough to suggest either incompetence or complicity. He has raised this concern in three formal sessions and been assured that the situation is being monitored. He finds this assurance inadequate but has not yet decided whether to escalate publicly, because public escalation would reveal how much he knows about Bonehands commercial activity, and that knowledge was not acquired through official channels.

His relationship with [[Grolgrurim Bluntgut]] is the closest thing he has to an alliance — Grolgrurim provides blunt honesty, and Aldric provides political navigation, and between them they have prevented four trade disputes from becoming guild wars. [[Yseult Marchant]] occasionally provides him with intelligence from her foreign trading contacts, and he compensates her with favorable berth allocations, which is technically an abuse of his position and practically essential to Amberburgh's foreign trade.
