# Archmage Tessavel Orindal

Tessavel Orindal holds two titles that should not be held by the same person, and has held them simultaneously for twenty-three years without anyone finding a legal mechanism to prevent it. She is Archmage of the [[Duskfrost Academy]] and Grand Oracle of the [[Oracles of Might]], which means she controls both the institution that regulates arcane practice and the institution that pushes its boundaries. Whether this represents checks and balances or their deliberate elimination is the central question of Duskfrost's current arcane politics.

She is, by any honest assessment, brilliant. She maintained the Academy's independence through four Triumvirate power shifts by being indispensable to all three houses while beholden to none. Her binding magic is among the strongest in living memory — she once sealed a ruptured ward in the Academy's restricted section by improvising a containment sigil from memory while the researchers around her were still evacuating. The seal has held for nineteen years.

Her relationship with the Triumvirate is one of calculated opacity. [[Duskfrost Triumvirate|House Morveth]] considers her an ally. [[Duskfrost Triumvirate|House Harcyne]] considers her a necessary evil. [[Duskfrost Triumvirate|House Velkhast]] — what remains of its functional leadership — considers her the only person who might stabilize the [[Velkhast Succession Crisis]] and resents her for not doing so.

She works through people. [[Gorduin Wynlar]] runs the Oracles' day-to-day operations. [[Professor Taevis Wrent]] handles Academy curriculum. Tessavel handles the things that require someone who can say no to a Triumvirate house head and make it stick. She says no frequently, precisely, and without raising her voice.

Her ninety years are visible only in the patience with which she handles crises — she has seen enough of them to know which ones resolve themselves and which ones require intervention. She intervenes rarely. When she does, the intervention is decisive and often irreversible, which is why people pay attention to the rare occasions when she stands up from her desk.
