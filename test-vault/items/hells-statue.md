# Hell's Statue

A half-meter obsidian statue of unclear subject that radiates low-level infernal energy detectable to [[Selaveth]] and sensitive arcane instruments. Recovered from the deep [[Highwallow]] tunnels. Currently sealed in a lead-lined container in the Archive's restricted storage. The [[Duskfrost Academy]] is not sure what to do with it.

The statue depicts a figure that defies consistent description — viewers report seeing different things depending on angle, lighting, and, disconcertingly, their emotional state. The most common descriptions include "a robed figure with too many arms," "a seated animal of no identifiable species," and "something geometric that looks like it should be organic." The Academy's documentation includes twelve sketches by different observers, no two of which resemble each other. Photographic capture via alchemical plates produces a uniform black shape with no detail, as though the statue absorbs the recording process. The obsidian itself is genuine — volcanic glass, chemically identical to samples from three known quarry sites — but its surface temperature is consistently four degrees warmer than ambient, with no internal heat source detectable by any instrument.

The infernal energy signature is the statue's most concerning property. Selaveth who approach within five meters report a sensation they describe as "recognition" — not their own recognition of the statue, but the statue's recognition of them. This distinction matters because the Selaveth are not frightened by much, and this frightens them. The Academy's demonology consultant (a position that exists but is never listed in the official faculty directory) has measured the statue's energy output and classified it as consistent with a "dormant binding vessel" — an object designed to contain an infernal entity in stasis. Whether the vessel is currently occupied is the question the Academy cannot answer and does not want to test.

The statue was recovered in Year 298 from a section of the [[Resonance Scar Tunnels|Scar tunnels]] that the survey team described as "architecturally distinct" — the walls carved with deliberate precision into patterns that the team's sigil specialist recognized as containment geometry. The statue sat at the center of a raised platform, surrounded by a ring of [[Antecedent Script]] characters that [[Gorduin Wynlar]] later translated as either "do not move this" or "this cannot be moved," depending on tense. The survey team moved it anyway, which Gorduin has described as "the most optimistic reading of ambiguous instructions I have ever encountered." The lead-lined container was his suggestion, on the theory that if the statue contains something, the lead might slow its communication with whatever it communicates with. The container sits in the Archive's deepest storage level. The room it occupies is checked daily. Nothing has changed. Nothing has changed for twenty years. The Academy's position is that this is good news. Gorduin's position is that twenty years is not a meaningful timeframe for something that may have been sitting in that tunnel since before the Calamity.
