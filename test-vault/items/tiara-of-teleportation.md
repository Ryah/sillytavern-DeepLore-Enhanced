# Tiara of Teleportation

A delicate tiara that enables short-range spatial displacement — up to approximately 50 meters, line of sight only. Requires a rest period of approximately ten minutes between uses. Currently missing from the [[Oracles of Might]] vault where it was last catalogued three years ago.

The tiara is a slender band of silver-white metal (unidentifiable, consistent with the [[Angel Crown]]'s composition) set with three small clear stones that glow faintly when the displacement effect is available. When the stones go dark — immediately after use — the tiara enters its rest period, during which it is a piece of jewelry and nothing more. The displacement itself is instantaneous and silent: the wearer is here, then there, with no visible transition, no sound, and no residual arcane signature at either endpoint. This last property is what makes it dangerous — most teleportation methods, including the [[Duskfrost Academy]]'s experimental spatial sigils, leave detectable traces. The tiara leaves nothing. A person wearing it can appear and disappear from secured locations without triggering ward-based detection systems.

The Academy tested the tiara extensively during its two decades of Oracles custody, documenting its capabilities and limitations. The 50-meter range is absolute — the wearer can visualize destinations further away, but the displacement simply does not occur. Line of sight is required; the wearer must be able to see the destination, not merely know where it is. The rest period varies between eight and fourteen minutes, averaging ten, with no discernible pattern to the variation. The displacement carries the wearer and anything in direct physical contact — clothing, held objects, one documented case of a researcher who displaced while holding another person's hand and brought them along. The passenger experienced severe nausea. The wearer did not. Whether passengers can be transported deliberately or only accidentally has not been tested further, as the volunteer pool dried up after the nausea incident.

The tiara's disappearance from the Oracles vault follows a pattern that [[Gorduin Wynlar]] finds disturbingly familiar. Like the [[Divine Tiara]]'s departure from the Champions of Patience chapel, the teleportation tiara vanished without evidence of unauthorized access: no ward triggers, no lock tampering, no security anomalies of any kind. Gorduin's working theory is that the tiara displaced itself — used its own capability to leave its storage location. This would require the tiara to possess some form of autonomous intent, a property not previously documented but consistent with the behavior of other artifacts from the same apparent manufacturing tradition. The Oracles have not issued a public notice of the loss. Gorduin has added the tiara to a private list he maintains of artifacts that appear to be moving under their own volition, a list that is shorter than he would like and longer than is comfortable.

The most credible recent sighting came six months ago from a [[Salvagers Compact]] diver who reported seeing a silver circlet matching the tiara's description in a submerged chamber near the [[Hollow Roads]] entrance beneath the harbor. The diver was unable to retrieve it — the chamber flooded before she could reach it — but her description of the three clear stones and the faint glow was specific enough that Gorduin considers it worth investigating. The [[Bonehands]] have also shown interest in this lead, which suggests the information has spread further than the Oracles would prefer.
