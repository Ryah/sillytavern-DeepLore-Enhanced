# Sigil Theory — Fundamentals

Sigils function as arcane resonators — geometric patterns that establish a specific harmonic relationship with ambient arcane energy and channel it toward a defined effect. The geometry is not arbitrary: every angle and curve in a working sigil corresponds to a specific [[Resonance Frequency Theory|resonance frequency]]. Errors produce failure or, at high energy levels, catastrophic results.

Foundational coursework at the [[Duskfrost Academy]] covers this material in the first year — six months of theoretical geometry followed by six months of supervised practical work. Students begin with Tier 0 sigils: patterns so simple that even a badly drawn version produces only a faint warmth or a brief glow. The progression is deliberately slow. A Tier 1 sigil (basic kinetic effects, minor heating, simple illumination) requires approximately five hundred hours of practice to draw reliably. A Tier 2 sigil (binding effects, sensory manipulation, defensive wards) requires two to three years of training. The gap between what a student can theoretically understand and what they can reliably execute is where most injuries occur. The Academy's medical wing treats an average of forty sigil-related injuries per academic year, most of them burns. The fatality rate has been zero for eleven consecutive years, which the Academy considers an achievement and which the students' families consider the bare minimum.

The theoretical framework rests on three principles established by [[Thornwick Pallasys]] and refined over four centuries. First, the Resonance Principle: a sigil does not generate energy but selects and amplifies a specific frequency band from the ambient arcane field. Second, the Precision Principle: the relationship between geometry and effect is exact — a deviation of even two degrees in a critical angle produces a different resonance match and therefore a different (or no) effect. Third, the Cascade Principle: complex effects require multiple resonance frequencies engaged simultaneously, which means complex sigils are actually overlapping simple sigils whose interactions produce emergent behavior. This third principle is where the theory gets dangerous, because emergent behavior is, by definition, not fully predictable from the component parts.

The [[Forbidden Sigil Catalog]] represents the practical limit of the Cascade Principle — sigil combinations whose emergent behaviors proved too dangerous, too unpredictable, or too close to the patterns that powered the pre-Calamity resonance network. The Academy's restriction tier system exists specifically to control access to increasingly complex sigil patterns, on the theory that a practitioner who cannot reliably draw a Tier 2 sigil should not be attempting a Tier 3 pattern whose failure mode might level a building. The theory is sound. Its enforcement relies on the assumption that students and practitioners do not experiment with restricted patterns outside supervised settings, an assumption that the Academy maintains publicly and that no one involved actually believes.
