# Bonehands Counting House

A legitimately licensed financial services office on the primary dock street of [[Bayside Shushail]] that processes currency exchange, shipping insurance, and debt collection for the harbor district. It also happens to be the [[Bonehands]]' administrative headquarters, which everyone knows and no one says aloud within earshot of the door.

The building is unremarkable from the outside — stone-fronted, two stories, a polished brass nameplate reading "Harbor Financial Services" beside a heavy oak door. Inside, the ground floor operates as a genuine counting house: clerks behind iron grilles process transactions with professional efficiency, an abacus clicking on every desk, the air thick with the smell of coin metal, ink, and the particular staleness of a room where windows are kept shut regardless of season. Customers queue in an orderly line, watched by two attendants whose tailored vests do not quite conceal the weapons underneath. The exchange rates are competitive. The debt collection terms are not.

The second floor is where the counting house becomes the Bonehands' nerve center. Access requires passing through a reinforced door at the top of the stairs, watched by people who are not clerks. Behind it: offices where the harbor district's shadow economy is administered with the same bureaucratic rigor as the legitimate business below. Shipping manifests annotated with cargo that does not appear on official records. Ledgers tracking debts that no court would recognize. A map of Bayside Shushail on the wall with pins marking every business that pays protection, every dockworker on the payroll, every [[Solarguard]] officer whose gambling debts make them cooperative. The room smells of pipe tobacco and the cold sweat of people negotiating from positions of weakness.

The [[Solarguard]] knows exactly what happens on the second floor. The Bonehands know the Solarguard knows. The arrangement persists because the Bonehands keep the harbor functioning — smuggling aside, they enforce contracts, settle disputes, and maintain a peace that the official authorities cannot replicate without tripling the harbor patrol. The counting house is, in its way, the most honest building in Bayside Shushail: it does precisely what everyone understands it to do, and its only dishonesty is the sign on the door.
