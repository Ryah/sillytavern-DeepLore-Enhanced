# Scourge of Vitality

The Scourge of Vitality is a small cult operating covertly in [[Highwallow]], extracting life-force from marginal populations to power extended-duration binding rituals. The [[Solarguard]] has tried to dismantle them twice. Both times, key members escaped prosecution — once through a jurisdictional technicality with the [[Wardenship]], once because witnesses recanted under circumstances that no one involved believes were voluntary.

The Scourge's methods are grimly practical. They target people who won't be missed quickly — transients, addicts, laborers with no family in the city — and extract vitality through a ritual process that leaves victims alive but depleted: chronic fatigue, accelerated aging, immune collapse. The victims rarely connect their deterioration to a single event, and Highwallow's baseline poverty means that a person wasting away is more likely to be attributed to malnutrition or Scar exposure than to deliberate extraction. The harvested life-force fuels binding rituals of unusual duration and stability — the Scourge's actual product, sold to clients whose identities are the cult's most closely guarded secret. The [[Highwallow Mutual Aid Society]]'s medical volunteers have noticed the pattern of unexplained vitality loss in the district but lack the arcane expertise to identify the cause.

The cult's leadership remains genuinely unknown. Internal references use the title "the Pulse," but whether this is a single individual or a rotating position is unclear. The Solarguard's two failed raids — in Year 306 and Year 310 — both targeted safehouses that had been vacated days before the warrants were executed, suggesting either exceptional security discipline or an informant within law enforcement. The [[Wardenship]] considers the Scourge a priority target but faces the same intelligence problem: the cult operates in Highwallow, where institutional trust is low and cooperation with any authority — even one trying to stop people from being drained of their life — is complicated by generations of institutional neglect.

The Scourge's tension is between ambition and survival. The core members who practice the extraction rituals want to scale operations — more victims, more product, more revenue. But the cult's survival depends on remaining invisible, and every additional victim increases the risk of detection. The two Solarguard raids, even though they failed, forced the Scourge to relocate, rebuild, and operate at reduced capacity for months. Someone within the cult is making pragmatic arguments about limiting operations. Someone else is pointing out that their clients are not patient people. The [[Keepers of the Scar]] have begun documenting what they call "vitality shadows" near the Scar perimeter — areas where the ambient life-energy reads anomalously low — without yet connecting the readings to human-caused extraction. When they do, the Scourge will have a much larger problem than the Solarguard.
