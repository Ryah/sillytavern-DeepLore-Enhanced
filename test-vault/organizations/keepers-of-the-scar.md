# Keepers of the Scar

A [[Highwallow]] spiritual organization that regards the Resonance Scar as sacred — a wound in the world that must not be healed but understood. They monitor Scar activity, help residents cope with its effects, and strongly oppose any Academy research that might destabilize it further. In a district where the ground hums on bad days and the air tastes of copper before a resonance spike, the Keepers are the people who know what the signs mean.

The Keepers maintain a network of observation posts around the Scar perimeter — crude shelters with resonance-sensing instruments that the [[Duskfrost Academy]] would call "unreliable folk devices" and the Keepers would call "accurate enough to have saved lives six times this decade." Their monitoring is continuous. Teams of two rotate through twelve-hour shifts, recording fluctuations in Scar intensity, ambient temperature changes, and the subtle shifts in local wildlife behavior that precede major resonance events. This data is kept in handwritten journals stored in the Keepers' archive — a reinforced cellar beneath a Highwallow boarding house — and is not shared with the Academy, which has asked repeatedly and been refused each time.

Grandmother Tessavyne, a [[Selaveth]] elder who has lived within sight of the Scar for over sixty years, leads the Keepers with the authority of someone who has simply outlasted every challenge to her position. She teaches that the Scar is not a malfunction but a consequence — the world's honest response to the Antecedent Culture's overreach — and that attempting to close or "heal" it would be both spiritually wrong and practically catastrophic. This philosophy puts the Keepers in direct opposition to the Academy's [[Scar Mapping Expedition]] and any research program that proposes active intervention. The Academy views the Keepers as superstitious obstructionists. The Keepers view the Academy as the inheritors of the same arrogance that created the [[The Calamity Onset|Calamity]] in the first place. Neither view is entirely wrong.

The Keepers' internal tension is between the spiritual core — those who genuinely venerate the Scar as sacred — and the practical wing, who joined because the Keepers are the only organization that takes Scar safety seriously and provides early warning to Highwallow residents. The practical members attend the rituals and respect Grandmother Tessavyne, but their loyalty is to the monitoring network rather than the theology. Tessavyne tolerates this because she needs their labor, and because she privately suspects that understanding the Scar and protecting people from it are closer to the same thing than either faction admits. The [[Highwallow Mutual Aid Society]] is the Keepers' closest ally and most frequent source of friction — allied on keeping Highwallow alive, divided on whether observing a resonance spike takes priority over evacuating from it.
