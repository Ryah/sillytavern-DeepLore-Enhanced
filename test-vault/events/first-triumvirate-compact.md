# First Triumvirate Compact

Signed in Year 23 — less than a quarter century after the Calamity reduced the region to rubble and survivors — the First Triumvirate Compact formalized the power-sharing arrangement between the three families that had emerged as dominant through a combination of pre-Calamity wealth, post-Calamity ruthlessness, and the simple fact that they had more armed people than anyone else. The Compact was not an act of statesmanship. It was a ceasefire document dressed in governance language, signed because the three families had fought each other to a standstill and continuing the fighting would have left nothing worth governing.

The original document was seven pages long and addressed three concerns: territorial division of the emerging settlement (each house received a third of the habitable area), mutual defense obligations (an attack on one house was to be treated as an attack on all), and resource sharing during scarcity (a provision that was invoked twice in the first decade and ignored both times). The signatures were sealed with [[Blood Binding Contracts|blood-binding]], making the Compact one of the earliest documented uses of arcane contract enforcement in post-Calamity Duskfrost. The binding's enforcement clause was deliberately harsh — violation meant death — which has kept even the most ambitious house heads technically compliant while they found creative ways to circumvent the Compact's spirit.

It has been amended fourteen times since its signing. The amendments address everything from trade regulation to succession protocols to the definition of "habitable area" (a term that has required re-negotiation every time the city expanded). Legal scholars argue about which amendments supersede which, because the amendment process itself was never formalized — some amendments were negotiated, some were imposed by whichever house held temporary advantage, and at least two were signed under duress that the signatories later denied. The current document is forty-three pages long and internally inconsistent in at least three places, a fact that every Triumvirate lawyer is aware of and none have been authorized to resolve.

Later superseded in scope by the [[Binding Compact]] of Year 145, which addressed the growing city's needs more comprehensively. However, certain original clauses of the First Compact remain legally binding — most importantly, the blood-bound mutual defense provision, which has never been formally dissolved because doing so would require all three houses to simultaneously agree that they no longer need each other's protection. No house has been willing to be the first to suggest this, for fear of what it would imply about their intentions.
