# Redpond

A residential district surrounding a small natural lake in Duskfrost's interior. The [[Redpond Fishers Cooperative]] holds the freshwater fishing license. The lake itself is too small for significant commerce but valuable for water access in a district otherwise dependent on wells.

The district is shaped by the lake at its center — streets radiate outward from the [[Redpond Waterfront]] like spokes, and every resident lives within a ten-minute walk of the water. Housing is modest and well-maintained: two-story stone buildings with small gardens, most of them owner-occupied by families who have lived in Redpond for generations. The district has the feel of a village that happens to exist within a city — neighbors know each other, children play in the same streets their parents did, and the pace of life is governed more by the cooperative's fishing schedule than by the rhythms of [[Amberburgh]] commerce. The air smells of lake water and cooking smoke, and on still evenings, the sound of the cooperative's sorting work carries across the district.

Redpond's isolation from city politics is partly geographic — it sits in Duskfrost's interior, away from the cliff districts and the harbor — and partly cultural. The Fishers Cooperative is the district's dominant institution, and its members have historically preferred self-sufficiency to engagement with the Triumvirate or the [[Merchants League]]. The cooperative provides employment, the lake provides water, the district's small markets provide necessities, and the combination produces a community that asks remarkably little of the city government and receives roughly the same in return.

The lake itself is unremarkable: a hundred paces across at its widest, fed by underground springs that keep the water cold and clear year-round. Its name comes from the reddish clay that lines the bottom, visible in the shallows where children wade and cooperative members launch their flat-bottomed boats each morning. The [[Duskfrost Academy]] tested the water once, found nothing of arcane interest, and has not returned. Redpond's residents consider this the lake's finest quality.
