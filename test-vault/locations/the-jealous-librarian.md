# The Jealous Librarian

A faculty tavern adjacent to the [[Duskfrost Academy]] in [[Bellsummit]]. The name refers to the [[The Archive Building|Archive]] Head Archivist's rumored opinion of the [[Oracles of Might]]'s private collection. Serves decent wine at high prices. The primary venue for academic gossip, hiring negotiations, and post-seminar arguments.

The Jealous Librarian occupies a narrow stone building wedged between the Academy's eastern wall and a stationer's shop, its entrance marked by a painted sign depicting an owl clutching a wine glass with an expression of scholarly resentment. Inside, the space is surprisingly deep — a single long room of dark wood paneling, bookshelves that serve as decoration rather than lending library, and tables spaced far enough apart for private conversation but close enough for eavesdropping if one is skilled at it. The lighting comes from brass oil lamps that cast a warm amber glow, and the air smells of red wine, candle wax, and the particular dusty warmth of a room full of people who spend their working hours in old buildings. The wine list is curated by the owner, a former Academy procurement officer who left academia for hospitality and considers his current profession less political, which says something about academia.

The clientele is almost exclusively Academy-affiliated: faculty, senior researchers, advanced students who have earned (or assume they have earned) the social standing to drink here, and the occasional [[Oracles of Might]] member who is either brave enough or socially oblivious enough to patronize a tavern named for their guild's most contentious rivalry. Conversations at the Librarian follow predictable patterns — department politics over the first glass, research disputes over the second, candid opinions about colleagues over the third, and regrettable admissions over the fourth. More hiring decisions, publication disputes, and departmental alliances have been negotiated in this room than in any official Academy chamber.

The tavern's back room is available by reservation and is where the truly sensitive academic business occurs: tenure committee pre-meetings, grant allocation discussions, and the informal negotiations that determine which researchers get access to the [[Grand Resonance Observatory|Resonance Observatory]]'s classified data. The back room has no windows, thick walls, and a door that closes with a satisfying finality. The owner charges triple for its use and considers the premium a reasonable tax on institutional secrecy.
