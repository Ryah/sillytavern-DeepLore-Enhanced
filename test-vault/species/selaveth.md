# Selaveth

Selaveth are the rarest species in Duskfrost — fewer than forty individuals, and the number has not meaningfully changed in decades. They are slight, pale, and quiet, with an unsettling stillness that other species notice before they can articulate what feels wrong. What feels wrong is that the Selaveth are always perceiving something you cannot: the ambient arcane field that permeates everything, visible to them as naturally as color is to the sighted.

Selaveth arcane perception is not a skill or a spell. It is a sense, as involuntary as smell. They perceive arcane energy as overlapping fields of pressure, temperature, and what they describe as *texture* — the residue of a powerful enchantment feels "rough," while ambient natural magic feels "smooth" or "wet." [[Ayre Waesphyra]] has published the only peer-reviewed study of this perception, concluding that Selaveth process arcane information through a modified sensory cortex that other species simply do not possess. The study also noted, with careful academic restraint, that the implications for arcane detection are "significant."

The Academy employs every Selaveth it can recruit — currently twenty-three — at salaries that other species would find offensive if they knew the numbers. Selaveth work as arcane auditors, wards inspectors, and research assistants in fields where instrumental detection is too slow or too imprecise. A Selaveth can walk through a room and tell you which objects have been enchanted, how recently, and roughly how powerful the enchantment is. The [[Resonance Observatory]] relies on a team of four Selaveth for its most sensitive calibration work.

Living as a Selaveth in a city like Duskfrost is not comfortable. The ambient arcane noise of a dense urban environment is, by their account, exhausting — the equivalent of living beside a busy road where the traffic never stops. Heavy enchantment districts like [[Bellsummit]] are actively painful. Most Selaveth in Duskfrost live in the quieter edges of [[Rimeborough]] or [[Saseopt District]], where the arcane background is lower, and they commute to work through routes chosen for minimal magical interference rather than shortest distance.

Selaveth do not form large communities because there are not enough of them to do so. Their social bonds tend to be small, intense, and often cross-species — a Selaveth and their three or four closest friends, bound by the kind of trust that develops when one party can literally perceive the other's emotional state through their arcane signature. They are quiet in groups, direct in private, and deeply uncomfortable with deception, which they experience as a physical dissonance between what someone says and what their arcane field communicates.

Their origin is unknown. No Selaveth homeland has been identified. No historical record describes their arrival in any city. They appear in census records as far back as records exist, always in small numbers, always scattered, always rare. The Selaveth themselves offer no origin story. When asked, they tend to change the subject with a gentleness that makes further questions feel intrusive.
