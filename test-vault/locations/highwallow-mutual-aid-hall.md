# Highwallow Mutual Aid Hall

A technically-condemned building that the [[Highwallow Mutual Aid Society]] has occupied for twelve years without resolving its legal status. Provides food, shelter mediation, and basic medical services. The Triumvirate has twice discussed demolishing it. Both times, the political cost was calculated to be too high.

The building is a former textile warehouse on [[Highwallow]]'s central street, recognizable by the condemned notice still nailed to the door frame — faded to illegibility but never removed, because removing it would acknowledge someone's authority to have posted it. Inside, the ground floor has been converted into a common hall: mismatched tables and benches, a serving counter along the back wall where volunteers distribute meals twice daily, and a notice board so densely layered with flyers, job postings, and mutual aid requests that the wall behind it has not been visible in years. The smell is institutional — boiled grains, lye soap, and the damp wool of coats drying near the single wood stove that heats the space inadequately.

The upper floor houses the Society's offices and the shelter mediation service, where volunteers help displaced residents negotiate temporary housing with landlords who have learned that the Society's advocacy, while technically without legal standing, carries enough community weight to make cooperation the simpler option. A small room in the back serves as a consultation space for the [[Highwallow Night Clinic]]'s daytime referrals — a retired Academy researcher sees patients here on Tuesdays and Thursdays, treating what she can and referring what she cannot to practitioners who are willing to work on credit.

The Hall's continued existence is a source of quiet embarrassment for the Triumvirate and quiet pride for Highwallow. It operates without official funding, staffed entirely by volunteers, sustained by donations that arrive in amounts too small to track and too consistent to dismiss. The [[Solarguard]] patrols past it without entering. The Merchants League pretends it does not exist. And every morning, before the city's official services open their doors, the Mutual Aid Hall is already serving breakfast to people who have nowhere else to go.
