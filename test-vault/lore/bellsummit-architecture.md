# Bellsummit Architecture

Bellsummit's buildings are notable for their integration of arcane warding into structural design — the [[Oracles of Might]] tower's outer walls incorporate resonance-dampening stone quarried from a site that no longer exists. The [[Duskfrost Triumvirate]] administrative complex uses a pre-Calamity foundation that was never fully understood; the basement level has been sealed since Year 203 following an incident the official record describes only as "structural instability."

The district's architectural character reflects three distinct building periods. The oldest structures sit on pre-Calamity foundations — massive stone platforms whose original buildings were destroyed but whose bases proved too solid and too useful to remove. Reconstruction-era builders simply built on top of them, creating the characteristic split-level appearance of Bellsummit's government quarter: ground floors that are actually second floors, with sealed basements beneath that occasionally produce sounds the maintenance staff has learned to ignore. The second period, spanning roughly Year 100 to Year 250, introduced the ward-integrated construction that defines Bellsummit's skyline. Sigils are carved into load-bearing walls during construction, making the wards structural — removing or modifying them risks the building's physical integrity. This was considered clever engineering at the time. It is now considered a maintenance nightmare, as the wards degrade at different rates than the stone.

The third and current period is characterized by what architects politely call "adaptive reuse" — the ongoing practice of building new structures in, around, and occasionally through older ones as space in the elevated district becomes scarce. The result is a vertical maze where a building's front door may open onto a different street than its back door, and where neighboring structures share walls, plumbing, and ward networks in configurations their original builders never intended. The [[Solarguard]] patrol routes through Bellsummit require three-dimensional maps. Visitors get lost. Residents get lost in parts they haven't visited recently. The only people who navigate it reliably are the couriers, and they charge accordingly.

The Oracles tower itself is the district's most significant structure — seven stories of resonance-dampened stone rising from the district's highest point. Its construction in Year 156 required the demolition of three older buildings and the relocation of a market that has never forgiven the Oracles for the inconvenience. The tower's upper floors house the research chambers and the restricted vault; the lower floors house administrative offices and the public lecture hall where the Academy holds its annual symposium. The basement connects, through sealed and warded passages, to the [[Hollow Roads]] network beneath the city.
