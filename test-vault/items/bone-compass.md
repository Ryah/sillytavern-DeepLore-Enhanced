# Bone Compass

A compass that points toward the nearest recently deceased rather than magnetic north. Functionally useless for navigation in Duskfrost, where the nearest corpse is never far. Occasionally useful for finding murder victims before the city watch. The [[Bonehands]] use several.

The compass's mechanism is a fragment of human bone — specifically a fingertip phalanx, origin unknown — suspended in a sealed glass capsule filled with a preservative fluid that the [[Duskfrost Academy]] has analyzed and cannot replicate. The bone rotates freely within the fluid, orienting itself toward the strongest death-resonance within approximately five hundred meters. "Strongest" is a function of recency and violence: a person who died peacefully an hour ago produces a weaker signal than a person murdered five minutes ago. The compass cannot distinguish between species; it tracks any recently deceased sentient being with roughly equal sensitivity. It ignores animal remains entirely, which is either a deliberate design choice or reflects something about the nature of death-resonance that the Academy prefers not to examine too closely.

The Bonehands possess at least four working Bone Compasses and consider them essential operational equipment. Their primary use is locating bodies for the guild's corpse-recovery service — a legitimate business that serves as both revenue source and intelligence network, since the location and manner of death frequently provide useful information about neighborhood-level power dynamics. The [[Wardenship]] has attempted to requisition Bone Compasses for their own investigative work on three occasions and been refused each time, as the Bonehands' Charter guild status protects their proprietary tools from government seizure. The Wardenship responded by commissioning the Academy to produce replicas. The Academy's replicas point toward the nearest deceased, as designed, but also produce a persistent low-frequency hum that gives the user a headache within twenty minutes and migraines within an hour. The originals produce no such side effect. The Bonehands find this very funny.

The compasses' origin is disputed. The Bonehands claim they were crafted by their guild's founder, a Reconstruction-era mortician named Vessel Kade, who developed the technique through decades of working with the dead. The Academy's analysis suggests pre-Calamity manufacture, based on the preservative fluid's chemical composition. If the Academy is correct, the Bonehands' origin story is mythology. If the Bonehands are correct, Vessel Kade independently rediscovered a pre-Calamity technique, which is either impressive or troubling depending on what one thinks about people who spend their lives with corpses developing an intuitive understanding of death-resonance.
