# Dockmaster Bram Solvik

Dockmaster of [[Bayside Shushail]]'s main cargo pier — the person who controls berth allocation, oversees cargo inspection, and certifies that manifests reflect the actual contents of holds. He is methodical, experienced, and has a strong professional relationship with both the [[Solarguard]] customs office and the [[Bonehands]] dockside operation. He considers this relationship management rather than corruption, and the distinction is fine enough that he is technically correct.

Bram was a merchant sailor for twenty years before he took the dockmaster position, and he still thinks in tides and cargo weights rather than politics. He knows every ship that berths regularly, every captain's habits, and which holds are consistently heavier than their manifests claim. He records the discrepancies in a personal log that he keeps locked in his office, not because he intends to use it but because a man in his position who doesn't keep records is a man who has no leverage when he needs it.

The balance he maintains between the Solarguard customs officers and the Bonehands dockside crew is genuinely precarious. He gives the Guard enough accurate manifests to satisfy their inspection quotas, and he gives the Bonehands enough scheduling flexibility to move what they need to move. Both sides believe they are getting the better deal. Bram intends to keep it that way, because the alternative is a dockside war that would shut down trade for weeks, and trade is the only thing keeping Bayside Shushail functional.

He drinks with [[Gevven Troll]] at the morning market before dawn — two men who see everything on the waterfront from different angles. They have never formally agreed to share information, but their conversations over fish and coffee cover ground that intelligence professionals would find useful. [[Harvel Quince]] occasionally asks Bram about incoming cargo schedules, and Bram provides just enough to stay useful without becoming complicit.
