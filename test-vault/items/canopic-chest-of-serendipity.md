# Canopic Chest of Serendipity

A small stone chest of Antecedent Culture manufacture that produces a random minor beneficial effect each time it is opened — a coin appears, a mild wound closes, a misplaced object is found nearby. The effects are thoroughly mundane. The mechanism is completely incomprehensible. The [[Duskfrost Academy]] has studied it for forty years.

The chest is roughly the size of a bread loaf, carved from a pale stone that does not match any known quarry site. Its exterior bears [[Antecedent Script]] characters that [[Gorduin Wynlar]]'s translation renders as "small kindnesses" or possibly "the remainder of good fortune" — the ambiguity is typical. The chest opens normally, via a hinged lid with no lock. Inside, it is always empty. The beneficial effect occurs within approximately thirty seconds of opening, in the immediate vicinity of the opener, and is always minor — never life-changing, never dramatic, never anything that could not be explained as coincidence if one were determined to do so. A researcher opens the chest; a colleague mentions they found the book the researcher had been looking for. A guard opens it; a persistent toothache subsides. An archivist opens it; a quill that had run dry suddenly has ink again.

The Academy's research program has documented 1,247 openings over forty years. The beneficial effects show no detectable pattern in type, magnitude, or timing relative to the opener's circumstances. Statistical analysis confirms that the effects are genuinely beneficial (not merely perceived as such) and genuinely random (not following any cycle or responding to any measured variable). The chest produces no detectable arcane emissions. It does not resonate with any known frequency band. It registers as inert stone on every diagnostic instrument the Academy possesses. Three separate research teams have concluded, with varying degrees of professional frustration, that the chest should not work.

Currently stored in the [[Oracles of Might]] restricted collection, where it is classified as a Curiosity-Class artifact — dangerous only in the sense that the Academy cannot explain it, which means they cannot rule out a harmful failure mode they have not yet observed. Gorduin has noted, in his characteristically dry way, that the chest has been producing minor beneficial effects for at least four centuries without apparent degradation, depletion, or malfunction, and that perhaps the Academy should consider the possibility that some pre-Calamity artifacts simply do what they do without requiring an explanation. The research program continues anyway. The researchers open the chest before each session. Small kindnesses accumulate.
