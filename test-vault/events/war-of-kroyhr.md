# War of Kroyhr

The War of Kroyhr is the only pre-Calamity military conflict for which sufficient records survive to construct even a partial narrative. Fought between two Antecedent Culture city-states — Kroyhr and a second city whose name appears in fragments as "Vel-" or "Velith-" but has never been fully recovered — the war apparently involved the deliberate weaponization of resonance grid energy, making it the earliest documented use of the technology that would eventually destroy the civilization that created it.

What is known comes from fragments translated by Thornwick Pallasys and subsequent Academy scholars, primarily from diplomatic correspondence and what appear to be military reports. The conflict's cause was territorial — both cities claimed control over a ley line junction that powered their respective resonance grids. The fighting escalated over what Pallasys estimated was a period of two to five years (dating pre-Calamity events precisely is impossible with current methods). Early phases involved conventional military action. Later phases involved directed resonance pulses — essentially, using the towers themselves as weapons to destabilize the opposing city's infrastructure. The correspondence fragments describe effects that sound disturbingly similar to localized versions of the [[Calamity Onset|Calamity]]: ground subsidence, arcane discharge storms, and mass casualties among populations living near targeted towers.

The war ended when both sides destroyed each other's primary resonance towers simultaneously — whether by coordinated attack, mutual miscalculation, or deliberate murder-suicide pact is unknown. The fragments suggest the mutual destruction was not planned: one report, translated with low confidence, reads "they struck as we struck, and the silence after was absolute." Both cities appear to have been abandoned within a generation of the war's conclusion. Kroyhr's location has been tentatively identified as a ruin site sixty miles north of Duskfrost. Velith's location is unknown.

The [[Oracles of Might]] maintain the most complete collection of Kroyhr-related fragments in their restricted archive — material that became a flashpoint during the [[Oracles Schism]] precisely because its implications for modern resonance research are so dangerous. Scholars studying the conflict have drawn parallels to the political dynamics behind the [[Watchers Treaty]], noting that the pattern of two powers dividing territory to avoid mutual destruction recurs throughout Duskfrost's history. The more uncomfortable parallel — that the Antecedent Culture fought a war using resonance weapons and then, apparently, learned nothing from it — is one that gains relevance with every new development in the [[Resonance Grid Discovery]].
