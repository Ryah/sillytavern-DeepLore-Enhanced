# Cliffside Stairs

A public stairway cut into the Duskfrost bluff connecting [[Bellsummit]] to the mid-cliff districts below. Technically maintained by the city, practically maintained by whoever feels like it. The lifts are monitored. The stairs are not. Anyone who uses the stairs instead of the lifts has a reason to prefer not being observed, and everyone on the stairs understands this about everyone else.

The stairway is carved directly into the cliff face — over three hundred steps of rough-cut stone, worn smooth at the center by centuries of feet and treacherous when wet. Iron railings were installed forty years ago and have rusted to a color that matches the stone. Every seventh landing has a recessed alcove originally intended for rest; these alcoves now serve as dead drops, meeting points, and sheltered spots for the kind of brief, quiet conversations that require neither witnesses nor walls. The descent takes fifteen minutes at a brisk pace, longer if the wind is up. The wind is usually up. It funnels through the cliff channel with enough force to make the middle section of the stairway a genuine hazard in storms, and the city has posted warning signs that no one reads.

The view from the stairs is the only honest panorama of Duskfrost's vertical geography — [[Bellsummit]]'s institutional spires above, the harbor sprawl of [[Bayside Shushail]] below, and between them the layered districts of the cliff face, each terrace a different era of construction stacked like geological strata. On clear days, the sea is visible past the harbor breakwaters. The beauty of it is incidental; no one on the stairs is there for the scenery.

Traffic on the stairs follows its own patterns. Pre-dawn sees Academy staff who live below their means descending to the lower districts after late shifts. Midday brings couriers and messengers for whom the stairs are simply faster than waiting for the lift queue. After dark, the stairs belong to people whose business benefits from the absence of the lift operators' logs. The [[Solarguard]] occasionally proposes installing monitoring on the stairway landings. The proposal never survives budget review, which is convenient for everyone involved in ensuring it does not.
